A secretive system or organization connected to the Justicar order, the Aletheia Procedure governs how Justicars track and collect individuals marked by their blades. Leon's sword carries the names of his charges, and when a name is collected or the individual dies, the runes respond accordingly.

When Quin was disintegrated by Ozythar, Leon's blade extinguished Quin's name without a proper collection, an event that should have been impossible. Sir Sunder's investigation into this contradiction led him to discover potential corruption within the Procedure, connected to a dark entity in the Shadowfell known as the Pallbearer.

Per Art Doyle's explanation in Session 46, the Procedure has a failsafe: votes of no confidence from both the High Haven Arcane Academy and the Order of the Ju-Shi will trigger a governmental review and freeze the Procedure. In Session 47, Gideon invoked the vote on behalf of the Academy. In Session 48, the Ju-Shi tribunal prosecuted Divine Judge Vine Ashen Bloom and invoked the second vote. Both institutional checks have now been triggered, and the Procedure is frozen pending review.
