## Overview

The Ruins of Kalari campaign takes place in the aftermath of Old Perile Falls' catastrophic collapse roughly 200 years prior. The party begins in the rebuilt settlement of New Perile Falls and gradually uncovers the truth behind the original city's destruction — a chain of events linking Project Anima (soul-binding construct research), divine punishment through dragon releases, and an imprisoned Void Dragon weapon hidden in the plane of Orbos.

## Major Arcs

### Harwich Village Curse
The party investigates a pseudo-vampiric curse afflicting the village of Harwich, discovering a conspiracy between Nula Tipster, Finnan Tispter, and reluctant accomplice Ambiar Widdle.

### The Cult of Grave Dirt
In New Perile Falls, the party uncovers a sewer-dwelling cult led by Grave Dirt, defeating puppet-making rituals and the doppelganger creature Nina.

### Kuo-Toa and the Power Plant
The party explores the ancient hydroelectric power plant above Perile Falls, navigating rival Kuo-Toa factions and inadvertently empowering GaraBaga as a newborn deity through worship.

### Sunken City Exploration
Diving into the ruins of Old Perile Falls, the party recovers magical memories from the arch-gate revealing the city's fall — a storm drake attack, Senate Speaker Runva Doberdower's evacuation, and Byron Wintar's sacrifice to keep the gate open.

### Vespa and the Fey Threat
The fey entity Vespa attempts to use Gwen's portal network to breach the Hearth (Kip's sacred home). The party defeats Vespa in a dreamscape battle with the help of the giant's son Tempest.

### Drelduin and the Desert War
The dragon Drelduin terrorizes the Emerald Crescent, exploiting the conflict between the Tanner and Dweller desert factions. The party defeats Drelduin and brokers peace between the factions.

### Orbos and the Void Dragon
The party infiltrates the plane of Orbos, destroys Arianna Wintar's construct laboratory, rescues soul-bearing constructs, and learns the truth about the Void Dragon — an ultimate weapon smuggled out during the divine punishment. They travel to Mechanus to challenge the Grand Contract governing divine interference.

## Themes

- The ethics of creating sentient constructs and soul-binding
- Divine politics and the consequences of mortal hubris
- Found family and protecting what matters
- The weight of ancient mistakes on the present
