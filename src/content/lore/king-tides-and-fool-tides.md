## Overview

The three moons of the Celestial Braid create a tidal cycle that profoundly affects life in Astoria, particularly for sailors, coastal communities, and wetland ecosystems. As Weft and Cross spiral around Warp each month, their combined gravitational pull alternately supercharges and counteracts the tides.

## King Tides (Days 19–23)

King Tides begin on the 19th and run through the 23rd of every month, peaking on the 21st during the Full Moons. During King Tides, all three moons are aligned in their pulls. High tides are exceptionally high, and low tides are exceptionally low. Currents become turbulent and violent. Sailors typically prefer to remain dockside or in open sea during these days. Coastal wetlands become prone to flooding.

## Fool Tides (Days 5–9)

Fool Tides begin on the 5th and run through the 9th, peaking on the 7th during the New Moons. During Fool Tides, Weft and Cross are at their furthest from Warp, working against her pull. The fluctuations in tides are at their weakest, currents lie relatively calm, and the shoreline remains relatively stable. Wise ship captains time their passage through the most treacherous waters on these days.

## Impact on Daily Life

The tidal cycle shapes maritime culture across Astoria. Port cities schedule major trade runs and naval operations around the Fool Tides, while King Tides are periods of caution and preparation. Coastal festivals often coincide with tidal peaks, and fishermen plan their seasons around the monthly rhythm.
