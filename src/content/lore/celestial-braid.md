## The Three Moons

The world of Astoria is orbited by three moons, collectively known as **The Celestial Braid**. Folklore tells that Sehanine, the Moonweaver, braids the three moons every night into a single braid, as a mother braids the strands of a child's hair. All three moons wax and wane, but only the two ancillary moons dance across the sky.

## Warp — The Central Moon

Of the three strands of the braid, **Warp** is the central moon around which the braid is spun. Relative to observers in Astoria, her position remains relatively fixed in place, and she occupies the same general position in the sky from night to night. She is visible across all of Astoria on a nightly basis. Because of her fixed position, Warp exerts predictable, daily tide cycles.

## Weft and Cross — The Ancillary Moons

**Weft** and **Cross** are the twin sisters dancing around their elder. Each night they pass across the heavens as Astoria turns beneath them, but over the course of a month they trace their true spiraling path around Warp, tightening and loosening the Celestial Braid. Because they move through the heavens and vary in their distance from Astoria, their apparent size grows and shrinks, and there are nights when one or both are not visible in the sky from certain regions of the world.

## The Moons and the Calendar

The calendar of Astoria includes fourteen 28-day months in observance of the cycles of the Celestial Braid. The 7th day of every month corresponds with all three moons being New, and the 21st day of every month coincides with all three moons being Full. On the 21st, Warp, Weft, and Cross are all full and visually aligned in the night sky.

## Moon Phases

- **Day 7**: New Moon (all three moons)
- **Day 14**: First Quarter
- **Day 21**: Full Moon (all three moons aligned)
- **Day 28**: Last Quarter
