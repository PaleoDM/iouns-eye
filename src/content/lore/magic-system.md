## The Three Sources of Magic

Magic in Astoria flows from three fundamental sources, often visualized as three overlapping circles. Most spellcasters draw primarily from one source, though some draw from the overlap between two or even all three.

## Arcane

Arcane magic is the magic of study, intellect, and raw magical energy manipulated through knowledge and will. **Wizards** and **Artificers** are the purest practitioners of arcane magic, mastering spells through rigorous study and experimentation.

## Divine

Divine magic flows from the gods — both prime and corrupted — channeled through faith and devotion. **Clerics** and **Paladins** draw their power directly from their patron deities, serving as conduits of divine will.

## Primal

Primal magic is the raw power of the natural world itself — the storms, the tides, the growth of living things. **Druids** and **Rangers** tap into this ancient force, drawing power from the land, the sea, and the sky.

## The Overlaps

Some casters draw from the boundaries between sources:

- **Bards** draw from the overlap of Arcane and Divine — weaving magical effects through music, performance, and storytelling that touches both intellectual understanding and spiritual resonance.
- **Sorcerers** draw from the overlap of Arcane and Primal — their magic is innate, flowing through their blood like a force of nature rather than something learned.
- **Shamans** draw from the overlap of Divine and Primal — communing with spirits of nature that bridge the gap between the gods and the wild.
- **Warlocks** sit at the center of all three, drawing power from pacts that can touch any source depending on their patron — an archfey (primal), a fiend (divine), or a Great Old One (arcane).
