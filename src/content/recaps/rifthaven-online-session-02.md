We opened on the 15th of Raei, with our party have high tailed it, at Dante's direction, to the closest thing for an inn here in the Sparkworks District. Along the way, we got a good look at just how bad off the district is doing. We saw the former wealth—large Victorian homes dotting the cliff faces—but all in terrible states of disrepair.

When our heroes awoke in the morning, they found that their accomodations, the House of Respite, had seem much better days. Cobwebs lined the corners and much of the house was in bad shape. Nevertheless, their hosts, the Stoutwaters, were doing their best to present a sense of normalcy. Arden however, had heard some news of the neighborhood, and quickly gathered that they may be the Stoutwater's first real customers in months, or even years.

As the abject poverty of the district began to set in, our heroes reviewed the plan with Dante. Chase down rumors of these tunnels, find them if they're real, and render the operational once more. In exchange, some stake of the business, as Dante is looking to foster locals with a vested interest in improving things.

As Dante departed, our heroes turned to the Stoutwaters for leads and information. Along the way, they learned that the Stoutwaters, much like the rest of the district, is under threat by The Hushed (the cult that attacked our heroes the night before) and the Ledgermen (the local militia with a vendetta against Dante). Our heroes quickly realized that their mere presence here put the Stoutwaters in danger, as their transaction would inherently look like support, tacit or otherwise.

Unsure exactly how to help, our heroes settled on "starting a tab" that they might pay down the line. In exchange, Maribel, the matriarch halfling Stoutwater, shared a map of the Sparkworks District and pointed them in a few directions. Our heroes settled on a mark—Lord Harwin Brassfell—who typically is found at an old club/watering hole. Instead, our heroes decided to try to break into his home, dig up dirt, and use it as leverage for aid.

Along the way, Tyrel had the brilliant idea to recruit a few local scamps for a distraction. In doing so, he discovered two scamps being harassed by a pair of Ledgermen. Before long, a scuffle broke out, as one constable knocked the child out with his baton. Our heroes, thinking they were defending to lads, suddenly found themselves in the middle of a Ledgermen trap, sprung to flush them out after their battle the night before.

The fight that ensued was brutal. Thorn found himself distract several Toughs all on his own, while Tyrel pursued a mage and Veil mopped out a pair of archers harassing the party. Quin landed a clutch Hold Person, but it was short lived as the mage brought the brunt of their spells down on the party. In the ensuing melee, lots of blood was shed. Veil threw a man off a roof, Thorn decapitated a Thug, Arden speared another with a divine smite from his Trident.

But when the battle came to a climax, the mage dropped a heavy fireball which knocked multiple players down. They desperately fought to bring the mage down, but a follow up Thunderwave knocked Arden and Tyrel down and forced Veil and Quin to retreat. With a single hit point remaining, the Mage chose not to run, hoping that our heroes were running for good. Instead, Veil doubled back and stablized Arden while Quin blasted the mage with one final magic missile, ending the threat for good.

Unfortunately, moments later, Thorn breathed his last breath, and went cold to the touch. We join our heroes mid-morning on the 15th of Raei, in the streets of the Sparkworks District with bloody corpses all around, as one lonely urchin drags his unconscious friend into the safety of a nearby building. Arden and Tyrel are both unconscious, making retreat a difficult proposition.
