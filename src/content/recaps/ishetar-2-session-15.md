We opened mid-combat in the palace gardens of Ember’sPost. Ethereal scorpions still swarmed the courtyard, while an invisible stalker struck unseen among the fountains. The fight was tense but manageable. Thump was briefly ensnared by the stalker, the air drawn from his lungs as it tried to suffocate him, limiting his spellcasting. Lyra, anticipating such threats, had earlier cast *See Invisibility* on Vicas, which proved decisive—allowing him to spot the stalker and the remaining scorpions. With that advantage, Vicas downed the last scorpion, and the party quickly surrounded the invisible foe. A coordinated assault ended the threat before it could claim another breath.

As the battle subsided, the tension only grew. It became clear that the heroes had stumbled into the middle of an assassination attempt. Palace guards rushed to secure the scene, unsure whether the newcomers were culprits or saviors. Lady Rowena’s presence and recognition by her cousins helped avert disaster, but the group was promptly escorted to a secure chamber alongside the two young dukes while an investigation began.

During their impromptu confinement, the party spoke at length with the dukes, learning that the brothers cared deeply for one another despite clashing political ideals. Their mother—the Duchess Lyla—lay bedridden, her health failing, and her condition threatened to destabilize Ember’sPost at a critical moment.

Before meeting the Duchess, the party was introduced to her three healers: **Vicar Zona**, a half-orc cleric of the Moonweaver who oversaw herbal and spiritual remedies; **Father Asam**, a weary human cleric of the Dawnfather who had grown bitter after repeated failures to heal her; and **High Priest Hiccup**, a venerable gnome and long-respected figure in Ember’sPost. Each confirmed the same grim prognosis—no curse, no malady, only age and decline. Duchess Lyla, half-elven and more than a century old, seemed simply near her end.

When granted audience, the Duchess received them with dignity. She lamented calling in so great a favor from Lady Rowena but insisted the matter was dire. She spoke of the growing rift between the mainland nobles and Ember’s Post, and of her desire to keep the city neutral amidst the empire’s brewing wars. Worse, a catastrophic “accident” at the gold mine the previous day appeared to be deliberate sabotage—an act that could cripple the city’s economy and ignite political chaos. She implored Lady Rowena and her companions to investigate, stressing that without the mines, Ember’s Post could not endure.

Rooker, unconvinced that illness alone explained her condition, took a calculated risk. Reaching out telepathically, he requested to speak with her privately. His bold persuasion earned the party a closed audience, and once the healers departed, Lyra cast *Detect Poison and Disease*. The result confirmed their suspicions—Duchess Lyla was being poisoned. The toxin was subtle, its metallic signature masked within her wine. Rooker’s theory proved correct: she was suffering from slow heavy-metal poisoning.

The Duchess was stunned, but when Lyra and Vicas proposed a *Restoration* to purge the poison, she hesitated. To reveal her recovery too soon, she warned, would alert her enemies. Yet Thump’s impassioned plea—arguing that strength, not secrecy, would rally her allies—moved her. She consented, and Vicas performed the purification, drawing the toxins from her blood as her color slowly returned.

We ended with the Duchess restored but resolute. She charged the party with three tasks: uncover the cause of the mine’s sabotage, aid her sons in securing the political alliances needed to keep Ember’s Post neutral—and, lastly, discover who among her court had sought her death.
