We opened with our heroes already moving.

Session forty-nine had made it clear: the Coin Counting House was the misdirect. The tunnels under Sparkworks were where the Cassini Brothers had always planned to be, using our heroes' own smuggling infrastructure to move one hundred and eleven souls toward the Nine Hells. The window was closing. Our heroes moved.

The bastion was under attack when they came through. The Cassini Brothers had sent muscle to keep Sparkworks occupied and buy themselves time. Captain Reeve had it handled. That was enough. Our heroes pushed through without stopping and found Sister Ellarra at the chapel, its doors blasted in, standing behind her shield in the wreckage. The enemies had already come through, and she had stepped aside. The numbers weren't there for her to put up resistance.

She thanked Ty for showing up anyway and blessed him with Shield of Faith before they headed down.

Thorn pressed his hand against the old mushroom and listened. It knew things. It knew how many were already in the tunnels and how far along they were in the job. There wasn't time to be careful. Our heroes grabbed every consumable they had, potions of pugilism, potions of heroism, as much as they could carry, and went straight in.

The Rum Runner Aquifer was already in motion. Oleg and Perry Ellis Cassini were there with a crew of thugs, unloading crates of memory crystals and sending them into the current. The crystals held the souls. Let them float far enough downriver and they were gone from the material plane entirely. Our heroes had one shot at this and not much time to take it.

Veil went in first. She crossed the distance on a mage, put them down before they could cast, and pulled back. Ty stepped up.

The Chromatic Orb that followed was something. Close to two hundred damage. It hit the field like a cannon. Freddie came in right behind it with a Synaptic Static that caught five or six enemies in the blast and killed most of them. The ones still standing were so rattled they couldn't string two thoughts together. In just a few seconds, the whole shape of the fight had changed.

The Cassini Brothers had an answer for it. A necrotic aura, short range, that blocked healing entirely while you were inside it. Thorn had already pushed into the thick of the fight, which is exactly what Thorn does, and both he and Ty were absorbing damage they couldn't recover from. Things got serious fast.

Thorn went down. Relentless Rage brought him back. The Cassinis hit him again and he went down again. Rage Beyond Death brought him back again. A third time they dropped him. He stood back up. Three times they tried to kill Thorn and three times he refused. Stood there bleeding and furious and completely unkillable.

Veil had eyes on Ty. She crossed the fight, grappled him, and dragged him out of the aura before it finished the job. Once he was clear, Leon could reach him. Ty came back up. Freddie, meanwhile, had been raising zombies and pointing them at the crates. No one was watching the constructs. No one thought to stop them. The memory crystals were not going anywhere.

Perry Ellis Cassini went down first. Leon found Oleg after that, and the smites he landed were the kind that ends things. Clean and final. Oleg went down. Ty finished the last man standing.

One hundred and eleven souls recovered. All of them. We faded out on our heroes returning triumphant to the Sparkworks District.

The epilogue came in scenes.

We saw Leon adrift. He came back from the Shadowfell as something not quite a paladin and not quite alive, one wing broken and a disposition that didn't square with divine justice anymore. He couldn't lead with that calling until he understood what he had become. So he stepped back. Small jobs. Odd-job private investigations, the kind of work that lets a person exist quietly and figure things out. Leon, for now, is a man between identities.

We saw Veil take the crystals. All one hundred and eleven. She began the hard work of identifying who they belong to and how to get them back. She now works with Sunder to root out the corruption inside the Order of the Ju Shi and builds the case that implicates the House of Coinwrights in Haji's operation. When the time comes to send the souls on, she and Freddie plane-shift the lot of them to Captain Alistair, who can carry them across to the afterlife. The truth part of her name, after a whirlwind of living mostly as Tempest.

We saw Ty try to make something work with Sister Ellarra and Ghost. It didn't quite go the way he intended. They were, it turned out, more interested in each other than in him. Ty took the note and found his way to something he had been putting off. He went home. He spent time with his parents. And what he found there was hard. A curse from the Spider Queen placed on Captain Alistair, but it was not landing on Alistair. It was landing on Ty's mother. She was aging. Losing herself slowly. The talisman that breaks the curse is Alistair's old adventuring tricorn, the hat he wore before everything. It is not in easy reach, because it's at the bottom of the lake where Alistair's vessel was sunk. Ty has his next quest.

We saw Thorn gardening. He fought for Sparkworks and he stayed in Sparkworks, hands in the soil on rooftop after rooftop, teaching the industrious and the labor-focused how to grow things. It took some time. He showed up anyway. These days, Thorn is often observed by onlookers from other districts, because Arden has spread the word that Thorn "lectures" on underground horticulture. Thorn rarely speaks, but the lessosn are passed on all the same. 

We saw Freddie putting money and effort back into the district, reinvesting in the infrastructure they spent months defending. He and Arden made their peace, or something close to it. Freddie made a generous donation to the campaign and they shook hands. Then Arden announced a minimum wage platform, and Freddie discovered he had strong feelings about that. The reconciliation held, sort of. But a new rift forms between the brothers.

And Quin remained a painting. He may not always be. For now he was, and the people of Rifthaven had decided that this was a destination. They came from across the city to stand in front of the canvas and ask questions. Quin dispensed mathematical divinations. They paid a fee. The fees went to Sparkworks. The mastermind, running a side business from inside a frame.

We leave Rifthaven, or at least the Sparkworks District, a little better than our heroes found it.
