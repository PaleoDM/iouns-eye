We joined our heroes still encamped among the Silverweave Legion, their patience with Officer Oswald wearing thin. Oswald’s offhand remark to Veil—something about “returning to her needlework”—earned him a glare from Ty sharp enough to cut steel. Tension ran high, and soon the group found themselves splitting off to different pursuits.

Arden was approached by Jerusha, the legionnaire he had revived in battle and who had taken quite a shine to him. She arrived bearing a picnic basket filled with contraband delights—wine, cheese, and bread, the sort of fare rarely seen in a soldier’s camp. True to her lawful nature, Jerusha insisted on a full suite of consent forms before the date began, carefully outlining the escalation of amorous activities. Arden, unusually bashful but amenable, agreed and the two enjoyed a peaceful evening together—one that produced warmth, wine, and absolutely no actionable intelligence.

Meanwhile, Veil and Ty turned their curiosity toward the heart of the camp: the mysterious apparatus said to hold the Beholder, Ozythar, at bay and protect the Legion’s dreams. Their investigation led them to a tent arranged not for living but for secrecy. Through a combination of invisibility and confident bluffing, they gained entry. Inside, they discovered a dwarven artificer asleep at his post beside a humming gearwork engine that powered an arcane warding circle. But the true source of amplification came from two strange creatures suspended from tall racks—part fox, part bat, their fur sleek and their ears impossibly long. Veil and Ty learned that most mortals call them Arcano-bats, but in the underdark where they're born, they are known as Whisper-Wings.

Tended by a gnomish druid, these creatures channeled magic through sound and resonance, expanding both range and duration of enchantments. Veil and Ty learned they were semi-sentient, comprehending Undercommon but speaking in Deep Speech. Negotiation was awkward but fruitful. The creatures, who craved to feed on enchanted flesh, agreed to a deal: if the heroes slew Ozythar and let them feast upon its magic-infused corpse, they would turn on the Silverweave camp and disrupt their operations at Veil’s signal.

While the others schemed, Quinn spent the night gambling with the soldiers. Between hands of dice and cards, he traded coin for gossip. The troops spoke freely under the illusion of camaraderie: Oswald, they said, had been unable to pierce the magical wards protecting the grove and had called in a powerful favor—a great machine expected to arrive within the next day or so, capable of breaching the barriers that even the Legion’s mages could not undo. The news meant the heroes had little time to act. With a final lucky roll—a perfect natural twenty—Quinn left the soldiers both fleeced and cheering, his purse full and his cover intact.

Thorn, meanwhile, spent the night in quiet restraint, keeping to himself to avoid suspicion. Unfortunately, Oswald sought him out. What began as polite flattery soon turned sinister as the officer praised Thorn’s obedience and loyalty before reminiscing about “an operation outside Rifthaven” where only eight of thirty men followed orders without hesitation—orders to seize a district “at all costs.” The implication was clear, and Thorn realized Oswald was describing the destruction of his own home. Whether Oswald knew Thorn’s true identity remained uncertain, but the encounter left Thorn gritting his teeth hard enough to nearly crack one before dismissing the man with the coldest line ever uttered in Rifthaven: "The Mountain does not speak of its shadow."

By morning, the heroes were eager to leave camp—except perhaps Arden, who seemed refreshed for his time spent in respite. They made their way back toward the Beholder’s territory, where signs of a recent battle showed that the kobolds had repelled a counterattack. Taking advantage of the weakened opposition, the party pressed forward into the Beholder’s vestibule.

The cavern descended in spirals of stone and resin, clearly designed for aerial predators. There they faced a massive Froghemoth and several of Ozythar’s lesser kin, the Gauth. The terrain proved treacherous—Thorn was stunned by eye rays while Veil was nearly dragged into a yawning pit leading down into the Beholder’s lair—but teamwork and persistence carried the day. Quinn’s spells and Ty's support wore the beasts down until Thorn landed a decisive strike, launching the Froghemoth backward into the abyss. Whether it fell to its death or waits below remains uncertain.

We join our heroes standing on the edge of that great black descent, one Gauth still flitting in the dark, the fate of the Froghemoth unknown. The air hums with the presence of Ozythar beneath them, and the path down promises no easy rest.
