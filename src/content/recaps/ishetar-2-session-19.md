### **Begin Level 7**

We joined our heroes emerging from their rest in the Goldwarren Mine. As they refreshed themselves, having grown stronger, they were treated to upsetting news: the Duchess Lyla had been found dead overnight. They took a few moments to compose themselves, including debriefing with their newly acquired "assistant" Vero.

Vero assured them that the situation was about to become politically tense; many sitting nobles had a healthy fear of Lady Rowena and the folks from Ishetar on account of Rowena's relation to the other princes, and feared she might make a play to control Emberspost. Beyond that, each noble family had key business dealings back in respective Aroiin cities, making them naturally inclined to side with their own wallets. Needless to say, the death of the Duchess, whether foul play or otherwise, presented a sticky situation.

Our heroes rushed back to the Palace by carriage, where they found Lady Rowena and her cousins, Duke Simbon and Duke Kirin, flanked by numerous royal guards, as well as High Priest Hiccup. Rowena welcomed our heroes with exhausted relief, plainly fearing that somewhere in the city there may have been a plot to take them down. She was relieved they lived.

Rooker took charge, and frankly issues an order to everyone besides the party and those related to the Duchess to vacate. They did so, with High Priest Hiccup lingering just long enough to assert that "it's very important to me that a secular pronouncement of the death be made." Still, he stepped aside.

Immediately, our heroes sprang into investigation mode, not believing for one moment that the Duchess was dead without some trickery or foul play. Together, they learned some important information:

-   Duchess Lyla looked much more palid and rigid, devoid of live already despite having been restored by Thump a few mere hours ago

-   When the priests had all gathered here with the Dukes, there had been a great argument between Father Assam and Vicar Xona. Then, Hiccup had cast a protective magic circle, which had caused a powerful explosion of bright light.

-   When the light faded, there was a big magical scorch mark (visible only to Rooker via Detect Magic), and Father Assam was gone. Vicar Xona was fleeing, and guards tailed her back in the direction of her library at the temple.

In the process of investigating, Azrael accidentally found himself "scooby-doo"ing through a secret doorway. Suddenly, he was inside the walls in a set of passages clearly designed for snooping and spying. In a quiet alcove, Azrael found a mortar and pestle, as well as signs of a potent concoction being mixed including a jar of grave dirt. Beside it all was a golden scarab of beautiful filigree with a small vial or canister in its belly, as though meant to hold a liquid of some kind. Yet it was clearly non-magical.

Vicas, having pleaded with Lyra not to give away Lyla's plans too quickly, called our heroes off into a side parlor for quiet conversation. Here, he apologized for keeping the Duchess's plans secret, but explained that she got the reaction she wanted: she successfully spooked the bad actors into acting before they were ready.

Our heroes took some time to assess the situation, with Azrael returning via secret door, and decided that they now had powerful reason to suspect both Assam and Xona: the question was if they were working together or on separate plots. They were interrupted by High Priest Hiccup, who came and offered everyone Brandy while he retold them the story of the magic circle and the fiendish presence he had felt. Hiccup shuddered at the thought that he might have missed nefarious actors under his nose, and pledged himself to assist the party however they would have of him. "Keep a smile, and ensure politics continues to move slowly" the party asked of him, and off he went.

Then, our heroes entered the tunnels Azrael found and made their way to the Duchess's wing, where they found her handmaiden, who definitely recognized Vicas and the DM didn't goof here at all. They learned that the golden scarab had somehow super charged the spell, and that the Duchess would remain dead for 3 days before rising out of her Feign Death spell. In that time, her enemies would act with impunity, and our heroes would have the chance to expose them. But, as Lady Rowena had begged them, they must act with discretion, for she *must* still consider the politics of the situation.

Realizing they needed to act quickly, our heroes rushed to the courtyard, where Thump begged his favor off of King One Eye the Tressym. "There are rats in our garden, oh King. Will you help us rout them?" And so King One Eye took to the air, indignant and needing to lead earth bound creatures, but helpful nonetheless. He led them to a small shrine over looking the only canal here in Emberspost, where they found Father Assam desperately trying to scramble together some kind of back up plot.

It soon became clear that his plans had involved a slow poisoning of the Duchess, and that the mine collapse and now her sudden death had hastened things too quickly for his own plans to work. He was very much in triage mode. As hostilities broke out, Assam became posessed by some wicked, demonic form. No matter, for the dice were with our players.

What proceeded was the most thorough undressing of a villain I have ever seen. Azrael landed a mighty critical hit with his blades. Rooker took down nearly all of the minions with Hideous Laughter. Vicas laid into the devil mercilessly, including one crit of his own, and Thump grappled him with a powerful grasping Vine. All the while, devils made strike after strike on Lyra, only to miss every single one. When she finally burned out his impurities with the sacred light of the sun, Assam fell before her, unconscious and scorched beyond all belief.

We join our heroes having just combed through Assam's things. They've begun to uncover his plot to kill the Duchess and install one of the Dukes as his puppet, with the hopes of drawing Emberspost into the war on the side of Dedidan. Now thwarted, our players realize Vicar Xona may have been working in opposition to Assam, and our heroes wonder if they can reach her too before her own machinations advance too far.
