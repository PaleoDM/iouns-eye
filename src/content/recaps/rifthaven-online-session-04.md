We joined our heroes this week in the throes of combat, having just wandered into a small room in the tunnels beneath Sparkworks and encountered a set of ghasts and ghouls! The creatures, filling the room with the stench of rot and decay, leapt with their hideous tongues and attempted to consume our players. In the fray, Arden threw his new, magical trident that returns to him. Thorn and Veil did their best to keep everyone else clean, while Quin and Ty provided the artillery from the back line. For once, no one went down!

When the dust settled, our heroes took stock of their situation. They had made it a little ways down from the Ashen Chapel, and quickly realized that they were in a storage room of some kind. They investigated the area and found it to be predominately church supplies—this was an old storage room used by the church. Amidst the spoils were a few old barrels of potable water (now empty), religious spirits (also, regrettably empty), and a handful of ceremonial supplies and components. Nothing of value, or of particular interest. But, remembering the old distillery woman sneaking Thorn across town in a wine barrel, they wondered if there may be a story here.

This old storage depot had two ways forward: one bending southwest, which grew wider and transitioned from earthen dirt into rough hewn stone. Veil conjectured that this would lead to some type of main thoroughfare. The other, turning North, was a small smaller tunnel through which Ty could feel cool, breezy air and hear the sound of running water. He conjectured this would lead to an exit, whether that be up to the surface or down to lower districts. The party decided to head North first.

Here, they passed into an old, forgotten watering hole—an ancient mechanical contraption with gears and knobs and whistles loomed over an old well shaft dug down into the stone. The machine sat inert, but the well still filled with clean, potable water from a local aquifer. Unsure what to do here, our heroes pressed on, but not before they noticed the Sparkplugs very enthusiastically obsessing over the abandoned mechanical aqueduct.

In the next chamber, our heroes at last found what they were looking for—the smuggling route. They came upon an underground aquifer, with gentle, running water and a modest dock setup on the "beach" of this water table. They instinctively knew this was what they were looking for—goods could be loaded in water resistant vehicles and floated down the aquifer towards the lower districts and, eventually, the Drow Docks.

Unfortunately, their enthusiasm was short lived, as they were ambushed by two hideous creatures made of gnashing teeth and grotesque tentacles. As combat broke out, they were joined by Piercers—falling stalactites that plummeted onto the heads of our heroes and tried to impale them. The monsters, called Gibbering Mouthers, spit hideous poison on our players and incapacitated them with horrid whispers from their too-many mouths. Things looked dicey early on, but Quin and Ty managed to blast two and clear the way for Thorn and Veil to go to town. In the end, Thorn crit twice in one turn, and Veil finished things off by kicking a piercing into the gibbering mouther and shutting it up for good.

With the docks secured, our heroes reflected on the fact that they had now technically completed the bare minimum of their mission. Goods could be brought in through the church and smuggled out through this aquifer. If they wanted to collect their bounty from Dante and cut and run, they could.

They also, however, identified a few issues. (1) they still didn't have an origin point to their smuggling route that didn't involve moving through the Sparkworks district and in through the church. This led them to realize that the church supply depot *had been* the original entry point, but continuing to use it would hinge on a positive relationship with Sister Ellarra (or her ignorance). (2) This smuggling route currently lacked any formal security or defenses and, if our heroes wanted their cut of the action, it would quickly be exposed to Cultist activity.

In light of this, our party decided to explore these tunnels a bit more and see what else they could learn. Ty, Veil, and Quin examined a number of different fungi in the tunnels and came to understand that there was actually an elaborate system of fungal infrastructure here. Two primary fungi had been propagated in the cavern ceilings—glow domes, which can be agitated to provide bioluminescence as a light source, and shriekers, which learn to recognize faces and provide an ear-piercing shriek when they see someone they don't recognize. But both fungi were currently dormant, in a state of torpor, disconnected from their colonies. Perhaps there was some way to bring them back online?

We ended our session with our heroes moving down the southwest passage and entering what was clearly once a major crossroads. They found a cavern, some 120-150 feet across, with numerous entrances and exits. This cavern looked less like underground tunnels and more like it was once a central dwarven thoroughfare. And as our heroes explored, they discovered a set of 5 gibbering mouthers, all already slain, seemingly by some creature that can deal damage in the form of chemical burns.

We join our heroes mid day on the 16th of Raei, investigated the caverns and deciding just how much further they want to press on before falling back to rest and strategize. Meanwhile, in the sourthern side of the Sparkworks, the Hushed Choir moves against our heroes, hoping to stamp out a sudden rebellion and consolidate their own access to these tunnels for some nefarious reasons of their own.
