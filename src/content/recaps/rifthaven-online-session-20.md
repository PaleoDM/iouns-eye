We picked up with our heroes still deep within the Moving Fortress, having just rescued the four RELICs—Rust, Rider, Clubs, and Spirit—and a still-catatonic but living Ernesto De La Alma. The battered party took stock. They were hurting, low on resources, and now responsible for six civilians. Add to that a conscious and bound Captain Reeve, and it was clear: extraction would be messy.

Before leaving the Fortress, Quinn, with help from Spirit, examined young Ernesto. The boy was savable—but not whole. Core memories had been extracted, and unless recovered, he wouldn’t return to himself. Then, Quin turned his attention to the moving fortress. He began to devise a plan to sabotage the machine. They’d send it off toward the mountains in a red herring direction, and rig the thing to fail and combust mid-journey. A nat 20 on the sabotage roll meant full control of how and when the rig would self-destruct. The House of Coinwrights would be down one fortress by morning.

Just then, help arrived. Luz De La Alma—Ernesto’s older sister—appeared at the head of a rescue party, alerted by the merchant from the Earthmovers Guild. Her reunion with Ernesto was teary, painful, and resolute. “We can fix this,” she said. Our heroes left Ernesto in her care.

Then came the matter of escape. The Sergente’s Ledgermen had imposed a hard curfew, and the only way out of the Salvation District was past a manned checkpoint. The party took a page from Quinn’s “Memo” playbook, disguising themselves as inebriated pub-crawlers. Arden sealed the deal with a **Suggestion** spell, dragging the lead guard into their revelry. They partied until he blacked out, then ditched him in an alley. The gambit worked.

Once back at the Bastion, Reeve was placed under **Zone of Truth** (thank you, Sister Ellarra), and the party got her full story. They interrogated Reeve and uncovered the unfortunate truth—she wasn’t especially loyal to the House of Coinwrights or the Sergente family. She had made a deal for coin and clout, not realizing the fine print meant that her soul would belong to Haji upon death. Understandably, she was now highly motivated to find her infernal contract and destroy it. Seeing her potential use (and lacking better options), the party agreed to bring her into their ranks as head of security back at the Bastion. Arden grilled her with some hard questions. Veil declared her incredibly likable. That was enough; they gave her a place, a title, and a shot at redemption by ensuring the four RELICs were housed and safe.

The next morning, the party returned to Salvation and found the town readying for violence. News had broken—four valuable “constructs” had been stolen from the Sergente estate, and the landowners were invoking an old property law to send enforcers door-to-door. If they couldn’t retrieve the “stolen property,” they’d levy damages against the townsfolk.

The people were bracing for it. Our heroes went to talk with Luz and Sergente's grandmother, Tota, the matriarch and head of an old Pulqueria. Tota thanked them profusely for returning her grandson and beseeched them to help the town one more time—by helping them rebuke the tax men that would be pillaging them that evening. In exchange, she offered two bottles of powerful Pulque, and her own services as a guide. She had served, as a young lady, as a house maid for the Sergentes, and she still new every coridor and secret passage of the old manor. Help her, and she'd guide our heroes right into the heart of whatever they sought.

So our heroes got to work.

They helped dig trenches and bottleneck alleys. They trained militias and motivated farmhands. They laid traps and fortified positions. Then the first wave came—armored heavies and elite archers. Veil struck from the rooftops, while Thorn locked the frontline down with a stoic fury. Ty took several powerful hits but even still, things were going reasonably well. At least until wave 2 arrived...with a mage.

We rejoin our heroes in the thick of battle, defending the soul of Salvation and rebuking these aggressors. When the dust settles, they'll need to lick their wounds and plan a proper assault into the Sergente manor to recover as much evidence and disrupt as much of the Coinwright plan as they can. And ideally, all before the Sergentes—or worse, Haji—can clean house and cover their tracks. The window to strike back is closing.
