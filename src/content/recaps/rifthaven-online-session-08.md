We re-joined our heroes in the middle of combat...again. This time, they were clearing out what had come to be known as The Killbox—a region of the tunnels that had been purposefully caved in and then lit on aflame, condemning all those within to a horrible death.

Our party had made their way through most of it, dispatching skeletal minotaurs and a strange Will-O'-Wisp, along the way destroying some strange effigies that seemed to be re-fueling the undead to keep rising over and and over again. Having mostly cleared things out, our heroes now faced a single flaming skeleton and a Will-o'-Wisp, but Thorn and Ty were looking rough.

As the battle raged on, Thorn took some heavy damage from both enemies, and then dealt it back again to the Flaming Skeleton. Arden came in with an extremely clutch turn—healing Thorn and letting everyone reposition with some clutch movement, thereby making Thorn a much less obvious and enticing target. Veil went to work destroying the remaining three effigies, and Ty and Quin stepped in to begin laying the damage down.

In the scramble that ensued, Ty was knocked unconscious and immediately rolled a natural 1 on two death saves. Then, the Will-o'-Wisp attempted to use it's "drain life" ability, which would have killed Ty properly. Thankfully, Quin had a portent remaining, and the ability was momentarily thwarted.

From there, our heroes went into absolute juggernaught mode. Veil destroyed the remaining effigies and returned to damage the Will-o'-Wisp—which she managed to do by refusing to leave its space. Quin landed a critical Ray of Frost on the Will-o'-Wisp which limited its movement, and Thorn quickly cleaned up what remained of the Flaming Skeleton. Unfortunately, it exploded.

When the dust settled, only Veil and Thorn were still standing. Thorn laid into the Will-o'-Wisp while Veil administered a healing potion to Arden, who in turn healed Ty from the bring of death, who in turn stood up and plunged his pact blade deep into the Will-o'-Wisp, finally killing it. A moment later Thorn administered a healing kit on Quin and our heroes were safe.

After a brief rest, our heroes began exploring the Killbox, finding a bout 1000 gold in valuables, a beautiful diamond necklace worth about 500 gold, a magical amulet of clockwork make, and a spell scroll of Grease and Darkvision. Finally, they made their way up into the noble's neighborhood of the Sparkworks District, where they were greeted by Shamil Humplebumple—one of Lord Harwin Brassfell's two remaining employees. She chided them for "being late" and led them up to speak with the master of the house.

As our heroes arrived in Harwin's home, they passed through a large estate with several old money manors build into the hills here. And along the way, they saw many signs of violence—it seems that the cultists and the common folk had tangled several times while our heroes were down below. Harwin greeted the characters with a friendly and welcome smile while Shamil set her husband Cog about making tea and scones for everyone. Then, Harwin delivered our heroes a letter from Dante:

> 17th of Raei, 1128
>
> **Greetings Sparkplugs,**
>
> You’ve done it. You've secured the tunnels, and in advance of some particularly nasty adversaries.
>
> They're yours. Not just half. All of it—every stone, every bolt, every forgotten path scraped back from the dark. You earned it. Not through deed or decree. Through fire and steel and grit. And maybe a little bit of blood...
>
> Harwin is a trusted ally.  He's been acting in our interests in this district since before I was born. And he's been a steady hand in this story. If nothing else, we can trust his own past to reliably anchor him to our cause. If this letter is not proof enough, consider that I have left Inferno in his care for the moment. You may speak freely with the two of them. 
>
> Say one thing about Dante, say he earns trust. I owe you an apology. The violence with the Choir—**I did not foresee its severity.** I will not pretend otherwise. For that, I offer both my apology and recompense. **One hundred gold each, sent with this letter.** It does not cover what was nearly the death of Thorn, but perhaps it's a start. I think you see now how deep this web weaves.
>
> Know this: The Choir is not broken. Harwin can update you on the violence and unrest since they entered the tunnels. They will continue to stand against you, and me, and our interests in this District. On this front, Harwin and I have a new offer you, if you're able to help us send these cockroaches scattering. If you're not yet ready to cut and run, hear him out. 
>
> As for me—I am among them now. They do not know. **Their masks hide much, and I have long been skilled at wearing the face of men.** In the days to come, I will pass you what I learn. Where they go. What they plan. Their timeline. Whatever I can.
>
> **Rest now.** But prepare. Arm up. Copy spells. Walk amongst the people. 
>
> **Be Ready.** We are not done here.
>
> —**Dante**

Our heroes learned more from Harwin, coming to understand that in the three days since they went down into the tunnels the violence really boiled over. The Ashen Chapel, under Sister Ellara, and Harwin's manor have emerged as two bases of civil disobedience, while the cultists have finally realized their losing ground and retreated to the Hearthfire Commons tenements.

Harwin also revealed to our heroes that they had scrounged up the last of his bonds and borrowed heavily from a pair known as The Cassini Brothers—a name Quin recognizes all too well—to get the Ledgermen to back off. At least now, our heroes will only need to deal with cultists. Harwin made them a tantalizing offer—expel the "cockroaches" from his district, and he would gift them his brothers old Manor and hire them a staff member of their choosing.

Why were he and Dante being so generous? As best as our players could tell, the two really believe that, by having a functional trade route and adventurers bringing back gold to their district, that they could provide a jolt to the economy that just might re-start things. Deciding that they were in, our heroes took 2-3 days to purchase some equipment, new healing potions, and gather some information.

Veil and Ty learned that the cultists have been disappearing people for some time now, and seem to be using them as sacrifices to bring forth fiends across into this plane, though for what purpose remains unclear. They also learned that Dante had previously tried a similar revolution in another district, and was caught and hanged for his crimes. The rope broke and Dante plunged into The Rift, only to resurface some two decades later. Many distrust him innately because of a minor undead presence on top of his own fiendish heritage, but our heroes became more assured he was on the up and up.

Along the way, we saw Veil receive a Ring of Inner Sight as a reward for her great work so far, and Ty had his eldritch friend expand their pact and become a Rapier of Warning.

When we rejoin our heroes, it will be the 22nd of Raei, just a week before the new year, as they plan how to assault the Hearthfire Commons and take out the cultists, presumably with as little collateral damage as possible.
