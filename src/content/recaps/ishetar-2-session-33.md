We opened with our heroes gazing across the field at the trapped juggernaut. Hrothgar had bought them a window. They weren't going to waste it.

The run was brutal. The juggernaut wasn't going quietly. It hurled boulders and massive hunks of debris across the open ground, shaking the earth with every impact. Our heroes ducked and dodged, hid when they could, and blasted rocks out of the sky with magic when they couldn't. Three checks apiece across the full distance. 15 for 15. Every single one. And before long they had converged on the trap itself, looking down at Hrothgar.

Rooker and Thump turned their attention upward, where Rooker could feel an arcane thrum emanating from beneath the chest plate. Thump could feel the life within the creature. Angry, in pain, howling. Lyra looked down and found Hrothgar alive, having driven a caltrop on a pike through the creature's foot as it collapsed into the trap, pinning it in place. For now. The juggernaut fought to get free.

Rooker headed down to check on Hrothgar while Lyra cast Bless and Azrael moved into the opening inside the tree. That's when the attack came. Azrael found himself one on one with a hideous plant creature emerging from the wood, while another rounded on Vicas. Two awakened trees harassed Lyra and Thump. Suddenly things looked dire.

Rooker was nearly felled. Then Thump was brought within an inch of his life too. He and Lyra were both grappled by a tree, pincered by gnashing bark. Lyra managed a fireball that finished off the creature Vicas was fighting, freeing him to help the others. Rooker landed a well-placed Dissonant Whispers and forced one of the trees to drop Thump and Lyra. Azrael nearly finished his off, but was promptly swallowed. Rooker took that one out before it could flee with Azrael inside it, and Thump and Lyra finished off the other.

Our heroes took a moment to collect themselves and downed a lot of potions. Hrothgar reported in. He could hold. But only minutes, not hours. No time for a rest. He spent a few hit points on Lay on Hands and sent them on their way. In they went, up into the belly of the tree.

They found two guards blocking the path. The guards spoke as the voice of the juggernaut itself, and they challenged Thump. By what right did he have to enter? Why had he caused so much pain?

Thump responded with pity. Pity for the sick and twisted existence they had known. Pity for the way they had been grown and shaped to be what they were. Pity for the anger and pain that had been cultivated in them against their nature. "Every seed knows in its heart how it ought to grow," he told them. "The mind may need to catch up, but the heart knows who it is."

The juggernaut pushed back. Thump's people had caused so much pain. They came with saws and axes. They hurt the wood and the wildlife. Even now, they had spiked its foot. But Thump followed a 22 on Nature with a natural 20 on Persuasion. He spoke as one who carries the duty of balance. Not destruction for its own sake. Not civilization either. Balance. And the juggernaut, at long last, stood down.

It showed him its power core: a seed plucked from the heart of the Myreshade, stolen by Sallow and used to grow the juggernaut twisted and corrupted. Save the seed, it asked him. Place it back at the heart of the forest and return it to a place of harmony. Thump vowed solemnly, and the juggernaut expelled Sallow from its own control.

With the creature no longer fighting them, our heroes pressed on. They skirted past a stake trap and a pit trap. Then they came upon a cloud of poisonous spores that would have stunned whoever breathed it. Thump and Azrael worked together carefully and extracted a single dose intact, enough to coat a weapon and deliver the stun on contact. A potent brew indeed.

We leave our heroes arriving at the juggernaut's command center, ready to confront Sallow and bring this arc to its end.
