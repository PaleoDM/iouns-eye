We joined our heroes *in media res*, having literally just defeated a troublesome mage, two of them unconscious and one of them dead. Quin looted the dead mage's spellbook while Veil looted whatever she could off of the bandit captain. Then, at the prompting of the young scamp they had moved to save (a lad named Zeke), they dragged everyone into the home of an elderly brewer woman who still lived in this neighborhood. She found them an old smuggling cellar in her basement and ushered them all in. This proved a tad difficult with Thorn's bulk, but Quin and Veil managed, eventually getting everyone inside to hide and rest.

The elderly brewer sent Zeke out on a mission: to alert the temple that their serrvices would be needed and to "light a small spark" amongst the citizens. Zeke looked nervous, especially every time he caught sight of Thorn's corpse, but rose to the task. Over the next few hours, Ty and then Arden awoke, each managing a short rest and rolling a few hit dice. In the intervening time, Ledgermen and began harassing citizens, and our heroes heard the sounds of a neighborhood becoming increasingly agitated. The elderly brewer woman, who never gave her name, rebuked them at her doorstep twice, each time insisting they move along.

Finally, when they were gone, she had our heroes load Thorn into a barrel of "wine" to be delivered to the temple. The rest of them donned cloaks and followed her cart as best as they could. Veil, nervous at the tension and apprehension in the air, took to the rooftops and the shadows as much as possible. Along the way, it became clear that the citizens of the Sparkworks District had become exceedingly unhappy with cultist and Ledgermen outsiders imposing their will—on one occasion our party rounded a corner to find a Ledgerman hanged from a lamp post.

When they arrived at The Ashen Chapel, they found a chaotic scene. Numerous civilians had shown up, many of them donating whatever meager family jewelry they had containing diamonds. As our heroes began asking questions, one theme materialized: the townsfolk were rallying around our heroes for having stood up for the children., and Zeke had made sure to spread the word. A resurrection scroll, donated by Lord Harwin, had been entrusted to Sister Ellarra to use as per her judgement. After interrogating our heroes carefully, she elected to use it on Thorn.

It was not without risk, made doubly so by the fact that outside in the courtyard tensions were increasing. It seemed that he cultists have arrived, looking to make a public display against the civil unrest and undermine the local house of worship. Sister Ellarra revived Thorn, and quickly pumped a Cure Wounds into him, just in time. The cultists blasted their way past the villagers—one of them leading a fiendish creature with four arms and a wicked trident.

Battle broke out in the sanctuary of his holy temple. Arden was knocked down almost immediately, and Quin just barely survived a similar fate. Veil, Ty, and Thorn leapt into the fray, quickly mowing their way through the mooks and looking to pump damage into the fiend, which they did with gusto. Arden came dangerously close to death when two cultists struck hime while down, but Sister Ellarra just managed to heal him in time. With the mooks coming off the board, the fiend cast darkness on itself, buying it some precious time and allowing it to deal damage from a position of advantage. However, a clever use of faerie fire and some fortunate rolls from Veil to disrupt concentration on the priest's spirit guardians allowed our heroes to blast the fiend into oblivion.

In the aftermath, our heroes were forced to confront the awkward situation they found themselves—having accidentally stood up to the bullies and inspiring the common folk to fight back. Not wanting to truly step into the role of "heroes", our party contented themselves with availing themselves of a long rest in the temple. Veil kept a low profile. Arden went out to speak to the masses, and even passed on his trident to an old farmer and his son who were quickly materializing as leaders of the resistance.

And Ty took a long walk into the catacombs with Sister Ellarra. Here, he learned that there are three known entrances into the tunnels they seek: here in the chapel (The Ashen Chapel), beneath the monument to the revolution (The Graves of the Revolution), and beneath Lord Harwin's Manor. Ty also learned from gossiping with the local teens that: (1) the Hushed Choir operate out of a gambling den and fighting pit in the Northwest side called The Brazier; and (2) they had a massively increased interest in an old tenement building in the Southwest side called The Hearthfire Commons.

Armed with all this information, our heroes discussed whether they should pursue their mission, or deal with the cultists. On the one hand, they saw little reason to shed blood fighting cultists only to learn that the tunnels wouldn't be operational. On the other, they worried that by entering the tunnels they'd expose their backs to the cultists. In the end, they decided to enter and investigate a little, and reserve the right to fall back and re-assess.

We join our heroes having just entered the tunnels, solving their darkvision situation, and making their way deep enough to meet the Sparkplugs: the district's namesake insects that trawl the underground and like to animate mechanisms into motion. Their delight was cut short, however, as Ty lead the way into a den that smells of rotting flesh, and they got one quick look at a hideous, elongated tongue moving deep in the shadows.
