We opened mid-combat, with our heroes still holding the line against the drowned master, several assassins, and a swarm of waterlogged undead. The zombie T-Rex had returned—badly singed but no less furious—and the dire peryton had descended from above, its antlered silhouette blotting out the stars.

The fight was chaotic and punishing. Azrael narrowly avoided a vicious T-Rex bite that would have dropped them outright. Vicas spent the second half of the battle inches from death, dodging and weaving on sheer willpower alone. Meanwhile, Lyra and Thump locked down the drowned master with clever spellwork, limiting its ability to command the field.

Then the peryton did something unexpected: it turned on the T-Rex, raking it with talons and dealing devastating damage. Worse still for the master, it cursed him with a bane-like effect that began to unravel his control. The creature then wheeled upward and began clawing through the monastery roof, seemingly intent on fighting its way through to our heroes.

Amid the chaos, Lyra caught a glimpse of something impossible: a golden angelic figure descending from the heavens, casting a spell somewhere out in the garden. She had no time to process it before the battle demanded her full attention.

That's when Lyra channeled the full radiance of Pelor and cast *Turn Undead*, catching both the drowned master and the T-Rex in its light. The T-Rex fled immediately, crashing through the outer wall. The master, reeling, ordered a full retreat—and in that moment, our heroes discovered something unsettling. The undead weren't simply following orders; they operated as a hive mind. When the master broke, everything broke with it.

Vicas, Azrael, and Rooker seized the opening and dispatched the remaining mooks before they could scatter.

As the T-Rex fled toward the garden, a pillar of fire erupted from somewhere near the treeline. When the smoke cleared, our heroes saw the impossible: **Archais**, the elven wizard they had watched die on the bell tower—heart torn out by the peryton—standing with his hand still raised, the charred remains of the T-Rex crumbling behind him. A golden glow from his chest suggested he had been resurrected.

Above, the peryton had nearly clawed its way through the monastery roof when Lyra hit it with *Mending*, repairing the stones as fast as the creature could tear them apart. With a screech of frustration, the peryton declared its efforts hopeless and fucked off into the night.

With the major threats neutralized, Thump turned his attention to the drowned assassin still lurking on the battlefield, striking it with *Call Lightning*. Our heroes bent their ire on it together, but Vicas learned the hard way that the creature bore an *Armor of Agathys*-like enchantment—every melee strike dealt retaliatory cold damage. Vicas nearly killed himself in the exchange, but held on just long enough for Thump to finish it off with another bolt from above.

In the aftermath, our heroes watched as the magical *Dawn* Lyra had cast the night before gave way to the real thing. Somewhere offshore, the Shooting Star had weathered its own battle; they could see movement on deck, but no distress signals. Rooker cast *Sending* to Vero to report their status—and learned, to everyone's alarm, that she had died in the fighting. "But I got better," she added, which raised more questions than it answered.

When our heroes finally reconvened with the Shooting Star, they found **Janore** thoroughly missing. They feared he had fallen in the fighting—but every single person aboard the ship insisted that Janore had been by their side all night, healing them through the worst of it.

**Deft** sat at the base of the mast, quietly whittling. When they approached, he showed them his work: a scrimshaw carved into a sperm whale tooth, depicting the Shooting Star bathed in a beam of golden dawn light from above. Then, without a word, he pointed up toward the crow's nest.

Vicas and Azrael climbed to find Janore—or what remained of him. His body had been transmuted into crystal, frozen in a posture of serene exhaustion. Two hollow sockets gaped where his eyes had once been. On his person, they found a scroll.

> Sister Lyra,
>
> Though it was millennia ago, for us elves and dwarves it was not so many generations ago that your **Dawnfather** and my **Knowing Mistress** stood together to banish **The Chained Oblivion.** I am grateful the stakes were lower tonight, but no less proud to have stood beside you in battle and see their divine might aligned once more.
>
> Here in the crow's nest, basking beneath the holy dawn, I feel myself truly and thoroughly laid bare. Through your divine light, my Mistress has allowed me to Know myself true. The cost of my already expended mortal vessel feels suddenly such a small price to pay for the gift you have made possible. 
>
> I am told that Ishetar is famous for its second chances and refusal to go quietly. I pray my quiet monastery take notes.
>
> \~ Brother Janore, holy priest of the Ioun Knowing Mistress.

Slowly, the pieces came together. Janore's body had given out during the night—whether from bluerot, exhaustion, or sheer strain, they couldn't say. But Lyra's *Dawn* spell had granted him something miraculous: a divine intervention. And he had rolled the hit. With that intervention, Janore had cast *Raise Dead* not once but twice, bringing back both Archais and Vero at the cost of his own eyes—material components offered freely in his final act of devotion. The golden angelic figure Lyra had glimpsed in the garden had been Janore himself, transformed by Ioun's grace, keeping everyone healed during the night.

Later, Lyra cast *Speak with Dead* on one of the fallen corpses to learn what she could. The answers were grim. The undead had been dragged to a place called **the Rift** and made into sentries for **Syrgaul the Drowned**, a death knight who served the demon prince **Orcus**. Their goal: raise an army large enough to wage war on land in Orcus's name. The triton and locathah who once guarded the Rift had been slain. The waters now stood undefended near the atoll where Syrgaul's cursed ship, the *Tammeraut*, had sunk. The corpse warned that no soldiers or fortifications would prove as deadly as the reef itself—nearly unnavigable without proper charts.

We leave our heroes in the early morning light, tending to wounds both physical and spiritual. Several among them suffer from bluerot; Rooker has the particular misfortune of suffering from both bluerot *and* sewer plague. Firewatch Island lies in shambles, its role as a western lookout now deeply uncertain. And Ishetar waits for their return—unaware of how much the world has shifted in their absence.
