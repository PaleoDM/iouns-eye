We opened with a series of flashbacks, centering on **Azrael** and his growing bond with **Th0l**. Azrael shared more of his story—how he had worked tirelessly to change his lot in life and invested deeply in building a new identity for himself. He spoke of **Ishetar** as a place where people could begin again, where they could be known and loved for who they truly were.

Th0l, in his dry and somewhat chiding way, revealed how different their lives had been. Raised among nobility, bullied for his intellect, and trained as both a warrior and a poet, Th0l had little in common with Azrael’s scrappy path to self-reinvention. Yet the two found mutual respect, and Azrael was heartened to learn that Th0l would indeed return to Ishetar—if only to send word to his commanding officer, **Nylis**, across the sea in the continent of **Noon**.

Meanwhile, **Lyra** performed a powerful ritual with the aid of **Cyrenna**, the water nymph, channeling divine energy to create a true *Greater Restoration*. Through it, she restored **Joy**, Th0l’s adventuring companion, to life. Th0l was overwhelmed with gratitude—relieved that he would not have to face Joy’s husband bearing only sorrowful news. The reunion was tearful and cathartic, a long-deserved moment of hope amid hardship.

Time passed, and Ishetar moved fully into winter. The town celebrated the **Moonweaver’s Full Moon Festival**, a rare alignment where all three moons shone full and high above the sea. As the reflections shimmered across the coast, Lyra took a step back from public life. The Temple of the Raven Queen began to draw more attention during the cold months, giving Lyra a brief reprieve from her role as the town’s foremost spiritual voice.

It was during this quiet period that **Lady Rowena** came to the heroes with a new plea. She had received a dire message from her aunt, the **Duchess Lyla O'Rin of Emberspost**, who ruled the coastal city across the bay. Rowena and the Duchess had once been close—Rowena had spent several summers there in her youth—but their relationship had cooled over the years. When Ishetar was first founded, the Duchess had cast the deciding vote in favor of Rowena’s leadership, a move that had surprised many who feared Ishetar’s creation would compete with Emberspost’s trade. Though that decision had left Rowena indebted to her aunt, the Duchess had never called in the favor—until now.

The message was urgent. The Duchess claimed to be gravely ill and that danger loomed over Emberspost. She requested Rowena’s immediate aid, invoking the favor owed to her. Bound by honor and obligation, Rowena agreed—and our heroes volunteered to accompany her.

The message had been delivered through a teleportation device, which they used to travel directly to Emberspost. The spell was one-way; they would have to return by foot or ship. Undeterred, the party stepped through.

They arrived in a lush, enclosed garden within what appeared to be a palace—and immediately overheard a heated argument between two young nobles. As Azrael crept forward to listen, the group realized too late that they had arrived in the middle of an assassination attempt. Several spectral, scorpion-like forms emerged from the Ethereal Plane, striking at the nobles. One fell instantly.

Chaos erupted. Guards scrambled to defend their charges, but several were slain before the heroes could intervene. **Vicas** and **Thump** moved to intercept the creatures, while **Lyra** and **Rowena** provided quick healing and protection. Thanks to Lyra’s radiant magic, the party exposed an **Invisible Stalker**—the unseen assassin behind the attack.

We rejoin our heroes in the midst of that battle, the air alive with tension. The nobles cling to life, the stalker flits unseen through the garden, and the party fights to bring the threat down before it can finish what it started.
