We opened just after the harrowing battle on the Myreshade River. Bruised but still standing, the party caught their breath and took a short rest. As they regrouped, Sombra checked in with each of them, and the group held a brief conversation about the buddy system—especially after Vicas pointed out that they had gotten their teeth kicked in. Sombra, attempting to claim Lyra as her buddy, was reminded that Lyra and Thump are already a standing pair. Begrudgingly, she resigned herself to being partnered with Rooker, again.

The party pressed forward into the **Myreshade Forest**, following rough game trails and working their way up a series of switchbacks along a wooded hillside. They heard music and laughter ahead, and Vicas and Azrael scouted forward, spotting two separate groups of **satyrs** camped on either side of the road. Though it didn’t look like an ambush, the satyrs’ fey nature put everyone on edge. The group attempted to walk through without incident, but the satyrs were persistent, taunting Azrael and suggesting he would make a perfect “Dirk Adventureman” with a little thespian training. When the head satyr loudly asked, “How much for him?” referring to Azrael, the situation escalated.

The ensuing fight was chaotic. Vicas landed a clutch Stunning Strike before one of the leaders could lock the party down with his enchanted lute. Still, several party members fell under the satyrs’ magic, compelled to dance and becoming incapacitated. Paradoxically, the satyrs couldn't do enough damage to capitalize, in part because Lyra had blinded several of them, so the fight became a frustrating grind. Lyra, growing increasingly angry, swore none of them would escape. Eventually, the party finished the job, though they were badly injured and more than ready to reach their destination.

That destination, **The Restful Lily**, was not what anyone expected. The old stories had pointed to a temple or a wizard’s tower, but what they found was a Victorian-style manor house, surrounded by a low brick wall and well-kept gardens. A wrought iron arch welcomed them:

> *“Welcome to the Restful Lily*
>
> *House of Rest and Respite*
>
> *Bed & Breakfast.”*

Confused by the presentation, Rooker began casting *Detect Magic* and Thump prepared *Speak with Animals*. Azrael stood to watch over them while Lyra and Vicas scouted ahead. They knocked on the front gate and were greeted by **Saeth**, a refined and formal porter. He offered a full menu of spa services: hot springs, massages, scale polishing, facials, and more! He also informed them that, since they had dealt with some troublesome satyrs, the first night would be **on the house**.

Reviewing the guest services menu, the group noticed one additional entry, written in moon runes which are only visible under moonlight or during the month of Sehanine. This one read *“For the broken spirits and tattered hearts, healing upon request.”* This, along with the general vibe of the place, left Lyra deeply suspicious. She suspected hags—fey or otherwise—and the group reconvened to finish their rituals before proceeding.

Rooker’s *Detect Magic* lit up with powerful enchantment, alongside traces of illusion and necromancy. He deduced that the entire estate was under a pervasive glamour, making everything seem unnaturally pleasant. He also spotted the outline of an invisible obelisk-shaped structure in the backyard—possibly a hidden tower. Meanwhile, Thump spoke to a local squirrel named **Cheeks**, who cheerfully shared that guests do come and go—though they often leave changed. She also mentioned that one of the owners was very sweet in public, but rather cruel in private.

Despite their suspicions, the party’s investigations (and a natural 20) suggested they were not in immediate danger of stepping into a fey-style word trap or unintentional contract. Hags, after all, play the long game. They decided it was safe to enter for now. As they were led to their rooms, they passed through a gorgeous sitting room with strange and powerfully magical portraits surrounding the mantle. Room keys were issued: Rooker and Sombra, Lyra and Azrael, and Thump and Vicas. From his room, Rooker could see a third structure on the premises: a crumbling and abandoned building that looked almost certainly off limits. They settled in for the night, planning to rest and begin exploring in earnest the next morning—ready to learn what secrets lie beneath the pleasant surface of The Restful Lily.
