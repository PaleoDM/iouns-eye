We rejoined our heroes in the aftermath of their confrontation with **Father Assam**, who had been decisively defeated. A search of his belongings and a careful assessment of his condition revealed several important truths.

Most notably, Father Assam had not been possessed, magically compelled, or influenced by any extraplanar force, but he was not entirely of his right mind either. Instead, he appeared to have acted entirely of his own volition. His extreme devotion to the Dawnfather, combined with growing resentment toward Duchess Lyla’s leadership, had led him to sincerely believe that removing her was both necessary and righteous. Lyra assured the party that the Church maintains established procedures for addressing clerics whose zeal turns harmful, but the situation was nonetheless unsettling. Assam was not a pawn—he was a fanatic, driven mad by his own frustration, who believed himself justified.

It also became clear that Assam’s original plan had been slow and subtle. The Duchess’s poisoning was meant to appear natural, unfolding over time. The party’s intervention—curing Lyla and then staging her death—had forced Assam into increasingly desperate actions. By the time of their confrontation, he was operating without allies, resources, or a viable endgame.

With onlookers beginning to gather and tensions rising, the party acted quickly. **Vicas** transported Father Assam directly to the palace by air, where he could be placed under guard. **Azrael** and **Lyra** attempted to manage the situation on the ground and calm the crowd, but with Lyra visibly armored and bearing the symbol of the Dawnfather, the public reaction was uneasy at best. The crowd dispersed without incident, but concern and uncertainty lingered.

The group did not linger. With Assam accounted for, attention turned to the more dangerous unknown: **Vicar Xona**, who remained at large.

Based on what they knew, Xona was most likely to strike either the **Mushin Majilis** (the parliamentary assembly) or the **Anis Academy**, home to the great library and the Temple of the Moonweaver. Calling upon **Old King One-Eye** and supplementing the search with **Augury**, Lyra confirmed their fears: assassins were already in motion, and Parliament was the target.

Faced with a difficult choice—pursue Xona directly or protect the assembly—the party chose to intervene at the Majilis. They arrived just in time to thwart the attack.

The ensuing battle was brief and largely controlled. **Azrael** engaged and defeated one of the most dangerous assassins present. **Lyra’s Spirit Guardians** limited enemy movement and prevented coordinated strikes. **Thump** and **Vicas** applied consistent pressure across the battlefield, while **Rooker**, after a moment of risk, stabilized and eliminated remaining threats. **Lady Rowena** provided critical healing to her cousins, and Lyra ensured that wounded nobles and attendants survived the encounter.

It was clear from the attackers’ tactics that the primary targets were the two Dukes and several high-ranking political figures. All survived. In the aftermath, Duke Simbon’s visible defense of an unconscious noblewoman did not go unnoticed, and the political consequences of that moment were immediately apparent.

With Parliament secured, the party moved quickly to the **Anis Academy**, unwilling to let Xona slip away. After navigating the library and disarming a trapped entrance, they entered Xona’s private cloister.

They found her dead.

**Vicar Xona** had been hanged by strands of spiderweb. The scene suggested divine or extraplanar retribution rather than murder by mortal hands. The Moonweaver’s influence was evident: shattered stained glass and seven distinct burn marks, each depicting a phase of the moon, radiated from the site. At the same time, the method and symbolism pointed to **Lolth**, the Spider Queen—Xona’s true patron.

Further investigation revealed the broader conspiracy. Xona had been a long-term sleeper agent planted in Ember’sPost by an assassin organization known as **the Seven Eyes**, acting on behalf of high ranking political figures in **Oglin**. Her assignment had not always been active; rather, she had been positioned to influence the city if and when the political situation demanded it. That moment had now passed—and with her identity exposed, she had become a liability.

A **Magic Mouth** left behind confirmed this assessment. It acknowledged the party as capable adversaries and warned them, in measured terms, that further interference would draw attention. The implication was clear: Xona had been eliminated to prevent her from revealing more.

The party wasn't exactly comforted to have attracked the attention of a group such as the Seven Eyes.

We join our heroes now with a period of transition. The political situation in Ember’s Post begins to stabilize. Duchess Lyla prepares to reappear publicly, Parliament regroups, and remaining loose ends are addressed. Arrangements are made for the party’s return to Ishetar by sea aboard the **Shooting Star**, captained by the aging **Captain Alba Skylar**.
