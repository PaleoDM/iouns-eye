We picked up with our heroes having spent some downtime and now getting ready to help Quin after he was "pressured" by the Cassini brothers. Quin petitioned his party members—if they would help him with this aspect of his past, they would have him fully committed moving forward. It didn't take much convinced. As Veil pointed out—they were pretty deep into business together now.

The task was simple: heist Quin's memories back from the Cassini brothers. To do this, Quin called in a little help from an old flakey colleague Cliff Tuskegee, a Loxodon janitor. Quin and the party met Cliff at "Dan's Diner", a front for the Cassini brothers, jamming out to *Bane Eat World* on the jukebox. Cliff was able to give Quin some important intelligence. In a few night's time, the Cassini brothers would be hosting a major event—an airship race that would pass over the casino in the Glamour District. There would be enough high rollers in town that "the cage" would be emptied out to make room to secure everyone's collateral. Instead, the Cassini's assets would be moved a few blocks down to *The Night Reserve*, an annex of one of the main bands associsated with the House of Coinwrights. Security would be light, and they had a window of opportunity.

But Quin also needed to be a little extra, and to leverage against the Cassini brothers. He made himself a persona, vis disguise self, **The Great Memo (Memorio)**. The plan was simple—pose as a super high roller and talk himself into the vault to "deposit assets". It worked, and quickly. Quin won big and attracted the attention of Oleg Cassini, who paired him his hired hand Major Dunthar.

The party was escorted past all of the security, all of the traps, basically everything, and walked themselves right into the vault. Ty charmed Dunthar into letting them into a vault with many ledgers, but things went sideways when an internal construct prompted combat. It was short lived though—Thorn took the opportunity to knock out Dunthar while Veil and Ty beat the absolute crap out of the constructs.

In the aftermath, Thorn destroyed a mechanical contraption in the center of the room, powering down most of the remaining traps. Quin scooped up some important ledgers marked "The Book of Souls" and "Debts, Deals, and Devils". Unfortunately, they also set off the alarm.

Moving quickly, Arden went to hold back the doorman (and his mechanical golem), while our heroes broke into the vault and moved to destroy it's golem guardian and steal the memory(s) that they came for. Quin scooped in the memories while Veil and Thorn held the front line. Meanwhile, Ty provided incredibly important support to Arden, who was alone distracting the doorman.

As our heroes made their escape, Thorn and Quin ran into some crucial friction. Thorn vehemently objected to stealing money, but Quin wasn't here for morals. They argued back and forth, with Thorn insisting that one memory crystal in particular needed to remain behind— a crystal with a mysterious leaf symbol etched into it. Thorn it back and walked out, but Quin picked it back up anyway...

On the way out, Quin found himself facing Dr. Solvane, a creepy man with a smile that extends to his ears, which are themselves several inches long and too pointed even for an elf. Solvane sought a conversation, but Quin denied. Dejected, Solvane hit Quin with a hold person, and implored him: "I don't care for Oleg and Pery. I can get them off your scent for this. I just want to be able to monitor the experiment. You're the only one that's ever been a success you know."

Quin had little patience for the practical experiments, and Solvane let him retreat to his theory. Our heroes fled, and we finished with them around the table in their estate, having a job well done. Veil learned that, in addition to memories, the Cassini brothers trade in souls. They trap souls of the dead to prevent their resurrection, and control of those souls is big money among Rifthaven's upper crust.

Finally, we finished with Ty and a new memory of a former life. He saw himself, in a previous life at about 8 years old, in a slave camp in the terrible dens of the drow before they had fled to Tyr'Zarety. An old matriarch told him the story of the Moonweaver and her secret 4th moon: a gift to the grow to lead them from the subjugation of her sister the Spider Queen. We saw little Ty flee into the tunnels and make a new friend, glowing with subterranean moonlight—the magical hollyphant Lulu. And we finished with the old matriarch hanged from the ethereal threads of spider thread.

We next pick up with our heroes ready to visit Tyr'Zarety, meet Ty's parents, and learn what the hell is going on with him...
