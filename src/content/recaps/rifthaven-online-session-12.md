### Begin level 5

We picked up with our heroes on the 23rd of Raei, having jut defeated the cultists and moving their way through the loot and exposition before moving into downtime. Our heroes began through the process of picking through the cultist's items and looking for any clues or any signs that there may be others in danger. In the process, Quin and Arden discovered a hefty loot hoard, most of which definitely belonged to the citizens of the Sparkworks before the extortion began. Being proper, noble heroes, they began coordinating to have it divided up as honestly as possible.

Lord Harwin passed on the responsibility—citing the optics of the only remaining noble deciding how to partition wealth as a poor fit—but he brought in the aging tabaxi revolutionary Whisper, who set to work. For their trouble, our heroes got to keep 5,000 gold, two diamonds for resurrection, 2 healing potions, and three magic items: A *Wand of Detect Magic,* a *Bag of Holding*, and a *Nature's Mantle.* Quin kept the wand and gave Veil the mantle, which allows her to hide as a bonus action.

Meanwhile, the others learned some valuable exposition:

> -   Ty discovered several pouches of "quicksilver", a mercury-like substance that shifts between liquid and solid at room temperature. Ty recognized it by its proper name—Selenualite, or 4th Moon Pearl. It's mined and refined by the clergy in his home district of Tyr'Zarety from the shores of Lake Umbrathir. And it should very much NOT be here—the clergy of the Moonweaver typically only sell tiny amounts and it's tightly regulated.
>
> -   Quin recognized one of the dead cult leaders as a man named Vandro Morrix, the owner of the Gilded Ember Gambling Hall—an up and coming gambling hall that was aiming to rival the operations owned by the Cassini Brothers. Oops, looks like our heroes accidentally helped them out a tad,
>
> -   Arden recognized another face as well. Once the mask came off, he learned that one of the leaders had been Seressa Vale, the owner of the Brass Wing. The Brass Wing was the mercantile company that owned an entire fleet of corsair ships, including the one Arden had served on. Though he didn't know Seressa personally, Arden suspected this would spell trouble for his old crew, who's boss was suddenly killed.
>
> -   Elsewhere, Thorn uncovered a number of ledgers and missives that unequivocally linked these cultists to more important factions in the upper districts. Several of them showed that the cultists were being fundedm through a few intermediaries, by the House of Coinwrights—one of the premier money lending and banking institutions here in Rifthaven. Unfortunately, there was no "loosed crossbow bolt", and our heroes lack the contacts to do anything about it even if there were.
>
> -   Veil uncovered one set of letters, nearly all burned away and covered up, that bore a curious name. These letters seemed to be giving orders to at least one cult member, and alluding to larger machinations that would be to come. And they were signed by one "Still-Ashen-Echo".

From there, we transitioned to the less cinematic but very real business of rebuilding. We saw our heroes mingle with the citizens of the Sparkworks, now their neighbors, in the local tavern. Here, Arden slipped away momentarily, and the party was treated to a remarkable personal compositon by the mysterious bard Bardrick Stonechord. It was epic (**thank you Dillon!**).

After some celebration, and some mourning, our heroes began about the process of making their newly gifted estate somewhat livable and functional. Veil established a *Tea House*, that serves to bring her rumors and gossip, as well as a *Storehouse* to facilitate the trade route they suddenly oversee. Which, speaking of...our heroes were left wondering if perhaps that's now just legal? Is it really smuggling if you own the district?

Quin set up an *Arcane Study*, and hired (built?) himself a new apprentice—a small, roomba-like automaton that helps do some chores around the place, as well as a *Library* that facilitates their important research. Thorn established a rooftop community *Garden*, which allows him to harvest rations for the district or produce medicinal herbs as necessary. To complement this, Ty also established a *Garden*, though with a focus on herbs and poison rather than food and healing.

Meanwhile, Arden set up a *Barracks*, to house and recruit many of the veterans from the revolution and put them to work as defenders of Sparkworks. Thorn also set up an *Armory* to equip all of their new recruits with arms and armor, and Ty set up a *Smithy* to give our party access to cheaper materials. Finally, Arden chose to bolster these efforts with a *Workshop*, which gives our party access to all manners of crafting and research.

Collectively, our heroes now employ about 25 people—12 recruited soldiers, 12 full time employees working throughout their estate, and 1 roomba. As a final thank you and housewarming preset, Lord Harwin gifted them a custom contraption he had built some years ago—a *Sending Crystal*. This large machine looks like a grammaphone, and gets installed in their base of operations. It can make and receive sending spells with a longer duration and no word restriction, so long as the recipient has access to their own or a means of communicating with the device. Ty's mom and dad immediately called, admonished him for not checking in, and inisted they throw a house warming party soon. Landed gentry? Goodness...so proud!

As the days ticked by with our heroes preparing, we saw them all travel to the Rift's edge for a new year's holiday! As the calendar ticked from 1128 to 1129, people were out in full force celebrating the coming of Avandra. The holiday includes a lot of "recycling" and "re-purposing" tings that have become damaged or broken, and generally preparing for good tidings in the new year. Our heroes travelled to the Rift's Edge to watch the sunset, and soon found themselves responding on a bell toll from Veil's monastery.

Our heroes fought off some strange fungal monsters and two hooked-horrors, only to find themsleves face to face with a monster known as a Violet Fungus Necrohulk. It hit hard, and soon had Ty and Veil in rough shape. Twice the creature tried and narrowly failed to "subsume" Ty into its mass of writhing, grafted flesh. Thankfully, in the end Thorn and Arden were able to output the damage needed to bring it down—helped along by Quin's first use of fireball!

We finished with our heroes returning to base and Quin discovering a particularly unpleasant note—from the Cassini Brothers. It read:

> We hear you've find some entrepreneurial success. Congratulations. But don't forget. We still [own]{.underline} you. We've got a job for you. Near the end of the month. Your friends are welcome, but their pay is out of your cut.

Quin was nearly resolved to having to pull this job, but then inspiration struck him. What if, instead, they heist away the stolen memory that the Cassini brothers hold over him as collateral?

We join our heroes on the 15th of Avandra, in a new year, having spent two weeks getting their affairs in order while we wait to see how Quin wants to approach this new job.
