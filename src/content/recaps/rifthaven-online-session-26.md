We joined our heroes on the verge of combat. After flying up into the highlands of Boughshadow, they came upon an old set of ruins long forgotten from the maps. At the center stood a cracked well, and as they approached to investigate, Veil overheard whispers in Deep Speech—a planned ambush. The warning spared them from surprise as primeval owlbears and a pair of spectators fell upon them.

The opening was rough. The owlbears’ screech rattled the party, dealing heavy damage. Quinn locked one down with *Hideous Laughter* while Veil stunned and dismantled a spectator. Reinforcements arrived—smaller owlbears and another spectator—but a critical *Chromatic Orb* from Quinn turned the tide, chaining devastation across the battlefield. With Thorn holding strong, Ty and Arden shoring up the line, and Veil carving through, the party mopped up the rest.

After a short rest, they investigated further. The well had broken through into an underdark cavern, the site of a grisly battle between kobolds and strange, bladed creatures called Kruthiks. Exploring the chamber, Veil uncovered Thieves’ Cant markings pointing toward a local witch. Taking the hint, they descended fully and followed the trail.

Soon they reached a gate wrought of bone, crowned with a minotaur skull knocker. Veil offered gifts of cheese, smoked meats, and baked goods, which proved well chosen—Nanny Pu’Pu revealed a sweet tooth and welcomed them in. Her garden was strange: disturbed graves, composting mushrooms, and on her porch, three pairs of Silverweave legionnaire boots—odd, given her claim she hadn’t seen visitors in centuries.

Nanny Pu’Pu herself was hunched and cataract-eyed, her skin dark and weathered. She spoke playfully of “Grandfather Bones,” the god of death she claimed to have kept at bay by flirting with him. The heroes pressed for answers: what had happened to Boughshadow, why she lingered here, and what she knew of the caverns. Slowly, the truth emerged.

The people of Boughshadow had been devoured by a dragon that sought to claim the grove and its ancient tree. Pu’Pu had placed wards on the tree, but its magic drew predators. The dragon, in turn, was slain by something worse—likely the beholder whose spawn the party had already fought. She confirmed the grove held one of seven cuttings of the World Tree. Each bestowed a gift of mortal existence: one cutting granted language itself, and the one here in Boughshadow, the gift of dreams—both nightly visions and mortal aspirations.

The conversation turned when Quinn pressed about memories. Could they be stored, replaced, or altered? Was he truly the monster he feared he had once been? Pu’Pu deflected with philosophy: what did it matter? But when Quinn demanded the power to know if he wished, she agreed to teach—if the party could pay her price.

That price was steep: a dragon egg. Pu’Pu insisted the fallen dragon had left a clutch, and she wanted one. She never explained why, but Veil suspected the errand was meant to put the heroes directly in the path of the beholder. Still, the bargain stood.

With that, Pu’Pu directed them toward the cavern entrance. She warned of powerful monsters guarding the way and urged the party to rest well, for once inside the caverns there would be no guarantee of safety—especially where a beholder dwelled.

We join our heroes as they descend toward the caverns, preparing to clear the entrance and decide how to face the horrors waiting beyond.
