We picked up with our heroes still deep in the Beholder’s lair, carrying the weight of the previous session’s losses. Quincrux had been disintegrated, Arden had been brought back exhausted and heavily penalized, and the party was trying to regroup and decide their next steps. Before returning to the present, we took a moment out-of-world to follow each of the characters through short flashback sequences. These were not visions the characters experienced in the lair; they were scenes shown to us as players to help reconnect with each character after the shake-up of the last battle.

Ty’s flashback took us to his childhood, sneaking through his father’s office after having a dagger confiscated. There he found an old sketchbook filled with charcoal drawings of his parents together with a third figure—a dark-elf man—shown again and again aboard a ship, all three of them clearly happy. This seemed to mark the start of Ty’s fascination with pirates, even if the man’s identity remained unknown. When his father caught him with the sketchbook, Ty expected anger, but instead watched him collapse to his knees, begging Ty through a broken voice to leave. The last thing Ty heard was his father sobbing uncontrollably as he ran from the room.

> "*Were my parents in a throuple?*"

Leon’s flashback began with him as a young boy being delivered to the Aletheia orphanage after his father was executed for a serious crime and his mother abandoned him in grief. A tall, armored Justicar—his mentor—told him life here would be hard but that he had a chance to make something of himself. At seventeen, as the other boys left to join the military, Leon chose to remain and enter Justicar training, wanting to do true good in the world. His mentor asked him why they bestow the helm before the blade, answering her own question with, “Any fool can cut flesh; it takes skill to cut to the truth,” before fitting him for his mask.

In the next scene, Leon swore his oath, affirming that the Aletheia Procedure is infallible. He was given his first blade after bringing in his first bounty alive and was required to personally carry out the execution, which he did without hesitation, receiving his next assignment immediately afterward. A montage followed of the ninety-eight successful collections that built his career. Finally, we saw the moment at Nanny Pu’Pu’s home when she showed him two contradictory memory crystals—and the runes on his blade bearing Quincrux’s name vanished, breaking the certainty he had built his entire life upon.

Thorn’s flashback took us to the day Silverweave Ventures attacked his people and uprooted the great tree his community protected. We saw Thorn fight for the first time with the raw, instinctive rage that would later define him, though he wasn’t present when the tree was finally torn from the earth. When he reached the crater where the tree once stood, an elderly matriarch lay dying and cut from her own flesh the tattoo that bore the symbol of earth and loam, giving it to Thorn and warning him that he must seek help from others—but that someone among them had betrayed their people. This was the moment Thorn’s relationship with rage crystallized: not as emotion, but as something that simply *happens*, the way rain makes a person wet, anger arriving only when needed.

Freddie’s flashback showed the fallout from a major business deal collapsing after he and Arden clashed over its details. Their family salvaged their reputation, but what should have been a celebration became instead a gathering to announce their sister’s entry into the marriage market. Freddie confronted Arden as he tried to slip away, telling him bluntly that he wasn’t cut out for leadership and needed to run before he dragged the family down any further. Arden fired back that Freddie was naive and didn’t understand what it meant to care about the things he was so casually dismissing. Their sister intervened, begging them to shake hands and part on better terms, which they did—begrudgingly. The scene made clear that the brothers see the world in completely different ways.

Veil’s flashback took us to her adolescence at the monastery, where her mentor, Stone Whisper Bloom of Sundering—known to most simply as Sir Sunder—was lecturing her about an old tale of a monk who rescued a kidnapped noble. Veil wasn’t paying much attention, seeing little meaning in the story, until Stone stopped and explained the point: there had to be a reason the rescuers didn’t assume the noble was already dead—someone valued him, and that value mattered. As he spoke, Stone drew a circle in the sand, then cut it short with a single stroke that formed a simple “G,” leaving Veil to ponder the implication. Veil realized he was trying to teach her something about her brother Gideon, and this became the moment she began shifting from being only a fighter to someone who also sought answers, patterns, and truth.

With the flashbacks concluded, we returned to our heroes in the Beholder’s lair. Arden was barely standing, restored from death with only one hit point and heavy penalties to all his rolls. The party quickly realized they needed to regroup and get him to safety. As they prepared to leave, they spotted a rope ladder descending from above. At the top waited Jerusha, who had come with several kobold escorts to mourn what she assumed were the deaths of their party. Her contract apparently contained a clause allowing her to grieve, and she had fully expected Arden to be dead. Instead, she found him alive, though in rough condition.

Jerusha alerted them that Officer Oswald had arranged some new method or machine to bypass Nanny Pu’Pu’s wards and that the attempt would happen that night. If the party had anything left to resolve on the dragon side of events, they had only the day to do it. She offered to escort Arden back to the Silverweave camp, which the party agreed to, and the kobolds guided them toward their Tyrant-Scaled Queen. Along the way they passed through the chamber where the dragon’s skeleton still lay, killed long ago by the Beholder. There they encountered two newcomers — Leon Empyrean, the Justicar whose blade until recently bore Quincrux’s sentence, and Freddie (Wilfredo), a cleric and Arden’s estranged brother. Freddie and Arden exchanged a stiff but sincere greeting, with Freddie learning quickly that Arden had actually died and returned.

Before anyone could get further acquainted, the group was ambushed by a pack of Slaads. The fight was dangerous, but Leon immediately proved his strength, landing two critical hits in his first two attacks and dealing massive damage. Freddie followed with a well-placed blessed strike, and Leon reinforced the party’s advantage with a successful *Command*. Ty was stunned in the opening round, but Thorn and Veil intervened and pulled him out of danger long enough for him to contribute heavily once he recovered. What could have been a brutal fight was controlled early through smart decisions and strong rolls.

Once the battle ended, the kobolds tended to the room and the heroes finally had a moment to address one another. Leon cast a *Zone of Truth* and issued the command that had been burning inside him since his arrival: **“Where is Professor Galton Quincrux?”**

It was an intense moment, but Veil answered by pulling out Quin’s ashes. The reaction that followed was chaotic. Leon immediately unraveled, realizing that Quin’s death — and the way his sword’s runes had gone dark — made no sense. Even ashes should have satisfied the Aletheia Procedure, yet his blade had extinguished Quin’s name without a collection. According to everything Leon had ever sworn, this was impossible.

He warned the party that they were all now implicated in something very serious. If the Procedure has contradicted itself, other Justicars will come, and they will assume the worst. Unless the heroes can uncover what went wrong, they will be hunted as if they are part of a major conspiracy.

This is where we join our heroes now, standing in the aftermath of the Beholder’s lair with new allies, new dangers, and a mystery that threatens every oathbound Justicar in the world.
