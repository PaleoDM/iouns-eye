We opened as our heroes finished their audience with Duchess Lyla. Before they departed, she asked to speak with Vicas alone. The specifics of that conversation didn't make it to our heroes, but they are logged for the GM:

> She pitched a daring plan to Vicas that involves her faking her own death and having Vicas, as a secular doctor, pronounce her dead, rather than the priests. she wants to do this to: (1) Flush out her enemies into acting prematurely (2) prove to her nobles that he sons will rally and unite instead of squabble (3) prove to the nobles that Lady Rowena comes in good faith and not to seize power. Vicas agreed.

When Vicas returned, the Duchess appeared composed, already healthier from the restoration, and the group moved on to their next task. From there, the party was presented with several immediate priorities: (1) investigate the collapse at the Goldwarren; (2) question the assassins’ trail at the Anis Academy; (3) accompany the princes to the Mushin Majilis to sway key nobles; (4) or interrogate the stablemaster—whose involvement in the assassination attempt seemed increasingly likely. They chose the latter.

The stablemaster made no attempt to flee. In fact, he was holed up in his quarters with a crossbow, believing he was already doomed. Convinced the princes had died in the garden attack, he assumed he would be hanged for treason. As the party questioned him, the truth emerged: he had purchased decorative topiaries from a new vendor in town, believing they contained festival fireworks. Instead, they housed the elemental used for the assassination attempt. He had been manipulated, but was seemingly not complicit.

A magical investigation revealed traces of altered memory—perhaps even a geas—clouding his recollection. The heroes ensured he was moved not to a jail cell, but to a protected location where the guards could keep him safe from further tampering.

Meanwhile, Thump gained an audience with Old King One-Eye, the venerable tressym who ruled the flying cats of the palace gardens. After proper deference and a formal exchange of respect, One-Eye confirmed he could retrace the steps of the cloaked figures who had met with the stablemaster. He also noted their unmistakable scent: sulfur. The heroes now had a direction to pursue whenever they wished it.

With that lead secured, the party traveled to the Goldwarren. Lady Rowena and the princes split off to attend the Majilis and rally political allies, leaving our heroes to investigate the mines directly. There they met **Foreman Uzoma**, who explained the situation: the mine had suffered one major collapse and three smaller ones. Only a single miner had been rescued so far, and he claimed to have seen a “golden, scaled creature with enormous claws” before the cave-in. They had been taken to the Anis Academy to recover for their wounds under the watch of the local clerics. The remaining trapped miners could not be reached for at least a day; the crews were focused on air tunnels and shoring up the unstable walls.

More troubling still, a massive keg of mercury had vanished in the aftermath. Mercury was essential for extracting gold from ore—and, as the party already suspected, it could also serve as the source of the heavy-metal poisoning used against the Duchess.

As they surveyed the scene, Lyra noticed one of the mine’s aides—a nervous accountant named **Vero**—clutching a ledger a bit too tightly. Working together, Lyra created a distraction while Azrael quietly snatched the book. Inside, they found records indicating that small amounts of mercury had been disappearing steadily for weeks, funneled toward a mysterious “benefactor.” Vero, relieved to finally speak openly, confirmed that the description matched someone within the clergy of one of the three temples, but couldn't tell which. Lyra could, however, from the description—it was unmistakably the temple of the Dawnfather.

But one question remained: if someone was already stealing mercury with impunity, why sabotage the tunnels and risk everything?

Before Vero could clarify further, elemental creatures erupted from the debris and attacked. Their target was obvious—they were trying to silence Vero and steal the ledger. The battle was fast and violent. Lyra shielded Vero long enough to keep him alive, while Azrael secured the ledger before an elemental could escape with it. Working together, the heroes destroyed the attackers and prevented the cover-up.

We ended with the foreman asking the party to enter the tunnels and help clear them of danger, but not until the following day, once her crews had reinforced the walls enough to make travel safe. Until then, the heroes have a brief window to rest—or return to the palace, the Anis Academy, or the Mushin Majilis to continue unraveling the threads of conspiracy now tightening around Ember’spost.
