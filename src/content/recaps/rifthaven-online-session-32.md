We rejoined our heroes bloodied and desperate, locked in a deadly battle with Ozythar the Tyrant and the Froghemoth that had survived its fall into the lair. They had fought their way to this point through grit and luck, but as the session opened, that luck turned sharply against them. Ozythar chose his eye beams with terrible precision. Arden was struck with a Death Ray, and even with his DM Inspiration, failed his saving throw. He fell instantly, his soul slipping from his body before anyone could intervene. Ozythar then swung the Disintegration Ray toward Quin who, at his remaining hit points, could not survive the blast. Pass or fail, the spell reduced him to dust. In the span of a single round, two of our heroes were gone.

We lingered on each of them as their final moments played out. Arden found a poignant peace in having died for something worthy—a death fit for song. Quin, in his own strange way, embraced the wild and statistical absurdity of being atomized on the spot. And then there were three left to face the Tyrant.

The battle raged on. Thorn pushed forward with reckless, heavy swings, absorbing punishment and dealing back even more. Veil pressed in with grapples and stunning strikes, forcing out the last of the Beholder’s four legendary resistances and creating crucial openings. Ty wrestled with the Froghemoth, narrowly escaping its grasp and finding room to breathe after a tense moment when he nearly vanished into its gullet. As the battle raged on, the beholder turned its disintegration ray right onto the Ty, and discovered that some part of Quin had still portended this moment, letting Ty pass the save and not be disintegrated.

As the melee spread across the jagged islands of the lair, the party slowly—painfully—broke the last of Ozythar’s defenses. Quin’s earlier barrage combined with the relentless pressure from Veil and Thorn had taken their toll. With the Beholder now badly wounded and flailing for survival, Ty rushed in and landed a devastating smite that at last brought Ozythar down.

The tremors of the lair’s regional magic faded instantly, leaving one final threat. The Froghemoth, still healthy after its own short rest, proved stubborn and dangerous in its final minutes. Thorn came close to being swallowed himself, but the attack missed, and Veil ended the fight with a single, perfectly placed strike that burst the creature apart from the inside out. The battle was finished—but only three heroes remained standing.

The party withdrew deeper into the lair where they found Ozythar’s hoard and, for the moment, safety. They unearthed an enormous sum of gold—some 26,000 pieces—along with several magical items: a rune of stealth, a rune of glamour, a double-bladed glaive-halberd perfectly suited for Thorn, and one Ring of Blurr. The victory was hard won. Revivify was gone. Quin’s dust was all that remained. And Arden lay still and cooling on the stone.

While the players made decisions out of character about whether their heroes wished to return, Ty wrestled in-character with the same questions. Quin’s fate, he knew, was grim—without a body, only powerful ritual magic or a dangerous bargain could hope to bring him back. Arden, however, was within reach. Ty searched himself for the newfound power he felt rising inside. During the long rest, he meditated on it, searching for connection, and found it.

Through *Contact Other Plane*, Ty communeed with his patron—the Moonweaver—and glimpsed the lives he had lived across ages alongside Lulu: liberators, wanderers, tricksters, revolutionaries. He saw Zaire, his first incarnation, leading the enslaved drow across the caverns of Benzavar and establishing the Free Society beneath the fourth moon, Selunalite, on the distant shores of what would eventually be **Tyr'Zareth**. In these visions, Ty finally understood the nature of his lineage and the truth of the Moonweaver’s care for her children above and below the surface.

He asked if Arden’s soul still lingered; the goddess confirmed it would return if called. He asked for the power to cast *Raise Dead*, and, on a persuasive plea, it was granted. He asked after Veil’s lost brother, learning that the boy—Gideon—had been taken to Quinn’s academy, grown into a diviner apprenticing directly to Quin himself, and eventually defected to study necromancy under Quinn’s rival. With multiple archmages from the school of necromancy now dead, Gideon had been elevated to headmaster as a political puppet. Finally, Ty asked whether Nanny Pu’Pu’s wards could be restored; the Moonweaver explained they required a coven of three to fully anchor them.

With this knowledge, Ty prepared Arden’s body. Veil aided the ritual with a rare bit of elemental magic, and Thorn lent nature and medicine to the effort. Their combined work lowered the DC to a gentle six. Arden rolled a seventeen and returned to the world—alive, but deeply exhausted and carrying the penalties of resurrection. His first question was for Quin, and the answer broke what remained of the party’s spirits.

Quin’s dust had been gathered, though perhaps not enough to qualify as a “meal” for the fox-bats, nor enough to easily anchor a soul. Veil briefly considered the creatures’ appetite and how Quinn’s remains might factor into a bargain, but the thought was left for later.

We join our heroes now at level nine, newly rested and nursing their grief, with Arden carried along in a weakened state and two new companions to be found soon. The lair of the Beholder lies behind them, but the road ahead promises no less danger. Next week, they face the need to locate a potential remaining dragon egg and tangle with Officer Oswald and the Silverweave Ventures crew before they can steal this ancient tree of dreams.
