We opened with our heroes making the acquaintance of **Amaq**, the locathah diplomat and his pet sea lion. Amaq had arrived several weeks early for a conclave of underwater peoples, hosted by the halflings of Ahanabai. He'd traveled from a deep-water community off the coast of the Windcutter Isthmus, and carried grim news: massive numbers of the dead now walked the ocean floor. The conclave itself was a remarkable affair — a gathering of peoples who are more often at each other's throats than seated at the same table. Sahuagin, merrow, merfolk, sea elves, triton, locathah, even the occasional bullywug delegation. The halflings of Ahanabai serve as neutral ground and facilitate the conversation, a role they've held for some time. Rowena and Rooker suggested that, though the surface folk be not members, our heroes might have pertinent information worth sharing. Amaq agreed — he could extend an invitation for them to testify as key witnesses to everything that transpired on Firewatch Island.

From there, we flashed through the many changes unfolding across Ishetar. The vegepygmies, once strange newcomers, have been weaving themselves into the fabric of daily life. Children played alongside them in the streets while townsfolk learned Vegepygmy and the little creatures picked up Common in return. Vicas caught up with Hrothgar, who explained that Ishetar had swollen to capacity. Talk had already turned to expanding the walls once again, though the old quarry was running dry — they'd either need a new local stone supply or make the shift to lumber. The conversation turned to the Turquoise Timber Trust and the danger it posed to Rowena. Hrothgar, never one for half measures, insisted he'd round up a proper militia for the barracks. And as part of that effort, our heroes caught a glimpse of **Jora** absolutely running the show as quartermaster for the armory and weapons supply — a young woman in her element, and then some.

Then we slowed down for a series of character vignettes, each one a quiet window into how our heroes have grown alongside this town.

Azrael stood back and marveled at what Ishetar had become. The community that had sprouted up around them — people joyfully pitching in together, preparing for a harsh winter not with dread but with shared purpose. For someone who had spent so long on the outside looking in, it clearly meant something. And even as Azrael back to enjoy it, the swelling of Ishetar's population loomed large, leading him to wonder how long the mood could be sustained.

Rooker wrestled with his new reality. The eldritch parasite was gone, but the arcane power remained — and the uncomfortable implication that it may have always been his. He spent a long night drinking with Thump, turning it over and over. Thump, emphatically and without hesitation, told him: "You weren't an asshole. You were walking the wrong path." And when Rooker pushed back, Thump chided him gently: "Would I follow alongside an asshole?"

Lyra stepped back into the community duties and quiet acts of service that have made her so beloved in town. She resumed her role as Ishetar's lamplighter, tending to hearths and keeping everyone warm through the long winter months — the kind of steady, reliable devotion and service that defines her.

Thump set about winterizing the town, sowing bulbs that would sprout come spring. Young **Mikkel** worked at his side, learning the rhythms of the land from a druid who speaks its language fluently. To help break up the frost, for our gardeners were late to the sowing season, Mikkel used his first, burgeoning elemental magic.

And finally, Vicas. Quiet after a few days of reflection, but at long last ready to share his story with his friends.

He had lived in a small fishing community, married to a woman named **Shallah** — who was, in her own right, a fantastic adventurer. Vicas stayed home, treating mundane injuries and wounds, while Shallah went out saving the world and being larger than life. It was a good arrangement. It worked.

Then a priest of the Dawnfather named **Kato** came to town, and slowly brought more and more outsiders with him until they had steadily outnumbered the original inhabitants. Kato preached his own strange brand of the Dawnfather's faith, heavy-handed and unyielding, and Vicas didn't much care for it. But he kept his head down.

Then Shallah returned. Vicas knew she'd be able to set things right — and he was eager to hear all her latest stories. But she was unwell. A sickness of some kind, one that ate at her and weakened her day by day. Vicas tried everything. Kato and his priests tried everything. And when she took a turn for the worse, and then another, and another, Vicas made her a cup of tea, added some of his own toxins, and let her rest. Finally.

In the morning, Kato was there. He had found out, somehow. He accused Vicas of denying Shallah the Dawnfather's plan — of condemning her to an eternity where her soul could not be claimed. Vicas was tried, found guilty of murder, and run out of town. That's how he came to Ishetar.

And once here, he made a quiet living healing cuts and scrapes, until Meris showed up at the gate and Vicas thought to himself: what a fantastic opportunity to go out with a hero's death. Only they didn't die. And they kept not dying. And when Vicas lied to Kareth Varn in the Red Vale and it *worked* — when it actually brought the man peace — Vicas started to question whether maybe the story wasn't over yet.

The banshee had shown him Shallah: happy, seated in a great hall of camaraderie among warriors like her, found worthy and welcomed with open arms. As Vicas put it, "that was the best news he could have gotten." And now he had to figure out how to move on for himself.

The table went quiet for a moment. Then, one by one, our heroes answered.

Azrael told him he loved him, and implored him to "stick around until the wheels fall off." Lyra and Thump each offered comfort in their own way — both affirming that Vicas had done the right thing, that nothing he had done could touch Shallah's soul or the peace she had found. Lyra insisted, with the quiet steel she reserves for matters of faith, that she would *handle* the problematic Kato. And Thump, gently, assured Vicas that the banshee had escorted Shallah because she had been found worthy. That was all anyone needed to know.

Before settling in, our heroes tied up a few loose ends. **Captain Alba** returned to Ishetar with the two Tritons and Vero in tow. Alba brought news that her ship was too fast to be seriously threatened, but that vessels from the Turquoise Timber Trust had moved to harass and board her. She expected all trade between Ishetar and Emberspost to be similarly harassed so long as the logging fortress remains in place.

Vero, meanwhile, pulled Lyra aside and delivered a gift — partially from them and partially from Deft. Deft had knocked a long rod off the personal effects of Janore: the shaft of a mace, its head broken in the battle. Deft had "done that thing Deft does," and then told Vero to deliver it to Lyra. Vero had fashioned a new head for the morningstar: a radiant sun. When the enchantment was complete, Lyra had a custom weapon built to deliver the Dawnfather's justice. Vero chided that it was meant to "help the boss stay on the straight and narrow."

We also caught a scene of Azrael and **Th0l** enjoying several mugs of ale while Azrael regaled him with every story from their adventure. The two chided each other back and forth over their respective misadventures. When Azrael asked what Th0l made of all the conflict, the serious young man admitted he knew little of the politics in these lands — this was the furthest west he'd ever been. But he had passed through Qonya, whose people are strange and reclusive, and had heard rumors of them seeking to reclaim ancestral lands beneath the Sundered Diadems. Th0l wondered if the Myreshade might not prove a bountiful trade route, should Ishetar manage to establish connections on its eastern side. The scene ended quickly from there, as the flirting reached peak.

Then we settled in for bastions.

Vicas kept busy. He recruited new members to the town guard through his barracks, used his storehouse to flip some gold for the party, made and sold vials of alchemist's fire, and trained in his new arena to improve his unarmed strikes.

Azrael ensured the armory and barracks ran smoothly, keeping the guardsmen well armed. He also dispatched Captain Alba on a mission: take a group of Ishtari volunteers willing to overwinter on Firewatch Island back down south, and on the way, detour toward the river valleys of the Aroiin Empire to check on rumors of refugees fleeing the fighting. Then, one morning over breakfast, Azrael was visited by two magic fireflies that promptly began transforming into floppy-eared, long-tongued, canine-like creatures.

A small man — about eighteen inches tall — zoomed in at lightning speed, apologizing profusely for the dogs getting ahead of him. He introduced himself as **Swift Quick, Fleet of Foot and Flat of Face**, the latter apparently being a source of self-consciousness for the lad. He explained that the hounds were a gift to Azrael from his master, **Lord Crescendo of the Thornsong**, for having rescued two of his most trusted agents: Minor Chord and Major Key. Azrael pointedly asked if the gift was freely given. "No expectations were explicitly placed," Swift Quick answered, "though no clarification either." Azrael understood this to mean a favor may be asked sometime in the future, but accepted the gift graciously and extended his friendship in return. Some discussion followed about Lord Crescendo eventually inviting them to his court — and the expectation that such an invitation not be turned down. Then, as quickly as he'd come, the little creature was gone, leaving Rooker and Vicas's shoelaces tied together and a sire and dame for a new line of blink dogs in Ishetar.

Lyra trained with the maul to sharpen her martial prowess, harvested a healing potion from her garden, and received a supernatural charm from her sanctuary. She also crafted a *Nature's Mantle* for Thump, who was thrilled to learn he could now hide as a bonus action.

Thump brewed numerous potions from his greenhouse and grew a staggering eight hundred rations of food for the community. He received his own supernatural charm as well. We also saw Thump step more thoroughly into his role with the Knights of the Solstice, and learned that Amaq is himself a ranger of the undersea equivalent: the Warriors of Weft and Cross. The two spent a long evening discussing the differences between arboreal and kelp forests.

Rooker executed a great deal of research in his library, regained his *Charm of Identify*, and set his hirelings to scribing books in the scriptorium. Then he brought in the school teacher — a dark-haired, dark-skinned halfling with a round face and round glasses named **Babs** — and insisted that she "move the schoolhouse in here" to his library and "set the children about learning all they'll need to be proper citizens in the world." Babs, Vero, and Sombra all remarked on this new Rooker, seemingly determined to do good.

As Rooker was finalizing his last bits of business, a red dragonborn wizard teleported through his teleportation circle. The young man, **Servatti**, was surprised to find no guards, and blustered out that he "didn't know the proper protocol for arriving at an out-of-network teleportation circle." When Rooker mentioned Sombra, Servatti perked up immediately — asking if she was "the brilliant mind behind the groundbreaking thesis on ritualistic combination of mixed-media magics to surgically repair the soul of a celestial Couatl." Sombra sheepishly admitted that, because Rooker was still an asshole back then, she had flipped their names in the authorship order. Rooker shook his head but took it in stride.

And finally, we were treated to a lovely scene of Azrael attempting to train his new blink dogs — one of whom insisted on pranking him by blinking off the cliff's edge and back again before Azrael could do a thing about it. The scene devolved into joyful play when Azrael unfurled his wings and suddenly they were romping through the air in a great game, chasing higher and higher along the Z-axis as the jubilant creature blinked out of his arms and reappeared just out of reach.

We join our heroes in Ishetar on the 8th of Bahamut, as they conspire with Rowena and Hrothgar over how to handle the situation at the Turquoise Timber Trust.
