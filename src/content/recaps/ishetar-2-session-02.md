We rejoined our heroes finishing up at the Dirty Gerty tavern, making their final preparations to go look for Dalric and Bran Caldrow, and the young children Jora and Mickell. As they finalized these preparations, it became clear that there was a choice of route to make:

> Travel along the cliffs, which were sure to be direct and fast, with no chance of getting lost, but full of terrible danger.
>
> Or
>
> Travel along the old hunting and trapping paths, which would leave somepossibility of getting lost and would add to their travel time, but would theoretically face less danger.

In the end, they chose the hunting paths, in part because they feared that their mounts wouldn't make the journey cliff side (spoiler alert, they were right). Thump transformed into a majestic, roan-colored elk, and the rest took the ponies, and off they went.

It was not too long before they found themselves coming upon a set of Ogres, of which they'd heard rumors. The ogre called out, insisting on paying a toll. When they asked how much, it stated simply "two". Rooker thought this entirely reasonable, but it soon became apparent that they meant "two people". Azrael tried to intimidate the monsters with a drawing on their ferocity, but the ogre was flattered by the image and insisted that Azrael now belonged to him and kept calling Azrael "his new son".

Predictably, violence broke out, and it proved quite dangerous. In the fray, Rooker placed down a crucial Darkness spell that allowed several of them to hide from the worst of the attacks. Azrael and Vicas did their best to front line, ducking and dodging around most of the attacks. Thump and Lyra provided critical support from the back line: Lyra trying to ration few spell slots while Thump put one of them to sleep.

Things turned quickly when the sleeping ogre awoke, and another landed a massive crit on Azrael that missed killing them by a single hit point. That spurred our heroes to decisive action—Lyra brought Azrael back from the brink with a healing word and together they laid waste to the last remaining ogre.

As they rested, our heroes settled in for a chat. Lyra invoked a prayer of healing, allowing them all to take a short rest in only ten minutes time, and restored a fair number of hit points. Vicas administered to his allies with healing hands, while the rest investigated the ogres, who had unusual branding burned into their skin in the form of celestial and infernal runes. Our heroes didn't know what to make of it, but knew well enough to know it wasn't done by a physical brand, but by magic. They were disturbed.

When they set off once more, Thump led them finally off the path and towards the shores. As they strayed from the path, Thump found a small roadside offering marker—simple little statues where travelers make offerings for the road. Every culture differs slightly, but typically folks leave small tokens or burn a candle or make an offering for save travels. Thump chided Rooker for typically pilfering whatever coins have been left, and druidcraft a lovely flower into it as an offering.

This triggered a magical communication with the Oracle of the shrine, who implored our heroes that time was short. Her wards were failing, and they would leak wild divinity into the world around Ishetar. She could help them prepare, but only if they hurried, for Vessan had nearly drained her of life and the civilians would be next. In thanks, Lyra cast light upon the vessel, and was rewarded with Quetzi's boon—a one time use of the Bless spell, cast at 2nd level, as a bonus action.

Now our heroes made double time, and soon arrived at the shrine itself. Old and forgotten, but the stone still remembered their old days. As they approached, they found four civilians (2 adult men and 2 adolescents) tied around a stone dais bearing a beaten and unconscious draconic creature. Before they could approach, Vessan made their appearance.

A tall, slender and gaunt androgynous figure came into view, with skin the color of blue-purple slate and eyes that glowed of pure light. They despaired at first, to learn that it was a group of adventurers who had come, and not the infant Ellie. They despaired more still to learn that this was why their "children" had not returned with the infant.

Our heroes set about trying to create an ambush for Azrael. Rooker got Vessan talking about their plan, and it soon became clear that they felt themselves a servant of the gods who had faithfully waited all this time for their return. They thought the child a sign that the gods were returning to restart the crusade, and they aimed to "fill the child with divinity" and for her to "rupture the divine energy across the land". Rooker asked what "rupture" meant and was assured it would be "wet".

Meanwhile, Lyra had deduced that this was a twisted and sick creature—they were never mortal to begin with and centuries abandoned to this land had twisted them to something most wretched and foul. Lyra introduced Azrael, who suddenly garnered all of Vessan's attention as a potential new vessel. Meanwhile, Lyra and Vicas unbound the civilians and primed them to flee upon a signal. And then, Azrael struck a surprise blow.

Vessan wept, globs of bight melancholy as the psychic blade pierced their body. And then, they exploded into divine light, reconstituting as a magnificent giant elk and 4 sparks of divinity. Combat broke out, with Lyra taking a mighty wound from the elk after dealing some damage of her own. Then Vicas closed ranks and decimated what remained of the elk. The wisps of divinity however, set about with healing spells, and soon the battle was afoot. Lyra used their channel divinity radiant blast, and the elk fell once more. Then everyone else went to work on the wisps. Azrael landed a massive crit, Rooker a max damage eldritch blast, and Thump landed a crit of his own before Vicas cleaned up the rest. In the end, Lyra convened on the final wisp with her mace, dealing the exact damage necessary to kill it.

We join our heroes in the late afternoon on the 10th of Obitris, having rescued the civilians but still needing to deal with a dying oracle and, ideally, make their way home before nightfall. And what of Vessan, who seems to have been felled so easily? Perhaps...too easily for one so long-lived and determined?
