We opened with our heroes at the base of the Great Pike, where the impaled form of **Kareth Varn**, the Warden Unyielding, still hung from a field of shattered cavalry lances. They attempted to speak with him, and quickly realized that he fluctuated between moments of lucid clarity and frenzied madness. Vicas climbed the spears to reach him while Azrael tested his mental state from below. Kareth explained that death eluded him—each dawn, his body regenerated atop the pikes, only to be torn apart again by wind, weather, and carrion. His suffering was perpetual, sustained not by life, but by unbroken oath.

Meanwhile, Lyra studied the nearby slate monolith. It contained two inscriptions. The first, etched in Celestial with a flawless infernal burn, had been written by **Zariel**, Archduchess of Avernus. It declared that the Red Vale had been lawfully ceded to the devils during the Myreshade Armistice. However, one paladin—Kareth Varn—had refused the divine order to retreat, claiming instead that his oath to the people of the Vale superseded any celestial edict. The devils destroyed his company, but could not kill him. And so Zariel, unwilling to let a legal loose end linger, impaled him as both tribute and warning. She left the body as proof of infernal victory—and the spear, helm, and armor Kareth lost across three separate battles were each named within the text.

> *(first half written in celestial, in perfectly flowing cursive. Despite being set in stone, the script looks as though written with a quill, and bears a slight char around the edges, as though burned in)*
>
> ***Edict of Infernal Record***
>
> I, Lady Zariel, Archduchess of Avernus, Lord of the First, and Bringer of Eternal Pain, file this decree in accordance with the Chainbound Compact.
>
> Let this serve as cosmic record. Under the terms of the Myreshade Armistice, the region known then as the Cliffside Vale was formally cedes by representatives of the Dawnfather and the Lawbearer to the Infernal Legion, and was therefore lawfully obtained as territory of the Hells. As acting emissary of the Supreme Master and Lord of Lies, I came to claim the Vale, but encountered great resistance in the form of one Kareth Varn. He was known to the locals as Oathwarden, for he was the last remaining paladin of the Lawbearer in Ec'Kor'Ai, but we would come to call him Warden Unyielding. The Warden Unyelding disobeyed a call to retreat from the Lawbearer herself, citing an unbreakable oath to defend the folk of the Vale until the end. We did not know it then, but this man's conviction bent even divine will. 
>
> We did now show mercy, but the Warden Unyielding and his platoon never wavered. At the Breached Hollow we broke their defenses, sent the local folk scattering, and wrested from the Warden his mystical plate. At the Helm Faced Pool we ambushed the refugees and dealt the Warden a great blow, parting him of his magical helmet. And at the Braided Stream we broke the last of their lines, causing the Warden to finally dropped his holy spear that had cursed so many of our kin.
>
> And so the Warden made his final stand on Pike Field, where every soldier fought to the last, with nary an oath broken. And in this, we found that the land observed the oath just as much as the Warden Unyielding—for no aspect of magic or steel, divinity or hellfire, seemed to bring the Warden to a final death. After much deliberation, I staked the Warden upon his pike, like a great banner to signal to celestial and infernal forces alike. For in this, our enemies and I agree—the world would be a far better place if all mortals shared the Warden's conviction of character.
>
> Let this be acknowledged by this inscription: the Infernal Legions make full legal claim to the Red Vale. Yet we record, with great admiration untainted by mercy, the oaths of Kareth Varn, Warden Unyielding. His vowcraft is so strong, I suspect his oaths cannot be broken at all, merely assumed.
>
> —Zariel, Archduchess of Avernus, Lord of the First, Bringer of Eternal Pain

The second inscription, chiseled shakily in Sylvan, had clearly been written by the vegepygmies. It warned of the **Great Skyrock**, harbinger of eldritch rot. The inscription described their ancient exodus from a world consumed by flame and worms. Their gods, it said, had led them here upon the skyrock, where they met the Fae—whom the vegepygmies mistook for divine beings. The Fae taught them how to survive in this land, including the ritual cycle of meat and sporing during winter. But now the Fae were gone, and the vegepygmy elders left this warning behind: if a new skyrock appears in the sky, the worms have found them again.

> *(second half written in sylvan, and clearly stamped or chiseled in by uncertain hands unfamiliar with the tools. The sylvan, too, is simple in construction, but elegant in prose. As though written by beings of intelligence, but in a fledgling language)*
>
> ***Beware the Great Skyrock, Harbinger of Eldritch Rot***
>
> The Great Skyrock brought us here. The earliest writings from our first elders tell us of this place. They say that our old gods came here, in the beginning, upon a Skyrock of their own, to subdue a great eldritch evil. But even the eldest among us do not now remember if these are fact or fancy. But our world dies, chocked with ash and exposed to the glare of the suns. And always, the worms and their maggots look for even more to consume.
>
> And so we came, by Great Skyrock, following our gods. And met them, upon arrival! Beautiful, tall creatures with smiles that extend from long ear to long ear. They were fair creatures with gentle faces and slender limbs. And they taught us the language of this world, which sings and chimes like pleasant bells.
>
> Without them, we would have died quickly in those early years. But they taught us the ways of this land. They taught us to hunt mix meat with oxygen to trigger our sporing cycles in the weeks before what we would learn to call Winter. And they taught us to avoid this monolith and it's impaled creature at all costs, for it is a cursed place they said.
>
> But now that they have left us, we feel compelled to do as the ancients once did, and record our dire warning upon the rock face. For our youth must know to look ever to the sky and be vigilant, always for a Great Skyrock. For the worms and their maggots will pursue us, even to hear. Their arrival is as inevitable as the return of our fair gods.

Our party, with new purpose, resolved to gather Kareth’s three lost relics. Whether to end his suffering, undo the binding magic spreading across the Vale, or prepare the land for a new age of cooperation, they weren't yet sure—but they knew it must be done.

Their first stop was the **Braided Spear**, site of one of the final battles of Kareth’s campaign. Azrael scouted ahead, but triggered the old defenses. The oathbound dead rose: ghasts and ghouls still fighting for the Warden’s cause. At the center of it all stood a long-dead lieutenant in tattered but regal uniform, clutching Kareth’s lost spear.

Azrael charged forward and struck the skeleton to take the weapon—but the corpse ignited with flame and struck back. The battle that followed was harsh. A fireball blasted the field. Paralytic bites dropped Azrael, Thump, and Vicas. Lyra was left alone with Rooker. But with resolve and radiant power, she kept them alive while Rooker blasted the flaming skeleton. Vicas and Thump rallied. Rooker dropped the lieutenant with an Eldritch Blast, and Lyra poured holy water over its remains to halt its regeneration.

Yet Lyra knew that wasn't enough. The lieutenant’s spirit remained, but funery rights are not the domain of the Dawnfather typically. She turned to Vicas, whose voice had cut through the chaos before. And he answered the call. Vicas delivered a eulogy—not for a warrior’s death, but for a life well lived. He asked Lyra: "*Does the Dawnfather care more for how someone died, or how they lived?"* Lyra answered firmly: *"It is their life that matters."*

As the ritual ended, all five heroes were hit with a vision. It seemed at first to show that final battle—but Vicas’s words redirected it. Instead of carnage, they saw family and friends, harvests and funerals, weddings and births and funerals. They saw a life well lived. A peaceful, ordinary life, lived well. With that, the lieutenant’s soul found rest. The **Verity’s Edge**, Kareth’s spear, materialized in their hands. The dead no longer needed to guard it

That night, the group made camp and rested. Tensions flared as Rooker and Azrael clashed over Azrael’s habit of rushing in before danger is assessed. It wasn’t pretty—but it might mean Rooker *cares*. Lyra and Vicas spoke about the Dawnfather—and the pain Vicas carried from priests of that same faith. Lyra made clear: her path is not theirs. And Thump shared a quiet moment with Azrael about the moon, about power, about peace.

We ended on Thump kneeling in the dirt, planting **Warrior’s Plume**, a red and white flower of deep mythos—marking the lieutenant’s grave as one of a particularly loyal and courageous soldier. The site is no longer haunted, but **consecrated**.

Our heroes now rest, and grow to level five. Verity’s Edge has been claimed. Two relics remain. And the Warden Unyielding waits.
