We joined our heroes on the 17th of Melora as they finalized preparations with Tota and the De la Alma family, ready to strike against the Sergente plantation. Teaming up with Luz, they first set out to secure the Dendritium Augers—massive farming engines capable of tearing through walls. Luz outfitted the party with Feldunost goats as mounts, and they rode north to the Auger camp.

Before leaving, Quin cast *Divination*, uncovering a ciphered communication system—like Morse code or Chinese Telegraph Code—broadcast at high frequency across the region. From this, he learned that Romero De la Alma had built a failsafe into the Arcane Forge, giving the party the means to destroy it if they reached it.

At the Auger camp, the party went in hot. The defenders—a mage, their pet giant scorpion, two heavies, and two archers—put up strong resistance. The fight turned dangerous as the mage landed several key spells. Arden was nearly dropped, clinging to life at two hit points, while Thorn and Veil cut down the mage. Quin bounced *Chromatic Orb* across multiple targets, and Ty’s *Eldritch Blast* added much-needed pressure. In the end, the party prevailed, securing the Augers. Luz and her rancher allies quickly familiarized themselves with the controls while the party rested, and then all returned to Tota to set the full plan in motion.

That night, the strike began. Luz and her crew charged the plantation walls with the Augers, drawing the defenders into a desperate fight. From Tota, the party learned the layout: the plantation manor, a secondary building housing the Arcane Forge, and two barracks full of guards and Ledgermen. Unsure where Romero was being kept, the party chose the Forge, reasoning it was their best chance to cripple Haji’s schemes.

As the Augers tore into the walls, the party slipped through the servants’ quarters and crossed a field of burning, spined cactus. Only Arden took damage on the crossing, and soon they were in striking distance of the Forge. Unfortunately, their luck turned. Reinforcements were already moving out from the Forge toward the walls, and the two groups collided. Veil quickly carved down the mage apprentices, Thorn locked up the enemy heavies, and Ty and Quin added support. But in the fray, Ty pushed open the Forge doors—alerting the enemies still inside and dragging them into the battle.

We left our heroes in the thick of combat at the Forge. Romero may be inside, and certainly the true plans of Haji’s machinations lie within. The question is whether the party can finish this fight quickly enough to achieve their goals—before reinforcements from the House of Coinwrights, or even Haji himself, arrive on the field.
