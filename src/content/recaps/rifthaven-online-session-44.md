We opened where we left off: the quiet back wing of the Actuarium, two imps and a High Justicar worth of ash still settling. Veil was already at work on the body, setting fire to what remained of Josephine Maren to cut off whatever resurrection insurance she'd been counting on. While she worked, Sir Sunder was reading. The ledger Josephine had been holding when the fight broke out was more than confirmation of Quin's soul. Buried in the evidence was a second name. **Divine Judge Art Doyle.** Another High Justicar, his soul held here in the Potter's Field alongside Quin's.

The stakes snapped into focus. What better evidence for the takedown of the Aletheia procedure than a high judge, himself a prisoner of it? They needed both souls out of the Galleria. They moved quietly through the marble streets to a tavern, where Little Shit pulled up a chair and the real planning began.

The answer, as Freddie saw it, was paperwork.

The plan: present as a Justicar on official business, searching for a missing person. Leon had the bearing. They had the sword. All they needed was documentation. Freddie sat down, cracked his knuckles, and rolled a natural twenty. By the time he was done, the forgery had stopped being a lie and simply become a better truth.

They arrived at the gates of the Pallbearer's estate to find the major domo waiting. Her name was Doragnes. Stern, precise, constitutionally incapable of resisting a good piece of gossip. The moment Leon explained they were searching for a missing girl, she lit up. The bad news was they'd have to split. Only three could go inside to speak with the Paintress. The rest would be escorted on a tour of the grounds.

Leon, Thorn, and Freddie went inside with Lulu. Veil, Sunder, and Ty followed Doragnes, with Little Shit scampering at their heels.

---

The Paintress was blind. She sat in a room full of portraits and seemed to feel rather than see the people who entered it. She knew things about Leon she had no business knowing: the shape of his history, the weight of his question. She pressed him, over and over, to stop trying to find the truth and simply paint it. Give up the investigation. Create the narrative. Decide what the truth would be going forward.

Leon moved toward the canvas. A brush pricked his finger. His blood moved on its own. The painting showed Leon at eight years old, battered and adrift, searching for a home after being taken by the Justicars. Not a hero. Not a Judge. A child the procedure had claimed before he had any say in the matter. The Paintress offered no explanation. She didn't need to.

---

On the tour, Veil's eyes were moving constantly. Doragnes walked them through gallery after gallery, cataloging the dead with practiced ease. Veil filed it all away and stopped cold when she turned a corner and found an empty portrait frame with a name beneath it. Her brother's name. Gideon.

The frame beside it explained it quietly: someone had paid to hold the spot. His portrait wasn't here yet, but there was room for it. Reserved. Waiting. Veil kept moving. But she'd be back for that frame before she left.

The tour revealed one more critical thing: the real collection, the private one, was in a cellar below the main galleria. That's where the serious inventory lived.

---

The groups reconvened. Sir Sunder, recognizing a cue, turned his full social attention on Doragnes and held her there. He insisted on talking about his favorite footwear.

In the true gallery below, our heroes moved fast. Quin's soul was there, held in a portrait. They lifted it cleanly. Art Doyle was there too, a former Divine Judge. They took him as well. When his gaze landed on Leon, it was not gratitude that came through. Disdain was closer.

Then security arrived.

What followed was not a prolonged affair. Veil locked onto a death lock and took it apart completely, one target, total focus. Thorn planted himself between the others and the boss and refused to move, absorbing hit after hit, a wall with opinions. Leon worked the angles around him, landing well-placed strikes with a paladin's economy. Freddie and Ty settled into a steady cantrip rhythm, damage that quietly added up. Freddie closed out two kills himself. Not just support this session.

When the last guard went down, Leon walked the remaining portraits. He wasn't stealing them. He was memorizing faces and names, souls who might still be helped once they got back to Rifthaven. There was work left for the dead, even after this.

Thorn found a rumrunner's tunnel. An escape route. One problem: Sunder was still out front with Doragnes.

Everyone else went through the tunnel. Veil went through the building at full sprint, grabbed Sunder mid-sentence, and got them both moving. Doragnes, presumably, finished her story alone.

---

We leave our heroes scattered across the white marble streets of the Potter's Field, running. They have Quin. They have Art Doyle. They have Gideon's portrait tucked under Veil's arm. The Pallbearer's people are somewhere behind them. The docks and Captain Alistair's ferry are somewhere ahead.

The plan was never to stay. They just have to get there first.
