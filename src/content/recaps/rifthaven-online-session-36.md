Balanced on a coin's edge indeed! We opened with a Mage and a Warpriest taking turns, and immediately whopping Thorn and Leon badly. Thankfully, they were still standing, leaving only Freddie down on the ground from the session before.

The top of the round brought some much needed reinforcements. Arden, literally brought from death a day before and still on the brink of exhaustion, came teleporting in to the fray, Jerusha in tow. Somehow, against all odds (thanks to a nat20 from Paul), he'd managed to keep all of the hitpoints from Freddie's 4th level Cure Wounds sessions ago. Arden promptly hit a Mass Healing Word, which brought Freddie back up!

Then it was Owald's turn. He swiftly cut through Thorn with a vicious critical hit, charming him with his blade, then cut into Leon and put him down. After stepping away from Freddie, he shot Veil with the pistol, but Veil managed to negate most of the damage. At least until his next Legendary Action when he landed another critical hit on Veil. Suddenly, she was looking as rough as everyone else.

As the rest of the battle progressed, Thorn was downed as well, but swiftly brought back up by Freddie with another Mass Healing Word. Things finally turned a corner when Ty pumped two eldritch blasts into Oswald and gave Veil an opening. A flurry of blows later, Oswald was down, and Thorn came charging back through the remaining spell casters. Suddenly, everything was under control.

As the battle ended, our heroes looked around to lick their wounds and assess the chaos of Kobolds harassing Silverweave soldiers behind them. Thorn, however, had eyes only for the apparatus. He charged the cart, ripped it open to reveal a crystal sphere with metal armatures around it. Without hesitating, Thorn sank his magic-disabling blade into it, and triggered something crazy.

Our heroes, having all passed the saving throw, found themselves in the effect of a timestop for half a minute, at the end of which a mile of anti-magic field would pop off. They took a moment to think, and promptly tossed it to Veil who used the Cape of the Montebank and he insane movement speed to rush it to the lair of the beholder. Just as the machine went off she launched it into the Beholder's lair. And good thing too, for it collapsed the hundreds of micro-portals that Ozythar had dreamed into existence.

From here, we joined our heroes in several scenes as they wrapped up much of the arc. We saw Ty work with Arden to cast Suggestion, compelling the Silverweave agents to "leave and report this tree lost ot their superiors". This effect was empowered by the Whisper-Wings into a powerful Gaes spell, magically binding these agents to execute the suggestion at all costs.

Our heroes took the time to rest, and looted a few items off of Oswald and his camp. They each were able to select one of: (1) a ring of 1/day Misty Step; (2) a ring of water breathing; (3) goggles of night. They also snagged two healing potions, a magical Flying Carpet, and Plate Armor of Etherealness.

They also took a long time to debrief with Nanny Pu'Pu. First, they discussed the eggs, the fact that they had all hatched, and and the value of the cracked shells. Old Nanny lauded Thorn for his infinite wisdom in exploiting her loophole.

Then, she kept up her end of the bargain and discussed Quin's memories. Through discussing with Nanny, our heroes learned the real events of what happened between Quin and Bilver Shadowgift. Unbeknownst to Quin, Bilver orchestrated a weaking of the glass, and goaded Quin into blasting him with the spells. Then, when Quin tried to portent a save to assist Bilver, Bilver countered with a portent of his own, ensuring his drop from atop the tower.

And so Nanny asked Leon: "who killed the mage? The one who orchestrated the death? Or the one that draws the blade?" Leon didn't have any answers, and was forced to face the potential reality that the Alethia procedure may be flawed. It was not a comforting thought.

Nanny agreed to replace the wards and help Scourge protect the tree. Scourge agreed to make this his domain, and protect the tree, with the support of Skyward Industries and Freddie to supply him with arms, ammunition, a traps expert, and a court jester. Then, Nanny walked Thorn to the gates of the garden, and introduced him to Rosie, the Dryad spirit that protects the tree. Rosie, a spirit of crystaline quartz, ushered Thorn, Veil, and Ty beyond to walk the garden, while Freddie and Leon chose to give them their space.

Here, we saw the Tree of Ambition, a short but massive tree of crystals: a quartz trunk, gypsum leaves and opal flowers, and basaltic roots down into the mountain. Thorn learned that all of the trees were cut by the original gardeners, in a time long forgotten, and that each was entrusted to a guardian like Rosie. More importantly, he learned that 2 had been compromised: his own and one other.

Rosie chided Thorn, asking what he truly wanted. Thorn insisted that he need not want anything; the seed does not have to *want* to be a tree. But Rosie pushed back: she was a spirit of the Tree to Ambition. He *must* have some ambition. "Five is too few", Thorn decided. "Seven is better." And so it was decided. And Rosie gifted Thorn with the Gift of the Lore-Axe, allowing him to speak for the trees.

We join our heroes some weeks later, having taken their wrapped things up in Boughshadow and returned to rest reflect, and improve their bastions. But Veil's naming ceremony looms on the horizon...
