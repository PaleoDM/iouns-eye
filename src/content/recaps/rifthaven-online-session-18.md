We opened with our heroes finally enjoying a bit of well-earned downtime. As the first two weeks of Melora went by, our heroes took their Bastion turns, checked in with staff, and tended to their growing district. Their rest, however, would not last long.

They received a visit from a curious guest: Sartori, the stylish RELIC they’d first met at the gala weeks prior. Sartori revealed himself as the owner and artisan behind **Sartori’s Arcane Outfitters**, a premiere high-fashion magical armory that caters to the elite of Rifthaven. And just like that, our heroes were perusing his wares.

Arden crafted himself a **Cloak of Billowing** and acquired a **Dash of Inspiration**, granting him the power to rally his allies when they falter. Thorn picked up a **Kusarigama**, a versatile chained weapon to extend his reach and versatility in combat. Veil outfitted herself with **Bracers of Harsh Rebuke**, punishing foes who fail attacks against her. Ty, meanwhile, awoke **Lulu’s Friendship Locket**, granting Lulu the ability to manifest corporeally and cast limited spells to aid her friend. And Quin invested in a **Clockwork Amulet**, had his spellbook enchanted to endure wear and tear, and picked up a few new scrolls for good measure.

Once business was settled, Sartori revealed a second purpose—a quest. He quickly got to the heart of the matter: he was seeking a rescue team.

Four other RELICS—construct folk like Sartori—had recently arrived in Rifthaven. However, they had been intercepted by agents of the **House of Coinwrights** and misclassified as non-sapient constructs. They were slated to be transported north to the **Salvation District**, where the infamous and morally dubious artificer, **Dr. Sprocket**, was believed to be performing experiments on automatons and creating twisted new forms of labor. Sartori feared his kin—**Rust, Clubs, Rider, and Spirit**—were soon to be scrapped, dissected, or worse.

The party agreed to take on the mission. As payment, Sartori offered five powerful potions (Flight, Invisibility, Invulnerability, Frost Giant Strength, and Heroism) and something even more valuable: insider access. Sartori works closely with the **House of Coinwrights**, and should the party complete this mission, he would become a covert ally within their ranks—an enormous boon for future entanglements with **Haji** and his ilk. To assist the rescue, Sartori gave the party a secret weapon: his true name—**Relic Beta07**. By invoking this name, the captive relics would know the party came in trust, not treachery.

Our heroes made their way to the Salvation District, a mountainous agricultural zone known for its barley crops, leathery cactus fruit, and animal husbandry. They crossed paths with a dwarven merchant from the Earthmovers Guild who was lobbying for a skyship landing pad to boost trade. From the merchant, the party gleaned insight into the economic tensions within Salvation—oppressed laborers, controlling landowners backed by the Coinwrights, and growing discontent.

Eventually, the party tracked their targets to the **Moving Convoy**—a massive, wheeled fortress used to haul important goods and prisoners through the district. It was still several hours from reaching its destination—a wealthy plantation home where the local landowners conduct business on behalf of the **House of Coinwrights**. Realizing this was their best shot, our heroes struck.

Thorn took point, growing large and smashing through the outer fencing. The group quickly found themselves facing a ring of crude, makeshift constructs—frightening amalgamations of scrap metal, twisted gears, and voice boxes ripped from children's toys. Though disturbing in design, the defenses were formidable. Thorn was temporarily paralyzed by a potent poison, and the constructs fought with reckless abandon.

In the end, our heroes cleaned up, though not without damage. We rejoin our heroes standing just outside the mobile fortress, unsure if the interior has been alerted—and well aware that their mission has only just begun.
