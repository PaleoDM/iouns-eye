We opened this session with Ty meditating alone at the Bastion, caught in a struggle between the two entities within his sword: the strange, mustached nautilus aberration that had long served as the voice of his warlock patron, and Lulu, the recently introduced celestial hollyphant. After a tense confrontation, Ty made a firm decision—he banished the aberrant voice from his pact and embraced the guidance and powers offered by Lulu.

Soon after, a crew of Sky Corsairs arrived at the Bastion to speak with Arden. Among them were his former crewmates from the airship *Brass Wing*: Captain Lysa Vex, the gnarled tortle Old Borb, the reserved dwarf Chimmy, and a roguish halfling named Shooka. The party quickly learned that a woman they had previously killed—Saressa Vale—had been the former owner of the *Brass Wing*. With her gone, control of the company was in dispute between her two daughters, and Captain Vex was about to lose her ship. Her only shot at reclaiming it was to qualify for and win an upcoming grand airship race. To do that, she needed help winning the Roc Run—a qualifying race known for its danger and high stakes. Vex asked Arden and the others to join her crew and help. In her view, the party owed her one for killing her old benefactor, and more practically, having an airship that owes them a favor might prove useful to the Sparkworks trade enterprise.

During this visit, Veil noticed Shooka was wearing a hairpin with a familiar symbol—a crystal with a stylized leaf, matching the one found on the crystal that caused tension between Thorn and Quin weeks earlier. This prompted a round of Bastion downtime. Veil spent her time investigating the symbol, confirming it was associated with an old group called the **Earthen Loam**—an organization of druids, rangers, and naturalists with a long but quiet presence in Rifthaven. Their crystals seemed to be a kind of scrying device, allowing the Loam to monitor certain locations. Veil couldn't determine their full intent, but they were clearly watching the vault where the crystal was found. And perhaps now...our heroes' bastion...

Elsewhere, Quin began scribing a new 1st-level spell into his book. Thorn fed the entire district with nary a word—meals just began appearing, and no one could trace them back to him, resulting in a cryptid mythos forming around Thorn. Ty’s garden produced a new healing potion, and Arden recruited four new soldiers into the barracks, building up Sparkworks’ defense network.

With their Bastion efforts completed, the party set out for **Cloudpierce Spire**, the gleaming high-district launchpoint for the Roc Run. Arden and Quin had to do a bit of fast-talking to get through security—Veil’s status as a registered public servant helped grease the wheels. Once aboard the *Brass Wing*, they reunited with Vex and learned the rules of the Roc Run. The race was simple in theory: fly from Cloudpierce to the **Southern Roc Roost**, secure an **unfertilized** roc egg, and return it to the **Harrowside Docks** within 16 hours. Taking a fertilized egg would mean disqualification and hefty fines. Shooka claimed she could tell the difference and would help identify a safe target. There were five other ships racing, and anything short of outright murder was fair game.

With roles assigned—Arden as First Mate, Thorn as Bosun, Ty as Navigator, Veil as Medic, and Quin as the ship’s Cook—the *Brass Wing* took off. Early flight checks went smoothly thanks to Ty’s navigation, and the party even witnessed one rival ship torn apart by the grasping vines of the Rustwood. Thanks to Ty’s quick decisions, they avoided that fate and made strong early progress.

Their first major threat came at **Chorus Reach**, where they were ambushed by a flock of harpies. Several party members were hit by *Synaptic Static*, and Thorn was charmed and nearly walked off the deck before Veil physically snapped him out of it. One harpy grabbed Quin and hurled him overboard, but a clutch Portent-fueled Dexterity save had him clinging to the rigging. Veil prepared to leap and grab him, only to be reminded that Quin could *Misty Step* himself back aboard. Thorn continued to hurl hatchets mid-air, Arden disrupted a harpy with a perfect *Dissonant Whispers*, and slowly the crew beat back the attackers. Ty pulled off another strong navigation check to help gain separation.

We join our heroes now soaring above the highlands at the foot of the Riftkeep Mountains, scanning the cliffs for an empty nest and a precious roc egg. With half the race still ahead, the competition behind them, and the roc mothers circling somewhere overhead, the real test is yet to come.
