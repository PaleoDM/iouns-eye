We opened with our heroes digging through the Timbermaster's office, sorting through what Tolvane had left behind and settling in for a long rest. But before sleep, there were conversations that needed having.

Azrael and Vicas found each other again, as they've taken to doing. This time, Azrael went deeper than he ever has. He told Vicas about the Order of the Celestial Seed in terms he hadn't used before. The sexual depravity. The breeding rituals. The fact that children had to die for Azrael to be conceived. It came out over two or three separate exchanges across the evening, each one heavier than the last. Azrael kept calling Vicas "pops," something he'd done before but never this often, never this naturally. Vicas listened the way Vicas does. Present, steady, and carrying it with him.

While the rest of the camp settled, Thump and Rooker decided to make the most of the remaining daylight and hiked out to the logging site. They wanted to get eyes on the mechanical construct that had been fueling the TTT's operations. When they found it, the machine was inert. Powered down. They poked around and determined it needed the blood fuel to run, roughly eight hours per charge. Rooker still had a vial of the stuff from a few sessions back, given to him by the guard post. They discussed whether to destroy the thing or somehow repurpose it for Ishetar's defense, but before they could settle on anything, both of them moved in close to investigate.

That was when the Geas hit. Neither of them knew it at the time, but both Thump and Rooker fell under a powerful compulsion. The command was simple: go smite evil. And it filled them with a strong, immediate urge to do exactly that.

Back in camp, Lyra spent the evening in conversation with the mid-level guard who had vouched for her at the gate. The picture she painted was complicated. The people here hadn't set out to be revolutionaries. Nobody wanted what she called a "terrestrial mutiny." But they'd just watched their leaders get exposed, and the political reality was sinking in fast. Some were already talking about leaving, trying to make it back to the mainland. Others felt they had no choice but to stay and build something new. Everyone was trying to figure out what this meant for them.

Then Thump and Rooker came back. Thump had wild shaped into a bear and hauled the construct on a wagon all the way to camp. Lyra took one look at them and knew something was wrong. They were acting out of turn, pushy, not themselves. Thump in particular seemed agitated, as though Lyra was standing between him and something he desperately needed to do. Rooker was trying to brush past her, but had the presence of mind to cast Detect Magic. Through it, he spotted two invisible creatures tailing them back from the logging site.

Vicas and Azrael arrived just in time. Combat broke out, and it was ugly. The two creatures were a corrupted celestial and a corrupted infernal, and they fought primarily through charm effects. Thump spent most of the fight compromised. He tried to stall at first, then resorted to healing one of the enemies rather than attacking his own friends. Rooker, still under the Geas, cast Slow on Lyra and Vicas rather than the enemies. Lyra answered with Aura of Purity, which bolstered the party's ability to shake off the charm effects and make their saves. Once Rooker broke free, then Azrael, then Thump shortly after, the tide turned fast. Our heroes put both creatures down without much trouble after that.

But the fight had an unintended side effect. Celestial blood and infernal blood had been spilled on the ground near the construct. Just enough to bring it to life for a brief moment.

The machine lurched awake and immediately started asking questions. "Am I evil?" it asked. Then it asked them, too. "Are you evil?" It wanted someone to point it toward evil, practically begging like a child looking for purpose. Thump tried a different angle: "Would you settle for doing good instead?" The construct said no. No, it would not. It needed evil. It ran out of fuel shortly after and went still again.

Lyra dealt with it practically. She pulled the injector hose that feeds fuel into the tank, disabling the thing for now. Not permanently. They left it sitting in the dirt until they could figure out what to do with it. Meanwhile, Thump was left wondering if the thing was possibly sapient, and if it might deserve another chance...?

Morning came, and with it, Castellan Drev called everyone together.

He'd spent the night on a speaking mirror with his superiors, the Guild Masters back in Aroiinae. He laid things out plainly, and partook willingly of the truth serums Thump provided to prove his honesty. First: Timbermaster Brenna Tolvane had been declared an enemy of the state. A warrant had been issued for her arrest. The guild was throwing her under the bus entirely. Second: Captain Kess had been named acting Timbermaster, and both Drev and Kess had been ordered to direct their full efforts toward assisting Sallow in the capitulation of Ishetar.

The Guild Masters had decided this was the moment to bring Ishetar to heel and pull everything back into the fold. Drev's specific task was to bring Lady Rowena back to Aroiinae as a hostage, to stand before her brother. If he failed to accomplish this by the end of the month, two weeks from now, his lands, titles, and most of his assets would be seized. The implication was clear: his family would be in danger.

What followed was a long and careful negotiation. Each of our heroes spoke to the Castellan as a noble and dignitary of Ishetar, and Drev responded in kind. He was formally requesting asylum. Could Lady Rowena grant it?

Rooker asked if he would pledge himself to Ishetar. Drev explained that he had once sworn an oath to the former Emperor Aroiinae, who was now dead. He would be willing to swear a new one to Lady Rowena, but only to her directly. Not to the party. Not to anyone else. In exchange, he asked for one thing: help extracting his family before the Guild Masters moved against them. Our heroes thought this was a fair deal.

We leave our heroes there, turning their attention north. Sallow is marching on Ishetar and they need to move fast. The means of travel is still undecided, but the urgency is not. Thankfully, Ishetar is not undefended. A former war general, a holy paladin, and several capable allies hold the walls. But our heroes know better than anyone that walls only hold as long as the people behind them do.
