We opened in the planning meeting at the TTT compound, with our heroes deciding whether to race back to Ishetar or make a detour to extract the Castellan's family first. Rooker was the holdout. He fired off a Sending to Vero to gauge how urgent things were back home, but the consensus held: they had time, and leaving Drev's family exposed wasn't something any of them could stomach.

As part of the planning, Rooker cast Identify on the teleporting scale and learned that BDZ stands for Bonqka dhiz Zoink. It seemed to be an ancient form of draconic, and the players thought Zoink might be a person? The scale teleports users to a location and offers return teleportation for the price of 75 to 150 gold. Useful information, filed away for later.

The Castellan teleported to the foothills of the Astral Peaks, arriving in the stables of Manor Harsk. The Castellan barely dodged a crossbow bolt from Udo, the ancient stablemaster and groundskeeper, who'd mistaken them for enemies. Given what had happened the night before, no one could blame him.

Lady Callista Vohn, a goliath noblewoman from the Astral Peaks, had arrived under orders from the Guildmasters. She'd brought soldiers and papers declaring a conspiracy involving Brenna Tolvane, with the Castellan's loyalty in question. But the real damage was already done. Udo's wife Bessa, the headservant, had been executed and piked in the courtyard. Two hundred and twenty-four years of service to the manor, ended overnight as a message to the household. The Castellan's wife Isla and oldest daughter Lisel had been placed under house arrest. The logic was simple: either Drev was guilty and would walk into a trap, or he was innocent and would arrive with Lady Rowena to clear his name.

Udo, seeing the Castellan without Rowena, drew his own conclusion. He was ride or die.

The problem was that the family was scattered. The middle son Haven was out grazing the feldunost in the fields. The youngest daughter Rekka was in town, leading a breakfast "revolution lecture" at the local pub. The party would need to split three ways. The Castellan handed out magic mouth tokens carrying his voice and a code phrase: Operation Hippogriff. Any member of his family would know what it meant and trust whomever carried it.

Lyra and Rooker headed into town to collect the teenager. Thump went with the Castellan to the fields. Azrael and Vicas went stealth into the manor to extract Isla and Lisel.

Vicas and Azrael made it all the way into the courtyard without being noticed, navigating by blueprints of the servant passages that Udo had kept among Bessa's things. They found Bessa's body piked in the yard. They left her. It hurt, but stopping would have blown the whole operation. Using the secret corridors and a set of heartbeat-detecting potions Vicas had recently acquired, they maneuvered expertly around every guard in the manor. They ended up staring into a parlor room where Lady Callista was explaining to Lady Isla that she could save face by accepting her husband's disgrace and agreeing to a marriage proposal for Lisel. A consolidation of land holdings dressed up as mercy. Lisel protested. Callista backhanded her and called her a "heifer to be bought and sold," adding that oldest daughters don't get agency.

Out in the fields, Thump and the Castellan found Haven having coffee with his herd. After a warm reunion, Haven mentioned something odd: three extra feldunost had appeared overnight, and he couldn't figure out where they'd come from. Thump used Speak with Animals and learned that others had recently spoken to the ewe and then wild shaped into the herd. Thump identified the three imposters and told the prized bull stud, plainly, that those three were trying to "take his women". The stud didn't need to be told twice. He charged the first intruder and ran it straight over a ravine and into the river.

In town, Lyra and Rooker found Rekka holding court at a public house, offering to cover the breakfast tab for anyone willing to listen to her thesis that "monarchy equals poverty". Her arguments were, charitably, elementary, and somewhat undermined by the fact that her own father was very much the boot she claimed was crushing the common folk. But she had conviction, and that counted for something. Lyra, meanwhile, overheard a thug conferring with a priest of the Dawnfather. The thug implied that any violence done to Rekka could be justified by her anti-crown rhetoric, and that she was "practically asking for it" by showing up armed. The priest was one of Kato's.

All three scenes popped off at once.

In the manor, Azrael put a dagger through the parlor wall and things turned violent. In the fields, the three imposters shifted back into their true forms, goliath soldiers of Callista's, and Thump and the Castellan unloaded on them while the bull stud handled the third. In town, Rooker played the visiting researcher, dropped Operation Hippogriff, and Rekka left with him and Lyra without a second thought.

But the kerfuffle wasn't quite over. The Thug attempted to hit Rekka with a blowgun to deliver some kind of poison. Rooker landed a powerful Hold Person on the thug while Lyra turned her attention to the young Dawnfather acolyte. She didn't lecture him. She asked questions. Leading ones. The kind that made him question his own faith and how Kato ran things. The moment that broke him was when he asked, gesturing to the paralyzed thug, whether they would be leaving him to "burn beneath the wrath of the blazing sun." Lyra showed him another way.

Inside the manor, Vicas and Azrael had beaten Callista to a pulp while Isla and Lisel salvaged what wealth and assets they could carry. Azrael pocketed the marriage proposal for later reading. On their way out, he and Vicas stopped to bring down Bessa's body from the pike. Lady Isla, watching the manor she'd built her life in, made a decision. She set it ablaze. "I'll be dead before those bastards enjoy the comforts of my family home."

The group reconvened at the stables with more than they'd come for: six feldunost for Ishetar and a newly shaken acolyte of the Dawnfather in addition to the entire Harsk family. As Rooker prepared the teleportation, the Castellan spoke quietly with Udo. The old stablemaster cradled Bessa's body and refused to leave. "Operation Mindflayer," he told the Castellan. Drev reached up and, through tears, modified Udo's memory. When it was done, Udo would remember that the Castellan, his entire family, and the Ishetar Guardians had all perished in the blaze amidst a wave of reinforcements. No survivors. Nothing to chase. It probably wouldn't last forever, but it would stand up to basic truth spells, and it would likely by Udo the plausible deniability he needed.

They left Udo behind, holding his wife, and teleported away.

They arrived in a massive marble hall, likely underground. Before them stood an enormous marble wall fitted with rows and columns of spokes, giant gold rings bouncing back and forth along them like some colossal abacus. A tiny, ancient golden dragonborn with a wispy beard and mustache trailing to the floor greeted them warmly.

"Welcome to the Bank of Zoink. Do you have an account already?"

We leave our heroes there, standing in the vault of something far older and stranger than any of them expected. The Castellan's family is safe. Udo believes them dead. Ishetar waits. And Sallow's forces are still marching north.
