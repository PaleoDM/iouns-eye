We rejoined our heroes in a brief period of pseudo-downtime as they finished tying up loose ends in **Emberpost**. The immediate crises had passed, and what remained were consequences—political, personal, and spiritual.

In the days following the exposure of **Vicar Xona** and the assassination attempt against Duchess Lyla, several matters were resolved. **Father Assam** was formally placed under the authority of the Church, with **High Priest Hiccup** overseeing the process. Though the Church declined to make details public, it was made clear that Assam would not return to any position of influence.

During this time, **Vicas** met privately with Father Assam. The conversation was direct and difficult. Assam pressed Vicas with a pointed question: *“What are the words you wish you had heard, so that I may find it in my heart to say them?”* The two spoke at length about death, what comes after it, and whether the moral weight of a life can ever be cleanly measured. It was unclear whether the exchange brought Vicas peace, but it did seem to help him contextualize his own experiences as personal rather than universal.

Elsewhere, **Duchess Lyla** and **Lady Rowena** met with the party to discuss longer-term political realities. While Rowena would have preferred to remain in Emberpost through the winter to reinforce alliances, it was agreed that **Ishetar** could not be left unattended for that long. There was discussion of expanding the alliance beyond two cities—possibly even a third or fourth partner. The city of **Qonya** was mentioned more than once, though their reclusive nature and unclear allegiances made it an impractical option for the time being.

Before long, preparations were made to depart.

The party boarded **The Shooting Star**, captained by **Captain Alba Skylar**, a familiar figure to some of the players from earlier adventures. Alba was accompanied by two triton attendants, **Dagas** and **Uthe**, whose presence was less that of sailors and more that of personal guards. They were taciturn, observant, and clearly loyal. Alba explained that they had been assigned to her by **Tidemark Janus**.

With most of her seasonal crew already set dockside, the return voyage required the party to assist with sailing duties—a task they accepted readily. Before heading east, however, Alba insisted on a brief detour. Any passage through **Seraph’s Pass** warranted a stop at **Firewatch Island**, a small but strategically vital outpost.

Firewatch Island hosts a monastery dedicated to **Ioun the Knowing Mistress**, serving as an early warning station for vessels approaching from the west. Given the political instability following events in Emberpost, resupplying the island and confirming its status was considered essential. The delay would only cost a few hours.

As the Shooting Star approached the island, it became immediately apparent that something was wrong. No ships were visible in the surrounding waters. The docks were empty. The monastery itself stood silent—until the moment the island’s watchers spotted the ship. Then, the warning bells began to ring.

The party made landfall on the north beach, while Captain Alba remained aboard with **Lady Rowena**, **Dagas**, **Uthe**, and **Vero**, the alchemist Rooker had brought from Emberpost. The heroes were tasked with going ashore to establish contact and assess the situation.

They did not get far.

A group of **harpies** descended on them along the beach path leading up toward the monastery. The opening moments of the fight were dangerous, with several powerful spells landing in quick succession. **Vicas** managed to isolate and overwhelm one of the most dangerous attackers, while **Azrael** took to the air to engage the others directly. **Lyra** provided critical support with *Bless* and followed up with *Burning Hands*, while **Rooker** finished one harpy with a well-placed *Eldritch Blast*. Much of the battlefield control came from **Thump**, whose *Call Lightning* proved decisive—especially as he took cover beneath a pier, minimizing incoming spell fire.

The party survived the encounter, though not without strain. In the aftermath, **Azrael** and **Vicas** discovered the body of a dead dwarf partway up the path, as though he had been crawling toward the monastery. Drag marks on his legs suggested something had attempted to pull him back toward the shore. Among his effects, Vicas found a holy symbol of Ioun—the third eye—and took it with him.

Recognizing the danger, the party withdrew to the ship to regroup.

As they fell back, they witnessed something far worse. A massive creature—eagle-winged, with the antlered head of a stag—circled lazily above the monastery. It swooped down, seized an elven man who had been ringing the warning bells, pierced his heart with its beak, and consumed it before letting the body fall into the courtyard below.

The creature was immediately recognized for what it was: a **peryton**—a predator known for hunting and consuming the hearts of elves and halflings.

Back aboard the Shooting Star, **Dagas** and **Uthe** provided what healing they could. They explained that their people oversee Firewatch Island on behalf of Tidemark Janus, and that the monastery safeguards information of critical importance—specifically related to a dangerous region known as **the Rift**. The abbot of the monastery would have been responsible for that knowledge.

They asked the party to recover the information and learn what had happened. Our heroes agreed.

Before setting out again, **Vicas** pressed the holy symbol of Ioun to his forehead. In response, he received the dying vision of the dwarf who had carried it. He saw waves of undead clawing their way out of the sea, dragging people down below the high tide line, pulling them into the surf. Over and over, the same desperate warning echoed:

*They’re coming from the water. Everything is unsafe. Stay away from the shores.*

We leave our heroes in the mid-afternoon on Firewatch Island—rested, wary, and now aware that the danger here runs far deeper than a single monstrous predator. Whatever befell the monastery, it has already spilled into the sea… and may not remain contained for long.
