We rejoined our heroes in the dense caverns with Scourge, Plague of the Willows, as they tried to settle on some kind of plan. They knew they needed to return to the Silverweave Camp, and likely confront Officer Oswald before they managed to fully power down Nanny PuPu's wards. At their disposal was Scourge and his cult, as well as a pair of Whisper-Wings who owed them a favor in exchange for a spell-riddled corpse of a beholder. The one thing our heroes felt most strongly about was that they needed to treat carefully, for Arden and Jerusha were in there, as possible allies or hostages if they weren't careful.

The plan that formed was to use Scourge and the cult as an ambush. Our heroes would enter Oswald's camp under the pretense of a job well done and time to get a move on. They'd learn what they could about the apparatus that would powerdown the wards, and maybe see what other shenanigans they could get up to, before eventually leading Oswald and the troupe into Scourge and his cult. Scourge even picked out a bend in the caverns where they'd have a nice ambush point.

Back at camp, Veil and Ty paid a visit to the Whisper-Wings. The Whisper-Wings weren't happy to learn that Ozythar hadn't been filled with as many spells as they'd been promised, but quickly fell in line when Veil showed them the vial of Quin's ashes. They'd been *very* keen on eating Quin to begin with. After a quick negotiation, the foxed agreed to amplify player magic (likely a *Command* or *Suggestion* once Oswald was dead) in exchange for "one line each" of Quin's ashes. Ty and Veil were incredulous, but sure enough, the Whisper-Wings took a line of Quin-cane and happily came to heel.

Meanwhile, Thorn had learned the hard way that Officer Oswald, unfortunately, had a backstory, and a "write to monologue" clause in their agreement for hospitality. Thorn, Leon, and Freddie were forced to endure an awful story of Oswald growing up in a pearl fishing community in Pearl Bay, where it is customary for youth to bond with an animal. Oswald was bullied by all the rest for bonding with a lowly sea cucumber; but Oswald was not dismayed. He understand the beast: for it's only goal in life is to continue along doggedly forward, extracting all value and leaving the detritus in its wake.

Eventually, the camp got ready to move. The anti-ward apparatus was packed on a wagon and everyone moved towards the gates, only to be ambushed by a dragon. The fight got off to a great start when Scourge poison breathed all the enemies, and Freddie managed a decent ruse of pretending not to have antagonized the Silverweave folks.

Despite that, the fight has been tense. Freddie and Ty have both dropped at various points, and nearly everyone, including the enemies, is in rough shape. Oswald has a powerful pistol that can poison or charm, and Scourge has unfortunately missed every single attack except for one. Thorn was banished for one turn, but made up for it in spades once he finally rejoined the battle field. Veil brought Ty back from the bring, and has been dealing damage left and right alongside Leon, whose aura is likely the only thing keeping our heroes up.

In spite of it all, thing are dire, and the outcome is very much up in the air, a coin balanced on edge. We join our heroes at the top of Round 4 of combat, hoping desperately to claw their way out of danger.
