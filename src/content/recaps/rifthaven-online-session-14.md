We opened with a moment of calm in the Bastion. Ty, fresh from confrontation and reckoning, received a visit from young Pip Stoutwater—the pint-sized firecracker with a chip on his shoulder and a head full of bad ideas. Pip came seeking wisdom (or vengeance), and Ty, perhaps sensing the danger in both, tried to steer him clear. Pip wanted to make potions—*cool* potions, *angry* potions. Instead, Ty bought him a drum set. A gesture of goodwill? A calculated deflection? Either way, Veil and Quin were not thrilled.

From there, the party descended into the deep: the under dark district of **Tyr’Zareth**, home to the drow and the lakeshore haven where Ty was raised. The district's entry was straightforward—Ty’s identification papers opened the gates, and the city’s soft, phosphorescent glow gave way to the manicured cavern gardens of his family estate.

Only… it wasn’t his parents who greeted them.

It was **Phoebe**—Ty’s elegant, composed sister—and **Breccia**, her closest friend…and Ty’s ex-girlfriend. The encounter was predictably awkward. Breccia was sharp-tongued, poised, and clearly *still wounded.* The conversation danced around old wounds, but finally cut to the chase: Breccia, now working for the **Temple of the Moonweaver**, needed help. There had been break-ins at the temple—not robberies, but *offerings*. Ritualistic deposits of rare incense, powdered gems, and that same strange mineral our party encountered earlier: **Selenualite**. She was desperate enough to ask Ty, and Ty agreed—awkwardly.

After Breccia departed, the party moved into Ty’s childhood home and settled in for tea with his parents, the delightfully opposite couple **Roe** (his sweetly spacey drow hippie mother) and **Cliff** (his gruff, human, “hard-knocks” father). Conversation was strained, especially as it became clear that Cliff *very much* wished Ty was a little more like Thorn. Still, despite the misunderstandings, their love for Ty shone through—especially in a private moment between mother and son, where Roe implored Ty to open up about whatever burden he carried. He didn’t. Not yet.

And so, the party made their way to the **Hall of Reflection**, the Moonweaver’s temple. There, they met **Shai**, another temple acolyte… and maybe something more? Tensions simmered between him and Ty, though the real tension came when the party learned the truth of the break-ins. Whoever was sneaking in was *leaving* items of arcane value—offerings with purpose. Something was being built here. And one of the components? Selenualite. Veil and Quin immediately suspected a ritual of some kind, and Quin honed on teleportation or summoning magic.

Suspicion mounted. Veil and Thorn scouted the area. Breccia suggested a stakeout, and the party agreed. Watches were set, skill checks rolled, and just before exhaustion could set in—the infiltrators arrived. The ensuing skirmish was chaos. In the frakkas, Ty was stabbed with a dangerous poison, and they followed up with a syringe to extract his blood. In the fray, it was unclear if it had been Shai or Breccia, and Ty took his chance to plunge his blade through Shai, who dropped like tissue paper.

But Veil’s eyes were faster than anyone else’s hands, and soon the truth came out: **Breccia**. Traitor, or pawn? Willing, or manipulated? The party took her down but spared her life, binding her and leaving her to face judgment. Meanwhile, Thorn burried his hatchet in the leader like it was an ex lover of his own.

Combat was rough, but **Quin** was transcendent. Fireballs roared. A well-timed **Counterspell** saved lives. Seven enemies died in blazes of arcane fire. Truly, it was Quin’s coming-out party as a full-fledged force of magical destruction. After the fight, Shai was stabilized and Breccia was taken into custody to be questioned. The temple of the Moonweaver had their peace restored.

We ended the session with one final, quiet note: **Ty’s two patrons**—the mustachioed nautilus fiend and the glowing hollyphant **Lulu**—increasingly at odds. Influence, allegiance, identity: Ty's future is not yet written, but the pages are turning quickly.

We rejoin our heroes soon as they ponder who wanted Ty’s blood, and what ritual the stolen offerings were meant to complete…
