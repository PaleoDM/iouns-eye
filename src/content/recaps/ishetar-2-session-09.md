We opened with our heroes still in the vegepygmy village, reflecting with Sprigg on the implications of their journey. The vegepygmies, it seems, truly believe they came from another world, fleeing an ancient threat—the devouring maggots—and their oldest traditions mandate eternal vigilance of the skies. They now fear the worst: that the meteor spotted weeks ago signals the maggots have followed them to Astoria. Though evidence is slim, they are preparing, and they view Ishetar as a vital ally.

Our heroes honored their word, escorting a half-dozen young vegepygmy adults back to Ishetar. Unexpectedly, Pinto Gambino elected to remain behind with the vegepygmies, citing his skill as a linguist, his fear of Turquoise Timber Trust reprisals, and a rare opportunity to do something meaningful. In Ishetar, Lady Rowena greeted the return with joy. To the party’s slight surprise, she embraced the alliance fully. As she put it: they need allies, and this is exactly what she had hoped for. She also apologized to Thump for her earlier slight and formally granted the party **bastions** as thanks.

We then entered a stretch of downtime. The party spent several weeks developing their bastions:

-   **Azrael** established a smithy, staffed by Boris (a burly, short-tempered human) and Grark (a bugbear apprentice). He began outfitting Ishetar with better arms and appointed Jora Caldrow as armory lead.
-   **Vicas** developed a training barracks for spearwork and drills. Old Hrothgar, now retired, took over as drillmaster—Gertrude was glad to see him leave the tavern. Vicas also set up a medicinal storehouse, using Reed the Fence as an intermediary for harder-to-acquire substances.
-   **Lyra** created an herb garden for healing potions, assisted by a vegepygmy named Barry. She also installed a permanent tea station with **Sprout** as the cheerful steward.
-   **Rooker** converted the academy’s office into a functioning library and arcane study, with Sombra assisting and Merris Caldrow beginning training as a magical apprentice.
-   **Thump** built a food garden (amplified by *Plant Growth*) with his new assistant Shoot. He also founded a sanctuary beneath the old maple tree where the Quetzi ritual had taken place, aided by Mikael Caldrow.

We picked up again around the **15th of Sahanine**, when Sombra and Merris called the party together. They had unearthed references to a curious site—**The Restful Lily**—mentioned across several books and trade records. Its location was said to be just beyond the Myreshade River, nestled in the forest of the same name. Some accounts called it a **temple of the Moonweaver**; others described it as a **wizard’s tower**, but all used the same name. With winter approaching and the TTT expected to shutter its river operations soon, this felt like the party’s best (and safest) chance to investigate without crossing paths with the loggers.

After some brief discussion about whether to ford the river or use the logging road bridge, Thump shut down the debate by proclaiming that he could simply let them **walk on water**. With *Water Walk* cast, they took the direct route—until they were ambushed mid-crossing by a cluster of **giant crocodiles and snakes**.

The battle was chaotic and dangerous. Rooker and Thump were nearly dropped in one round. Lyra was grappled and dragged under by a croc. Thump’s first two attempts at *Call Lightning* fizzled or were interrupted—but the third landed with a righteous bolt. Lyra cast *Fireball* for the first time, bathing the field in holy flame, followed by her radiant *Solar Flare*. Vicas and Azrael maneuvered across the battlefield as usual, trying to buy time and draw attention. Rooker, bloodied and reeling, finally landed a few decisive spells. Sombra, surprisingly, scored a critical hit to keep Thump alive.

We ended on the **16th of Sahanine**, the river now behind them, with only a few more hours of travel ahead before the party begins their search for the lost spa, temple, or tower—**The Restful Lily**.
