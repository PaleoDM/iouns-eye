We joined our heroes in their bastions, taking a well deserved rest after the chaos that was the Sergente District and the missing RELICs. Here amdist the safety of their bastions, we saw a few choice scenes:

Sartori came to Veil to indulge in a cup of tea. And from him we learned that the folks of Salvation were moving quickly try and sentence Maximillian Sergente. Sartori asked simply—the sentence was likely to be a hanging, but might end up being hard labor—did our heroes wish to put their thumb on the scale? Veil answered simply: "their district, their justice." The rest agreed.

Then, we saw the other RELICs, Rust, Rider, Clubs, and Spirit, all come to thank Thorn personally for his heroics and the fervor with which he fought to see them liberated from their cages. Spirit, the closest to a leader among the four, explained to Thorn that their people were newly born, with no place in the world and no cultures of their own. Their leader had tasked them with travelling the world and chronicling cultures across the lands, with the responsibility to electing which they should adopt as their own. Thorn and Spirit reflected on their respective people—Thorn's displaced from ancestral land, and Spirit's welcome in their but with no connection to it. Thorn passed on to the RELICs a ritual of his own: to begin every morning with a small meditation contemplating the smallness of self amidst the network of the world tree itself.

Spirit approved. And as a thank you for all he had given them, our RELICs gave Thorn something in return. Spirit handed over an old, gnarled wooden key. "It was given to use", he explained. "We do not know what it does, but we were told that, when your need is most great, one need only ask for a door." Thump wasn't too sure what to do with it, but accepted it graciously.

Then, we saw Captain Reeve thank our heroes for freeing her of her infernal contract, and recommit to serving as their minister of defense. Arden tasked her with bullying Ty just a little bit more, and she good naturedly went right to it. Here too, we saw Arden try on the *Winged Boots* he picked up off of Maximillian Sergente's body, and Ty unveil the Cloak of Mirror Image they found in the bolt room.

Finally, our heroes made their preparations and called on Captain Vex and the *Brass Wing* to see them up into the mountains, in search of an old district called Boughshadow, which doesn't seem to have existed for some 250 years. Here, we learned that Captain Vex is an old family friend of Arden's parents, and he delivered a kind note from Arden's mother:

> "Can't help but notice that, even when you're adventuring, fate brings you back into the air. We always were at our best with the wind in our hair kiddo."

And with that, our heroes were off again. Veil had done some research and learned that Boughshadow was rumored to have been home to an old witch—the kind full of spooky tales that don't really make sense. People insisting that she was good for making you forget what haunted you or making you remember what you'd once repressed. But besides that, the district seems to haave died off some 250 years ago, mostly through normal population decline. Needless to say, Veil had her suspicions...
