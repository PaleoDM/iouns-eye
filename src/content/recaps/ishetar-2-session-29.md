We opened mid-chase, right where we'd left off. Azrael was tearing through the TTT compound with Verlaine on his heels, and the rest of the party was scrambling to converge.

Lyra came in hot. Vicas moved for pure speed, unfurling his wings and taking to the air. Rooker, still stuck at the gate, made a scene: loud enough to pull guards away from their posts and toward the commotion. Thump flew in low and quiet, slipping over the walls unnoticed.

Verlaine closed the distance fast. Her blade caught Azrael hard, nearly dropping him in a single strike, and as it cut, it siphoned his blood, drawing it from the wound and into the weapon itself. Azrael, reeling but thinking clearly, did the smartest thing he could: he screamed for help. Loudly. In the middle of a crowded camp. It put Verlaine in an impossible position. She couldn't finish the job with two hundred witnesses turning to look.

She tried charm instead. Spoke to him like an old friend. Called him by his dead name. Azrael flinched. The rest of the table gasped. He spat at her feet.

Meanwhile, Vicas was coming in fast from above when an anti-air plant, some kind of druidic defense emplacement, spat a bola of vines that caught him mid-flight. He crash-landed hard but used his slow fall to stick the landing near Azrael, arriving bruised but upright.

Lyra, moving through the camp with purpose, noticed something critical: the two guards stationed outside the cellar had abandoned their post to respond to the chaos at the gate. She cast Arcane Eye and sent it down into the cellar, where it found a dead body slumped in one corner, and a barely conscious tiefling man, **Malaki**, the camp medic, manacled to the wall while a druidic shaman bled him from his forearms into a decanter.

With reinforcements closing in, Verlaine threw down a smoke bomb and vanished. Our heroes rallied around Azrael, healing him up and playing into a ruse that someone had just tried to assassinate visiting nobles. The camp bought it, more or less. Vicas, sharp-eyed as ever, noticed strange footprints in the dirt where Verlaine had disappeared.

Then Lyra made her move. She called the gawkers and bystanders toward the cellar for their own safety, given the danger. A brilliant piece of misdirection: she herded the crowd directly to the one place the TTT leadership didn't want anyone looking. What they found down there ended the pretense entirely. The crowd saw the shaman, the blood, the bodies. There was no putting that back in the box.

The druid had no choice but to fight her way out.

A new combat broke out on two fronts. Lyra, Vicas, and Azrael engaged the druid as she tried to flee, while Rooker and Thump squared off against a chain devil that materialized from the very spot where Verlaine had vanished, summoned to cover her escape. The druid opened with Chain Lightning, dealing heavy damage across the group, but she was cornered and outnumbered. She shifted into owl form and tried to take flight, but Vicas snatched her clean out of the air.

Mid-combat, two new arrivals charged onto the scene: **Castellan Drev** and **Captain Kess**, the camp's senior military officers. Drev moved to help against the devil, but Kess, reacting to the chaos and not yet sure who was who, put an arcane bullet into Rooker that nearly dropped him. Thump worked to talk her down while Lyra sealed the deal with a well-placed Calm Emotions.

The druid bled out from the creeping corruption of Vicas's weapon. The chain devil was blasted to pieces. And Kess, no longer firing on instinct, finally took stock of what was actually happening. Especially what was happening in the cellar.

Our heroes pieced it together quickly. The dead body was an aasimar. Malaki was a tiefling. Celestial blood and fiendish blood, harvested and mixed. The decanter wasn't for medicine. It was fuel. The terrible logging machines that had so frightened the wildlife of the Myreshade ran on the blended blood of celestial and fiendish creatures, drained from living prisoners beneath the camp.

Drev and Kess stood there, pale and guilty. Two career soldiers who'd just learned what they'd been defending. Drev, a man on his last job before retirement, suddenly found himself neck-deep in something he couldn't walk away from clean. Neither of them knew what to do next.

Thump and Lyra caught one last detail: Verlaine, perched on the back of a giant eagle, flying south and fast. Our heroes hadn't seen Brenna Tolvane since arriving at camp. The Timbermaster had carried Verlaine to safety and fled.

In tossing Tolvane's quarters, they uncovered one more piece of grim news: a man named **Sallow** had already departed with a contingent of the camp's fighters, marching north to strike Ishetar while its champions were away.

We leave our heroes settling into the Timbermaster's office for a long rest and harder conversations. The camp is leaderless and reeling. Verlaine and Tolvane are in the wind. Sallow's force is marching on their home. And before morning, they'll need to decide: chase their enemies south, race back to defend Ishetar, or try to hold together a camp full of people who just learned the truth about what they've been building.
