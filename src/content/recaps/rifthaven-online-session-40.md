We opened in the aftermath of the Warden's defeat, her puppet corpse still twitching on the orphanage floor. Veil, never one to hesitate, plunged her hand into the wreckage and tore free the human heart still beating within. The moment she touched it, a vision washed over them all — the orphanage as it had once been, long before the poppets and the darkness. A frightened little girl, alone and unwanted, and Guiseppe arriving like a savior, scooping her up and gifting her a delicate ballerina music box. The same music box our heroes now carried. Whatever the Warden had become, she had once been a child nobody wanted. This place has a way of turning that into something monstrous.

While the vision settled over the group, Thorn slipped downstairs and did what Thorn does — he spoke with the plants. An old tree and a clutch of rose bushes told him what they'd observed: the poppet hunters move in pairs, one Reaper and one Bruiser. They could probably handle one pair. Two would be a problem. Also, the Thorns were uncomfortably thirsty for Thorn.

Meanwhile, the rest of the group lingered with the urchins. Leon, drawing on his own journey from Justicar to rebel, tried to rally them — get right, find purpose, fight back. They listened politely, but it was Ty who actually won them over. He dunked on Leon for being a cop, and the kids loved it. There's something about Ty's particular brand of irreverence that resonates with people who've never had a reason to trust authority. The urchins who had initially bullied him for being loved — for having parents who actually cared — found themselves drawn to him instead. The urchins also revealed that the Birthday Host had volunteered herself as tribute to take the place of one full team in the deadly game of Cops and Robbers.

Quietly, off to the side, Sir Sunder pulled Veil aside. This mission to the Pallbearer's domain had turned out to be bigger and darker than he'd anticipated, and Odaire was proof of that. He didn't demand anything, but the implication was clear: the road ahead was his burden, and they didn't have to carry it with him. Veil heard him out but wouldn't commit either way — that would be a group decision. The group, for their part, never got around to making it.

Instead, they moved. Using Lulu as bait in a clever ploy, they lured one of the hunter pairs into a narrow alley for an ambush. The trap went off flawlessly. Leon's aura shielded the group from the worst of the hunters' effects, and nearly every saving throw landed. Thorn shoved the Bruiser to the ground and locked it down, while Veil grappled the Reaper and dismantled it one-on-one. Freddie maintained a critical Bless throughout, and Leon layered radiant damage onto everyone's attacks. Ty landed a clutch Faerie Fire that turned several near-misses into clean hits, accelerating the whole fight. The only real damage came when the Bruiser landed a vicious critical hit on Thorn — nothing Freddie couldn't patch up. All told, the ambush was textbook.

With the hunters dealt with, our heroes finally entered Guiseppe's Toy Shop. Inside, they found the saddest man any of them had ever seen. Guiseppe was courteous one moment and manic the next — clearly tormented by guilt over what his creation had become, yet fiercely, almost desperately protective of Maligno. He is a father who knows his son is a monster and cannot stop loving him.

He traded the compass for the music box willingly enough. But before they left, Guiseppe fixed his gaze on Ty and said something that landed like a blade: "You have the same eyes as Maligno... too sad to have ever been carved into a puppet."

Then came the warning. Ty's glamour had rendered him as a child — and that wasn't just cosmetic. The mists had recognized something unfinished in him. He would not be able to leave Odaire without reconciling his own story first. Ty, who had only recently discovered that he is fully elven — that the man who raised him is not his biological father — did not take this well. What reconciliation even looks like for him remains an open and painful question.

We leave our heroes in the alley behind Guiseppe's shop, compass in hand and an exit finally within reach — only for Maligno's voice to ring out across Odaire. He has captured the Birthday Host, and he intends to execute her unless our heroes show themselves. The way out is clear. The cost of taking it may not be.
