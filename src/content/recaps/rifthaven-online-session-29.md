We joined our heroes desperately holding a critical choke point between the Kruthik proving grounds and their War Chamber. Having dispatched several draconic-dinosaurs in the choke point, it was already beginning to grow quite tight. As Thorn finished off the last one, they found the tunnel partially blocked.

While our heroes took their time to catch their breath, Veil snuck ahead to check out the room within. She soon discovered another dinosaur, and a truly sinister set of creatures—swarms of eye balls that rolled and writhed as a single mass. The eye balls caused a terrible pressure in Veil, who retreated. Meanwhile, her companies began ripping their way through the blockade of dinosaur flesh in the tunnel.

What came next was a tense six seconds of combat during which Veil and then Thorn resisted some particularly dangerous abilities, followed by the total wet balloon of tension. It turns out that Veil and Thorn both have abilities that restrict movement, rendering these creatures unable to threaten our players. With the tension dispersed, we ended the combat early and moved along.

In the chamber beyond, our heroes found a vertical tunnel with a waterfall. Veil mused that they must be approaching the beholder's more inner sanctum, because they are fond of vertical passages that hinder non-hovering creatures. Partway down the tunnel they spotted something unusual—a body.

Our heroes hauled up the mid-thirties human woman and quickly clocked her legionaire boots. Thorn seemed disinterest in anything she might have to say, but Arden seemed inclined to discuss things with her. He passed her a single point of Lay on Hands and the party began interrogating her.

The woman—Jerusha—was appropriately enamored with her rescuer. His cloak billows after all! She was able to tell the players about the location of several key rooms in the dungeon. She confirmed that they had spotted what seemed to be an old dragon egg nest, but had not been able to approach because of harrowing kobolds. They also confirmed that they have physically seen the skeleton of the old dragon queen, and that they don't understand why the kobolds claim to communicate with her still.

Jerusha also revealed something she didn't quite intend—that the commanding officer of the Silverweave Ventures, Officer Oswald, has a means by which they can rest in the cavern without needing to make saving throws. This caught our heroes' attention, for they would much appreciate to rest before taking the fight to a beholder's lair.

With this new information, our heroes decided to visit the legionaires and see if they could find a rest. They arrived at a strange military camp that was a mix of soldiers training and engineers working. When they engaged with Officer Oswald, they found the most heinous and despicable type of person. His grand plan, it appears, is to seize the tree of ambition and cut off all ambition from the world. Then, sell it back piece by piece to communities or wealthy families. What rich family wouldn't pay a small monthly fee to ensure their sons and daughters have great ambition? What nation wouldn't pay heavy tithes to ensure the wheels of industry and innovation can continue? Oswald, like the **other Silverweave Officers plan to do to the other seven trees**, plans to commodify the tree's powers.

When our heroes bid a moment to side bar, Quin summed it up nicely. "Let's agree to any old deal, knowing we'll squelch on it. Anything to get a long rest, investigate the apparatus that enables resting, and return after the Beholder's been dealt with." The rest agreed, and a deal was struck.

We join our heroes late in the adventuring day, planning to settle in for a rest amidst tenuous allies, but also hoping to suss out all they can about this strange apparatus.
