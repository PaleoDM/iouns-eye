We joined our heroes having newly reached level 4 and completed a long rest in the gentle mushroom forest, protected by Care and several Spears and a few Acids. In the morning, they took stock of their situation, and resolved once more to help these mushroom-folk with their "disconnection" from The Great Self, if only to get their own colonial-based systems back up and running.

After a little book keeping and preparation, our heroes set off with Care accompanying them, and moved to investigate the chasm that blocks them from crossing over to the Great Self. With a little searching, they found a narrower section where the chasm was only 20-25 feet across. Close enough that they felt they could make a play at crossing.

It took them some time, but our heroes scavenged old equipment from the storage depot and the docks and managed to erect a makeshift crossing, using the trellises of the old bridge. Veil made it across quickly and with little problem, by Ty found themselves plummeting right through and dangling. Before anyone could move to help, a group of scorpions and spiders, led by a hideous man-spider creature known as an Ettercap, sprung an ambush.

The ensuing battle was pretty rough. The scorpions dealt a LOT of poison damage, and the terrain has our heroes separated at first. Veil actually had to use her reach and grapple to drag Ty back onto the ledge, which was wise because the Ettercap was moving to cut the rope. Quin was downed by poison damage from a scorpion, but Care brought them back up and then Thorn went to work with temporary hit points and a greataxe.

About halfway through the fight, Arden seemed to return to his normal self, and the trident started singing through the air. Veil punched one spider off the ledge and down into the chasm, not to be seen again. Meanwhile, Quin kept one, and then another, under control with a well times Hideous Laughter.

When the dust finally settled, our heroes took a brief rest, and pushed forward, eventually making their way to the heart of the fungal forest. As they approached the Great Self's former lair, they began to see many signs of violence: including deep gashes and burn marks scoring many of the fungal trunks. The Great Self still stood, alone in a thicket, and seemed partially awake still!

Our players actually exchanged conversation, if you can call it that when a creature speaks into your mind. But it quickly became apparent that the damage had been extensive, and the Great Self's sense of self had been split. As the giant fungus argued with itself, Quin stepped forward and urged it to let them help. A strong persuasion check later earned Quin and Thorn insight into how to help the creature—hack away at the blight growing around it and let the new growth spring forth.

The unwilling part of itself, however, resisted. Combat broke out once more. Ty and Arden each used Dissonant Whispers to protect the party from encroaching tree-mushrooms, while Veil began hacking away at the blighted growth. Meanwhile, Quin and Thorn went to town, dealing damage and trying to bring the blighted creature down.

We join our heroes in the throes of combat, with Veil having just finished removing the blight and releasing the new growth, re-awakening the Great Self to their full sapience. But can our heroes finish the job and get themselves to safety? And will the Great Self hold up their end of the bargain?
