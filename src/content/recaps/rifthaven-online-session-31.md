We joined our heroes in the Vestibule to the lair of Ozythar the Tyrant. They had just sent a massive Froghemoth tumbling down the pit that led deeper into the Beholder’s domain, leaving only one last Gauth to dispatch. The creature didn’t last long once Thorn, Ty, Veil, Quin, and Arden brought their combined fury to bear, cutting it down in short order.

With the immediate threat cleared, the party faced a choice: press onward or rest and risk giving the enemy time to recover. In the end, they decided that they would need their strength more than Ozythar would. They took a brief respite, though the Beholder’s psychic influence permeated the region, warping the very air around them. Each of them felt the lair’s oppressive magic press on their minds; all managed to resist it save Thorn, who thankfully needed the rest least of all after Ty’s restorative healing.

When they were ready, the party debated how best to descend the sheer shaft into the lair below. Knowing Ozythar’s anti-magic cone could nullify enchanted flight, they opted for rope and pitons over spells. Their caution proved wise. As they rappelled into the darkness, beams of arcane light streaked through the air, three rays grazing their descent, but none dealing too much damage. The party landed safely on the floor of Ozythar’s lair and the battle began.

The chamber was a labyrinth of floating rock and precipitous drops, a nightmare of vertical terrain designed for Ozythar’s advantage. Eyestalks jutted from the walls like arcane turrets, firing blasts of force, fear, and disintegration. But the Beholder’s own anti-magic cone hindered its defenses as much as theirs, disrupting the barrage. Unfortunately, their earlier opponent had survived the fall—barely. The Froghemoth, restored to full strength, rose again from the depths, and the heroes suddenly found themselves caught between two towering horrors.

Thorn and Veil surged forward, their speed and mobility letting them close the distance on the Beholder while Quin and Ty worked from range and Arden advanced steadily behind. Quin unleashed a devastating *Chromatic Orb*, destroying multiple eyestalks and searing Ozythar’s flesh. Arden’s foresight proved pivotal: his early *Bless* and steady concentration kept the party alive through several close calls, while his Bardic Inspiration turned Veil’s failed petrification save into a miraculous recovery.

Then the fight turned dire. The Froghemoth managed to swallow Quin whole, reducing him to unconsciousness inside its stomach. Ty responded instantly, unleashing a *Psychic Lance* that incapacitated the creature and forced it to regurgitate Quin before pouring divine energy into healing him back to consciousness.

By that point, Thorn and Veil had already dealt staggering damage to the Beholder—nearly 250 points between them—harrying it from every angle and forcing it to fight for every inch of altitude. Ozythar’s eyestalks turned inward, siphoning their own strength to sustain it as the battle reached its climax. The Froghemoth, still alive but dazed, loomed nearby as both threats converged on the party’s last reserves of strength.

We join our heroes at the height of the struggle: the Beholder bleeding and desperate, the Froghemoth reeling, and the heroes battered but standing. One failed saving throw could still mean death or petrification. Victory is close—but survival is far from guaranteed.
