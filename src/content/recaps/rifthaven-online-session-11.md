We joined our heroes in the midst of an absolutely hellacious battle. They, along with Dante, had just infiltrated th cultist's main ritual chamber, and walked in on a massive ritual with over a dozen cultists ready to sacrifice as many civilians. They got to work.

The ensuing chaos came with mixed results. Several civilians were lost before our heroes could act, largely due to initiative. Still, our heroes pushed further and further in to the massive chamber, managing to cut their way through several of the weaker cultists and engaging with the true leadership.

Ty found himself on the failed end of a Hold Person, but Quin soon freed him by returning the favor to the caster in question. Thorn and Veil fought their way through a few mooks up to the front line and began hacking and slashing as best as they could, while Arden did his best to support on all fronts.

By the end of the third round, 3 of the civilians had been saved and the rest fallen to the sacrifice. Ty had blasted several cultists on the far end of the room and caused at least one to sacrifice themselves into the summoning pit. On the eastern front, Veil and Dante had dispatched several, with the help of Ty's Faerie Fire, while Thorn stood a lone and held the Western Front like a one man wall. Unfortunately for our heroes, that was the moment where the ritual completed.

One round later, the battle was interrupted by the appearance of a strange new humanoid creature, pulled through from beyond the veil. They were tall, handsome, and dressed in fine jewelry and robes with intricate piercings ornamenting their tiger-like features. And their hands...on backwards at the wrist, like some strange glitch. They raised a pipe to their lips and threatened our heroes, but not before our heroes jumped into action.

Ty, Arden, and Quin took the opportunity created by this man's appearance to slay another cultist or two, while Thorn and Veil hit this newcomer with everything they had. And...got nothing for it. A few measly points of damage pushed through what must have been some type of immunity or resistance. Still, it was enough. The newcomer voiced displeasure that the ritual hadn't had enough blood to "bring their defenses fully on line". They elected to flee, and in their place left two Mezzoloth's to harass our players.

And *that* battle nearly did them in. One of the Mezzoloth's cast stinking cloud, while the other grabbed Dante and attempted to throw him into the summoning pit. Dante clung on with a dexterity save to catch the Mezzoloth's boot, and Veil soon dragged him back out while dispelling the cloud with a powerful gust. Thorn proceeded to do his best to chase the Mezzoloth's around the battle field, each time whittling away for a little more damage. Which proved essential, because Ty and Arden were each locked in one-on-one contests with cultists that prevented them from properly helping.

In the end, Ty managed to slay his cultist, only for the bastard to roll a natural 20 and rise again to keep fighting. Arden managed just barely to pull away from his own and buy enough time for Quin to provide some very timely assistance. Veil successfully distracted one Mezzoloth by reducing its damage over and over just long enough to kill it herself.

By the skin our their teeth, our heroes managed to keep Arden up to, in turn, keep Ty up. And Thorn came bellowing from across the battle field and threw one final, mighty hatchet that tore into the creature and finally brought it down. It was...intense.

We join our heroes having just been joined by their "distraction"—the townsfolk who managed to fight their way through and secure the tenements, since all of the cultist' forces were down here. Our heroes are badly wounded and in desperate need of a rest. But they were also promised exposition and loot. So here it comes!
