We opened deep in the Goldwarren tunnels, where our heroes were already bracing for a dangerous confrontation. They attempted one last scout ahead, sending an invisible Rooker into the cavern. He managed to slip past the enemy lines unseen and identified an elemental warrior, several more Aurumvaxes, and the sound of someone speaking Primordial to an unseen figure. Unsure whether to storm the chamber or hold the narrow hall as a chokepoint, the party chose the latter, fearing their enemies might use the same tactic against them.

The opening exchange went poorly. Azrael, Vicas, and Rooker held the line, but the cramped corridor left no space for Lyra or Thump to see or meaningfully contribute. Thump managed to set down his spirit animals, though with limited utility. Then, in an effort to force the party forward, the elemental executed one of the trapped civilians, leaving the heroes no choice but to surge into the room and engage fully.

The fight that followed was fierce and chaotic. Several mephits darted out from the shadows, carrying orders to flee and warn others that the princes were currently unguarded. Thankfully, the lingering spirit animals blocked the only exit, preventing the creatures from escaping. As the melee intensified, Vicas carved down the elemental piece by piece, Lyra blinded several enemies with a flash of radiant power, Rooker locked an Aurumvax in place with a hypnotic pattern, and Thump maneuvered the spirit animals with almost manic precision. Azrael hurled himself into the fray to reinforce Vicas at the front, expanding their defensive line and turning the momentum in the party’s favor.

But just as everything seemed under control, disaster struck. Although the elemental collapsed, Azrael had collapsed with it—unconscious and already one failed death save deep. A blinded mephit, cornered and desperate, threatened Thump: drop the spirits or Azrael would die. Unsure whether it was bluffing, Thump chose silence. The mephit was not bluffing. It slashed Azrael across the throat, killing them instantly, then charged toward the exit—only to disintegrate upon hitting the spirit animals, dealing even more damage to Azrael’s fallen form. In a moment, it was done. Azrael lay dead on the cavern floor.

The battle ended quickly after that, and the party rushed to begin a resurrection ritual. Rooker frantically attempted to stabilize Azrael’s shifting form, fearing their gender-affirmed vessel might revert in death, but was relieved to find it unchanged. Lyra began preparing her ritual tea, working quickly but methodically, while Vicas washed the body, tended the wounds, brushed the hair, and prepared the vessel with the reverence of a healer inviting a soul home. Thump used his Land’s Aid to grow a soft bed of moss and delicate foliage beneath Azrael, urging their spirit gently toward the world of the living.

Rooker attempted to contribute as well, calling to Bucephalus, the legionnaire’s skull from the Red Vale—only for another voice to answer, one that slithered up from the rot inside his own flesh. “Call me Maggot Daddy,” it whispered. When Rooker faltered, the voice pressed further: “Say it. Say the words, little grub.” Rooker broke immediately, flinging the skull aside and fleeing the ritual, much to Lyra’s relief; she feared the mingling of his magic might jeopardize the rite.

Azrael, meanwhile, found themselves before the Raven Queen, immense in her mantle of black feathers, and beside her the small porcelain doll of Obitris. “Are you tired?” asked the doll. “You can rest, if you’re tired. But your friend came by to play.” Azrael saw Lyra kneeling beside his body, rolling a small toy ball toward him. He rolled it back—and rolled his soul back into his waiting vessel. And then he awoke.

In the aftermath, the heroes collected several vials of Lolth’s Sting, a scroll of bless, a scroll of bane, and two healing potions. Uzoma gifted them a new diamond to replace the one consumed in the ritual. They escorted the rescued miners to safety and took time to check on Azrael, and on Rooker, who confessed during several long conversations that he was not a wizard and feared he had gotten in far too deep with forces he no longer understood. The group rested, finally, and slept deeply.

At dawn, a young newsie arrived with the morning’s broadsheet. “Extra silver today—big headline! Duchess Lyla found dead overnight!” Our heroes purchased the paper, and that is where we rejoin them now.
