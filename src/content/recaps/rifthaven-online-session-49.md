We opened with a sending spell.

Professor Serrano, the gnome necromancer from High Haven, had been trying to reach our heroes by homunculus. The homunculus had not made it past the front door. Captain Reeve had apprehended it before it could deliver its message and it sat downstairs waiting while Reeve waited too, suspicious.

Veil went down to sort it out. She vouched for the creature and got it in front of the party. It was a strange thing. Bits of driftwood and salvaged carcass held together by spark plugs and gnomish ingenuity, piloted remotely by Serrano from somewhere across the city. Unsettling in the way that all of Serrano's work tends to be.

His message was this: he had been given a job. The Cassini Brothers had instructed him to lure our heroes to a specific location, a trap, and make sure they walked into it. He was going to tell them this, though. Thorn had scared the old gnome thoroughly enough back at the High Haven Academy that he was not particularly inclined to lead people he was afraid of straight to their deaths. He figured that telling them it was a trap technically satisfied his obligation to both parties and kept him compliant with everyone. Technically.

The bait itself was real information. One hundred and eleven souls, the collateral the Pallbearer had been holding on Haji's behalf, were about to be moved. With the Pallbearer out of commission, Haji had an opportunity. Bring those souls to the Nine Hells. Spend them climbing. A rakshasa arriving with that many powerful souls and leverage over an entity like the Pallbearer would arrive very well-positioned. The window to stop it was closing. Serrano didn't know where the souls were going, but he knew where our heroes could find out. The trap location: the Coin Counting House in the Hustler's District.

Freddie's arcane eye did the rest. He cast it and walked it through the building from a safe distance, cataloging every figure waiting inside. They knew exactly who was in there before they touched the door. Then Thorn put his hand on a tree.

He has a relationship with trees, a particular kind of listening the rest of the party has learned not to question. The tree knew things. It knew where the second wave was waiting. How many. What they were carrying. Our heroes looked at each other and made the call: why deal with the front door when you can skip it entirely?

They went straight at the second wave. Major Dunthar was waiting there, but definitely wasn't prepared for them to find him.  The Cassini Brothers' heavy hitter though, hadn't come alone. With him were several assassins, a handful of thugs, and a bone devil for company. Not a small fight.

Freddie had come prepared. Dispel Evil and Good went up, and the bone devil found it couldn't touch him. It tried twice. Freddie stood there and took both rounds of attacks without a scratch. The spell held, and a devil that should have been terrifying became a very expensive distraction.

Thorn drove straight at Dunthar and locked him and one of the assassins down immediately. On the other side of the fight, Freddie, Veil, and Leon picked up the second assassin and the remaining thugs. Ty opened with Faerie Fire and the whole board lit up. Advantage across everything that glowed.

Veil was exceptional. Critical after critical, her strikes landing clean and hard. The fight went her way from start to finish. Major Dunthar went down. Our heroes took his signet ring.

Freddie put it to immediate use. He sat down with the ring and a blank set of documents and produced something that looked exactly like official Cassini Brothers orders. Strong work. The thugs waiting inside the Coin Counting House received their updated instructions: clear the place out, bring everything to a specific address. That address happened to be one of our heroes' own safe houses. They complied without question. Our heroes came into a significant amount of gold without ever setting foot inside.

Then they interrogated the one remaining thug and put together what had actually been happening. The trap was never about the Coin Counting House. The whole operation, the lure, the misdirection, the second wave, all of it was designed to pull our heroes away from Sparkworks. Away from the tunnels. Because the Cassini Brothers had always planned to move the souls through those very tunnels. Through the same smuggling network our heroes had been building and protecting for months. Use the enemy's own infrastructure against them.

Our heroes knew exactly where to be next.

We leave them heading back to Sparkworks. Back to the tunnels. Back to where it all started. One hundred and eleven souls are in motion, and the Cassini Brothers are at the other end.

Session fifty. The finale.
