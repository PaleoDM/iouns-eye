We opened with our heroes moving away from High Haven, crossing the upper district from its north face to its south, making for the Ju Shi monastery. One institution down. One to go. They arrived to find the gates down. The Ju Shi monastery doesn't do lockdowns. It was doing one now.

A young monk stood guard outside. Duty, by name and by posting. He was happy to talk. Yes, something had happened. No, he couldn't say what. Yes, the monastery was sealed. No, no one was getting in or out. Veil was already thinking about the long way around when a dart buried itself in Duty's neck and he dropped, out cold, propped up against the wall like furniture.

Veil tracked the movement and found Ghost. An old friend. Monk name, the only one she goes by. Come to collect. Ghost didn't have lockpicks. No matter. The lock was not much of a lock for Veil, and they were through it quickly. She led them to a quiet corner to talk.

Ty caught her eye. The leather pants, the Goth disposition. Ghost was smitten fast. Ty opened his mouth to respond and nearly fumbled it. Ghost shushed him before he could ruin it. Better, she said, if his personality stayed up to her imagination. Ty, for once, took the note.

What Ghost told them was ugly. Vine Ashen Bloom had come for Sunder and accused him of every crime she herself was actively committing. Collusion with the House of Coinwrights. Backroom dealings. The works. And Sunder had not fought it. He had played the part of a doddering old fool, simple and confused, and had gone quietly. Not out of cowardice. Out of principle. He refused to lift a hand against any of the other monks of the order, so he let himself be taken.

The tribunal had not been invoked in over sixty years. The monks were shaken. Lines had been drawn.

Ghost wrapped our heroes in Pass Without a Trace and led them deeper into the monastery. What they saw as they moved was wrong. Coinwright muscle. Hired mercenaries in every hall where monks should have been. Vine had come with purchased protection.

When they reached the tribunal chamber, Sunder had found a way to stall. The Aletheia procedure requires observance of every custom of every people who stand accused under it. On his grandfather's side there was a tradition. No court could be held until every name in the family line had been spoken aloud. A name at a time. Names for hours. Sunder sat calmly while a reader worked their way through his ancestry.

Ghost announced them. Our heroes strode in and declared themselves Sunder's legal counsel.

Sunder caught Veil's eye and made it plain. He had not come to be tried. He had come to be represented. He was invoking trial by combat, and our heroes were the ones doing the combating. Vine Ashen Bloom was not pleased, but she thought her goons would be enough. She was wrong.

It was a tough opening. An assassin closed with Veil fast, and for a moment it looked like the assassin might carve real damage out of her before Veil settled into the fight. She settled. The assassin did not last long after that. Ty, meanwhile, caught the attention of a heavy knight in Vine's pay and took some punishing hits before he found his footing and dropped a psychic blast that stunned the man mid-swing. Incapacitated, the knight became a problem the rest of the party could solve. Thorn and Leon tore through the remaining mooks. Freddie held spirit guardians, keeping everything else locked down.

It was over quickly once the momentum turned.

Sunder stood, dropped the old-fool act, and turned the tribunal back on Vine. Veil and Leon laid out the evidence the party had been gathering for weeks. Her ties to the Coinwrights. The soul trade. The Pallbearer. The bodies. It was damning, and the tribunal did not flinch from it. The vote went overwhelmingly against her.

The problem was what came next. None of them had presided over a prosecution in living memory. The tribunal was unsure what punishment a Divine Judge warranted. Veil and Leon pushed back hard. This was not a matter for a backroom tribunal. Vine should stand a proper trial in front of the people and answer for what she'd done in the open.

Vine did not care for that plan. She reached for a dimension door and the spell fizzled under a counterspell from Ty before she could finish the casting. Initiative. She rolled a two. Our heroes rolled a great deal better than that, and she was on the floor before she understood what had happened.

We leave our heroes between scenes, having taken a long rest and waking up at level thirteen. Both votes of no confidence have now been invoked. The Aletheia procedure is frozen.

One piece of business remains. The Cassini Brothers are still standing, and they are Haji's last remaining stool. Knock them out, and the rakshasa has nowhere left to sit.
