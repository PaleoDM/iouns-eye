The rest of the fight wasn't much to write home about. Veil finished absolutely 1v1ing the Shoosova, then ran full blown around the house to support her friends. Leon closed with one of the major spell casters and laid several debilitating blows. Lulu finished off the Shadow demon, while Freddie put down a critical healing and Thorn cleaned up. Before they all knew it, the fight was over.

As Sir Sunder prepared the portal, Veil quickly interrogated the trapped assassin. The hit had been ordered by a shadowy entity known as The Pallbearer, but these goons were merely hired hands. They didn't work for the Pallbearer, nor were they were from his domain. They came from a domain of a shadowy entity known as Maligno, the create Poppet Master, and feared something called The Progenitor. Thorn deigned the assassin could live, and our players flung themselves into the portal.

As they fell through the realms, they glimped the shadowy echo of Rifthaven as it exists in the Shadowfell. A wicked place of terrible darkness and sharp angles, with specters and wraiths flying about. But they did not linger long, for their plane shifting landed them somewhere else entirely—shrouded in deep mist.

Sir Sunder, still clutching the heart from the other assassin used as a focus, found that the mists receded from it wherever he directed it. With trepidation, they followed the heart as it led them through the mists and to an abandoned cottage on a hill, overlooking a quaint little village. Here, they set down to collect their thoughts and figure out what happened. Veil availed herself of the Architect's Key to check out Sir Sunder's research room.

Collectively, our heroes set out to put a few clues together. Thorn spoke with a rotten Pumpkin, Ty investigated the cottage, and Veil did some research and got some explanation from Sir Sunder about the nature of his newest mission:

-   Sir Sunder's investigation into Quin had revealed potential corruption related to the Alethia procedure.
-   This had led him to a dark entity in the Shadowfell known only as The Pallbearer.
-   Sunder had needed a dark talisman to come to the shadowfell—ideally one attuned to no particular domain so that he would arrive in Shadow Rifthaven.
-   The assassin's heart seemed to be attuned to their home domain—a third location entirely that is neither their destination (The Pallbearer's Domain), nor baseline Shadow Rifthaven.
-   Domains of Dread are ephemeral and dangerous, and can only be arrived at with a special talisman attuned to the domain.
-   They are governed by dark lords that are nigh unkillable, cannot be defeated, and are nearly impossible to escape.

Thankfully for our heroes, Lulu actually recognized the Domain's energy. "I have an auntie here!" she declared, and informed the party that they were in a land called Odaire. It is a wicked place ruled by an angry child who desperately wishes he had been made properly real. Her auntie, known as the Faerie Godmother, would be here somewhere, doing her best to preserve hope amidst the darkness. Finding her could win them a potentially powerful ally.

Uncertain how to proceed, but knowing that their only way out involves finding some kind of talisman to another location and "demonstrating to the mists that their story no longer compels them here", they set down into town. Lulu followed the advice of a celestial inscription Ty found, and placed a glamour upon our heroes—Ty and Sir Sunder were made teenagers, while all the rest were made wooden poppets.

The disguises quickly came in handy when they ran into a platoon of wooden poppets. They hailed Veil, and insinuated that they had been rounding up the oldest children. Finding two late stage teenagers flew several of them into a frenzy, and soon Veil and the other poppets were defending Sunder and Ty from these Carrionettes.

The battle was, mercifully, well handled by a combination of Spirit Guardians and insane battlefield movement from Veil and Thorn. Ty came dangerously close to being downed, and Leon was nearly body swapped by their evil, magical needles, but in the end it ended well for our heroes. We join them having just been hailed by a young teen looking out a window, who ushered Ty and Sunder up through the window and told them "their poppets are welcome so long as they don't narc". Then, they prompty offered Ty bubble gum and chewing tobacco, and let our heroes into the strangest clubhouse they've ever seen.

All this, and still not a clue how to escape the wicked domain of Odaire.
