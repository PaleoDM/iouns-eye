We rejoined our heroes in the thick of battle, with the streets and alleys of Salvation alive with the sounds of conflict. Reinforcements from the Sergente estate had arrived in waves, looking to crush the growing resistance—only to find our party waiting.

The second wave was summarily dismantled. Veil isolated the incoming mage and went to town, while Thorn unleashed a brutal new strategy: toppling foes prone, locking them in place with his roots, and watching as they failed—again and again—to stand. Once rooted, Thorn planted himself in the central square and made life an absolute nightmare for every soldier that crossed his path. Meanwhile, Arden (still off-screen this week) focused on keeping his comrades upright, slinging healing spells and stabilizing those teetering on the edge.

Things heated up as the third wave arrived—accompanied by a notorious bandit enforcer and a hidden mage who revealed their presence with a devastating *Cone of Cold*. With no counterspell ready, the blast hit hard. Suddenly, the fight shifted. Resources ran thin, health pools dwindled, and the situation grew dire. But that's when our heroes do their best work.

Veil, realizing that Thorn wouldn't survive another round of the Bandit Captain's poisoned crossbow bolts, grappled her and dragged the derocious woman an entire town block to bring them into line of sight of Quin. Then, she unleashed a powerful stunning strike which Quin, now in sight, was able to portent into a failure. Having bought themselves some time, the rest went to town on the mage. Ty landed a clutch counterspell while Quin ricocheted several chromatic orbs around, bringing everyone down quickly. In the end, Quin dealt the final blow with a final *Firebolt*, point-blank to the face, dropping the mage for good.

As the Ledgermen retreated from Salvation, the town was still. Victory had been earned, but at a bloody price. Most folks left the cleanup for the morning, turning instead to the *Pulqueria* and other taverns to shake the adrenaline and the edge. Our heroes stayed close, catching their breath amid the embers of victory.

Veil took the quiet moment to plant something louder—a whisper campaign. Gathering the local children, she armed them with the truth: that the "stolen property" the Ledgermen sought were people, and that the House of Coinwrights was emopowering slavers. One bold kid asked, “Is it true? ‘Cause lying costs extra.” Veil smiled, assured him it was, and sent them running. By dawn, the story would run across the broadsheets. The Ledgermen *and* House of Coinwrights would feel this one.

But if our heroes had earned a night of rest, the de la Alma family had not. From the periphery, the party watched as Tota and her granddaughters began mobilizing. They learned that Romero—Tota’s son-in-law and Clara’s husband—had been kidnapped and was now a prisoner within the plantation. An engineer by trade and respected among the people, Romero was now likely being forced into aiding the same horrific experiments that our heroes had interrupted in the Moving Fortress.

Tota had a plan. And she hadn’t been waiting on our heroes to execute it. The plan had three parts:

1.  **The Distraction.** Luz and her two cowboy friends would lead an external assault, on the plantation walls, driving as much Ledgerman attention to them as possible. But to do so, she needed the family's old dendritium augers—heavy-duty speeders capable of drilling through the mountain stone. Without them, they lacked the firepower to make a meaningful distration.
2.  **The Extraction.** While the distraction drew forces out, Tota and her daughter Clara would infiltrate the plantation through the old servants passages. Then, they'd attempt to sneak through to locate and rescue Romero.
3.  **The Kill.** Carmen, the youngest daughter—barely 17—had a role of her own. Lord Sergente, it turns out, is rather fond of her. Carmen would accompany him into the manor under false pretenses, hoping to uncover the location of his panic room, secret passage, or escape route. Once found, she’d earn her family's revenge.

It would be difficult and grim work, especially for one so young. And Luz complained loudly that their roles should be reversed, but Tota would have none of it. "Fetch me my adventuring pack, Mija." she calmly commanded, and that was that. Our heroes didn't argue with the plan. If nothing else, they were impressed that, for once, no one was begging them for help or assuming they'd be the lynchpin. That alone endeared them to the de Alma's, and they quickly offered their help. It helps, of course, that this is an opportunity to deal the Ledgermen a debilitating blow and potentially extract the kind of incriminating evidence that would cause real problems for Haji.

We join our heroes having rested and recovered, and found their abilities having grown. Now, they prepare their assault. Step one: recover the dendritium augers for Luz. From there, prepare to join Tota as she infiltrates the plantation and get to work. If they succeed, it may just be the catalyst that topples the entire Sergente operation—and with it, the Coinwrights' foothold in Salvation.
