This is a shorter recap, on account of much of it being devoted to downtime. We picked up after an extended period of downtime. Roughly four to five weeks passed as our heroes recovered, tied up loose ends, and prepared for what came next, returning in the latter half of the Month of Corellon. Thorn spent a night beneath the Tree of Ambition and emerged with a potion of *Telepathic Bond*. With that done, the group left the tree in the care of Rosie, Scourge, and Nanny Pu’Pu, for better or worse.

Back in Rifthaven, Leon and Freddie took up residence in Quin and Arden’s bastions, neither having a strong foothold in the city. The group expanded their holdings significantly. Freddie established a Workshop and Sanctuary, creating a second major temple presence in Sparkworks alongside Sister Elarra’s Temple of the Allhammer. He also oversaw the creation of a Scriptorium and a Trophy Room, allowing him to pursue information, magic items, and lore.

Leon settled into Quin’s former Library and Arcane Study, though he was disappointed to find the animated cleaning device still hostile to his presence. He added a Sacristy, enabling the production of holy water, limited spell recovery, and access to divine resources. Leon also constructed stables, and despite Sparkworks being an underground district, it now hosts one of the city’s most impressive elk farms—something to be revisited later.

Veil expanded her holdings with a Training Arena and a Gaming Hall. The Gaming Hall enhanced the intelligence network she had previously established through her speakeasy, moving her closer to a full spy pub operation. The Training Arena attracted skilled instructors, giving the party a reliable way to refine their abilities; Veil currently benefits from increased damage on her unarmed strikes thanks to training with Commander Reeve.

Thorn and Ty each expanded their gardens into powerful greenhouses. Ty’s produced potent poisons intended to support the party’s martial members, while Thorn’s yielded unusually strong healing potions. Ty also constructed a teleportation circle, a clear nod to Quin, which now allows the party to move quickly across Rifthaven.

From his Trophy Room, Thorn uncovered useful information: Silverweave Ventures was a relatively recent organization with early ties to Pearlhaven Forgeworks, the business run by Arden and Freddie’s father. Those dealings had soured in recent years, possibly connected to a failed attempt by Silverweave to acquire land in Pearl Bay for a resort project. Freddie’s role in dismantling Silverweave operations likely ended that relationship permanently.

With downtime complete, the party set out again. Veil had received a message from her old mentor stating it was time for her naming ceremony. The group traveled into the mountains to the Temple of the Ju-Shi and met with Sifu Stone, Whisper Bloom of Sundering—known to Veil as Sir Sunder—who had taken her in as a child. Sir Sunder was warm and welcoming, especially toward Ty, though the meeting began tensely when he mistook Leon for a Justicar sent to collect him. Leon clarified that Sir Sunder was not his target and that deception was not the Justicars’ way, easing the situation.

Sir Sunder asked Veil to organize his documents, giving her an opportunity—intentional or not—to discover that he was preparing for a mission involving the Aletheia Procedure. She also found notes resembling a large ritual, structured similarly to the way Quin once cast rituals. Outside, Sir Sunder teased Ty about serving as best lad during the ceremony, a role Ty accepted eagerly.

The naming ceremony proceeded without incident. Veil was given the name **Tempest**, then unexpectedly received a second name as well: **Truth**. Veil, Tempest of Truth. When asked if it was true she had slain a beholder, she answered yes, to the delight of the assembled monks, who had prepared beholder-themed piñatas. Ty spoke during the ceremony, crediting Veil as the first person who ever believed in him. As part of the ceremony, Veil received the **Belt of the Shi-Ho**, granting her expanded control over elemental damage.

After the celebration, as the group prepared to depart, they uncovered signs of trouble. Sir Sunder had left early, and suspicious figures were found near his quarters. Investigating further, Veil entered his apartments and found multiple dead bodies, along with one individual partially embedded in the earthen floor to restrain him. Outside, the rest of the party was ambushed by shadow elves—Shadar-kai—while inside, Veil confronted a shadow demon known as a **Shoosuva**.

Combat broke out on both fronts. Veil pushed inside to aid Sir Sunder, who was still alive and fighting back with elemental magic. Outside, Leon, Thorn, Freddie, and Ty held off the attackers, aided by the fact that one of the assailants badly injured their own allies with a poorly placed area attack. As the round turned, Sir Sunder tore the heart from one of the Shadar-kai, declared it “suitably macabre enough” to complete his ritual, and informed Veil—somewhat apologetically—that he was about to open a portal for a mission. She and her companions were welcome to join, but not required to.

Veil, exasperated but unsurprised, shook her head and began organizing the group to move through the portal.
