We opened with our heroes making their final preparations before nightfall. Lyra, having discovered the hidden cellar with what seemed to be survivors, attempted to make her way down, only to be hit by powerful ambient magic. The magic itself was overwhelming, almost nausea inducing, and took the form of illusion or enchantment. As Lyra pushed forward and attempted to speak with one of the survivors, it became clear that they too, were falling under its effect.

Eventually, the two survivors, a woof elf named Tallos and a dwarf named Janore, were able to recall that they had one more with them. They had been saved by a young human man, whose given name seems to be Daft, but who these two call Deft instead. It soon became apparent that Deft was the source of this powerful magic. The young man seemed to have some intellectual disability, but was capable in his own way, including inadvertantly wielding powerful sorcery for the protection of those he cared about. Deft had managed to drag Tallos and Janore alongside their wizard friend down to the cellar and hide them with shadow magic. The wizard Archais had since been lost trying to make a break for the bell tower to signal for help.

As our heroes reconvened, it became clear that the survivors were suffering from an illness known as Bluerot: a terrible wasting disease that suppresses the immune system and causes them to quickly wither to nothing. Our heroes quickl decided: the survivors needed Lady Rowena and the paladins aboard The Shooting Star.

While several of them transported folks back and forth via the southern cove of the island, the rest busied themselves with handling the "meat buoy" as Azrael put it: a sinister mound of corpses all balled up in a fishing net. As best as our heroes could gather, the hoard from the night before had been unable to carry away all their dead, and had stashed them here "for safe keeping". Our heroes knew these folks needed to be put to rest, most especially lest they join the undead masses the next night. But doing so would take time. Collectively, our heroes set about lighting and burning a funeral pyre all through the evening and into the night, while also bolting up the defenses and preparing the old pirate fortress for an attack.

Lyra cast Dawn on The Shooting Star and sent them out to open water, while Thump gave everyone Water Walk in case they need to support the ship in short order. Then, they prepared for the zombie apocalypse. It came with a whimper; a handful of weak forward troops clawing their way through the fortress's outer walls. Our heroes were ready. Azrael leveled attacks through the bolt hole, while several casters sat up on the second floor slinging spells and Vicas held down the one obvious entrance (where the T-Rex had previously busted through).

Things were going swimmingly (Spirit Guardians plus Entangle really putting in the work) until some of the shock troops revealed themselves to have a Davy Jones's Jaunt ability to teleport. And then the boss showed up astride a roaring zombie T-Rex (badly singed from a previous Turn Undead). We join our heroes in slightly rough shape, Vicas having taken a great deal of damage but still standing thanks to a critical Dodge Action. Just as our heroes are turning on the T-Rex and the boss, the giant Peryton has made its own arrival, with the bloody remains of many Elven zombies from a previous fight. Things are getting tense, and somewhere off shore, the Shooting Stat defends itself from problems of its own...
