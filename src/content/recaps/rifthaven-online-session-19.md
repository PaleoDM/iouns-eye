We rejoined our heroes mid-assault on the Moving Fortress in the Salvation District, having just dispatched a mix of crude and profane constructs guarding its perimeter. With Thorn recovering from paralysis, Veil and Arden scouted ahead through the upper windows and laid plans for a coordinated breach. When Thorn was ready, the party struck. He ripped open the side doors while Veil and Arden snuck in the opposite end, flanked by Ty and Quin for support.

They quickly came face-to-face with **Captain Reeve**, the only apparent human officer aboard, and identified her as the their primary threat. Captain Reeve was actively collecting documents, either to destroy them or to escape. Just as she attempted to cast *Dimension Door*, Ty hit her with a timely *Counterspell*, sealing her fate. The group surrounded her and, through sheer action economy, knocked her unconscious.

Meanwhile, animated mechanical defenders had come to her aid, but Veil, Thorn, and Arden worked quickly to mop them up. Quin drew one of them away and managed to disconnect the artillery ballista to turn it around on the enemies. Once the skirmish ended, the party tied Reeve up and rifled through her effects. They learned she was a hired mercenary bound to the House of Coinwrights via some unnamed but powerful blackmail. Among her notes, they found troubling references to a family in Salvation, and in particular, a youth named **Ernesto de la Alma**, who seemed to be of great interest to Dr. Sprocket. The tone suggested something deeply uncomfortable. even to Captain Reeve.

From there, the party descended into the belly of the fortress and quickly located a workshop filled with horrific sounds. After a brief banter back and Forth, Veil worked open the door, revealing **Dr. Sprocket**, the twisted artificer himself, and a hideous flesh golem shaped unmistakably in the image of Ernesto. The construct was huge, stitched together with cruel craftsmanship and fused to arcane bindings. His will seemed to be controlled by a painful crown embedded in his still open and lobotomized cranium. It was clear the young man had been violated and remade.

Combat erupted immediately. Thorn entered a rage and smashed through the doctor while Quin landed critical hits with *Chromatic Orb*. Once Quin deduced that the golem could be temporarily disabled by lightning, he and Veil focused their attacks accordingly. Ty and Arden provided crowd control and backup support, fending off smaller constructs that had joined the fray.

Near the end of the battle, Thorn found a cell housing the four RELIC prisoners: **Rust, Clubs, Rider, and Spirit**. He invoked Sartori’s true name—**Relic Beta07**—and earned their immediate trust. With the threat neutralized, the party tended to the catatonic Ernesto and began combing through Sprocket’s notes.

What they found was deeply disturbing. Dr. Sprocket's Journal read:

> **8th of Bahamut, 1128**
>
> "I must admit, I understand little and care even less for why the Lord Sergente and The Dividend are so hell bent on torturing the de la Alma family. But it is, by all accounts, fortuitous, for their youngest lad seems a prime candidate upon whom to begin our work."
>
> **26th of Bahamut, 1128**
>
> "It is better than I could have hoped. The lad, Ernesto, is as tall and broad as an ox, and nearly as strong. Better still, he as advertised—simple of mind and spirit. His thoughts and pure and sluggish, and he disposition trusting and innocent. Almost willingly he relinquishes to the garrote of the marionette."
>
> **4th of Raei, 1128**
>
> "The initial procedure worked. And, with the help of Dr. Solvane's crystals, we have largely blocked off the boy's memories. Assuming the procedure sticks, we will begin the augmentation upon the new year."
>
> **17th of Rai, 1128**
>
> "I think we can call the results mixed. The majority of the time, Ernesto recalls little of his former life, and obeys unfailingly. His mind seems susceptible, however, to strong sensory memories from his early childhood. Thus far, we've learned to avoid the smell or taste of *Pulque*, or of rising dough. It's not the full success I would have hoped, but it's enough to proceed with the augmentations."
>
> **12th of Avandra, 1129**
>
> "The augmentations proceed splendidly, though we are slowed still by occasional moments of lucidity in the lad. Still, we've nearly doubled his already prolific strength, and succeeded in silencing the incessant chatter the boy was once fond of. I fear I have taken the augmentations to their limit, however. But The Dividend tells me that our benefactors has returned at last, and new opportunities may soon present."
>
> **25th of Avandra, 1129**
>
> "Yes, I can comfortably call our work a success. It could be replicated, albeit at significant cost. But I remain convinced that it could be so much more, if only I could get my hands on those constructs from up North. Lord Sergente and The Dividend have been pleased. Pleased enough to try to source one of these for me. Now that, truly, would revolutionize labor!"
>
> **13th of Melora, 1129**
>
> "Not one, but FOUR of these northerner contraptions! I can scarcely believe it. The Divided tells me they will arrive in three days hence. Truly I must admit my benefactors to be both generous and motivated. But then, how can one argue with results? Ernesto is nearly fully recovered and operational. The possibilities are tantalizing."

The notes revealed that the **Sergente family**, the landowners who run Salvation District, were financing Sprocket’s work. Sprocket’s aim was to augment and control human laborers using bionic enhancements—creating hybrid labor units that combined the resilience of machines with the pliability of people. All at the financial backing of the Sergente Family...which in turn were backed by the House of Coinwrights.

Worse still, our heroes found reference to a previous attempt in an underground district called the Triticum District. It seemed that Operation Triticum had failed also, but had served as a proof of concept. The Sergente Family had been instructed to follow the blueprint here. The more our heroes read, the more the timeline became apparent. The Triticum District had been where Dante had been captured and Hanged for fomenting discontent. The timing lined up with the disappearance of Haji from the head of the House of Coinwrights, and the activity here in Salvation coincided with the return of Haji by the Hushed Choir.

The i mplications were simple—follow the money and all roads lead to Haji, the Rakshasa.

But worse still, Thorn found a draft contract—a prototype for what could only be described as a reverse mortgage for bodies. It laid out a scheme for workers to sign away posthumous rights to their own corpses, in exchange for small advances in life. The author? None other than **Dr. Bilver Shadowgift**, Quinn’s academic nemesis from the School of Necromancy, last known to have taken a long fall out of a high window.

We join our heroes with four rescued RELICs, one unconscious mercenary, and a traumatized boy in a monstrous body. What they do next—and how they’ll get out of here—is anyone’s guess.
