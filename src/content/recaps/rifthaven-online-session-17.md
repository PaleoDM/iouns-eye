We rejoined our heroes midair, egg in hand and desperately racing back to the Harrowside Docks to win the Roc Run qualifier. They charted a path around the Shucked Shell and made for a loot balloon, recovering a potion of healing and a potion of pugilism. From there, Ty threaded a perilous course through the Grasping Glade, only for the *Brass Wing* to be beset by cannon-like volleys of vine, twig, and needle blights. A massive tree blight leapt onto the deck while an enormous Bulthias Blight pounded them from the forest floor. The ship took heavy damage, and things looked grim.

Thorn delivered an inspired solution: using his Cap of the Mycelium, he invoked the Gift of Gab and negotiated with the tree. The tree recognized Thorn as kin—“where Root and Stone once embraced”—and agreed to a deal. Another team, the *Breath of the Morn*, was lying in wait, planning an ambush. The tree proposed that our heroes play dead, drawing the ambushers in to be devoured instead. Thorn agreed, and when the *Breath of the Morn* descended, the ambush was sprung. The trees annihilated the rival ship, and the *Brass Wing* escaped. One particularly tenacious pirate made it aboard, but our heroes subdued her and lashed her to the prow as they returned to port.

At the Harrowside Docks, the Bastions were hailed as victors. Their successful return with not one but two unfertilized roc eggs earned them gold, glory, and admiration. Captain Vex thanked Arden for his leadership, and the two shared a warm (if awkward) moment. Arden quipped that someone should tell his mother, to which Vex dryly suggested it might mean more coming from him.

At the Victory Banquet, the head of the House of Coinwrights made an impassioned congratulations speech to the victors, for they are the primary benefactors of the event. In doing so, our heroes learned that this person is none other than the rakshasa Haji. Draped in shining skin and signature chain piercings, he delivered a subtle warning to our heroes—reminding them that while they keep winning, the banks never lose. He advised them to be careful where they poke their noses.

Elsewhere at the banquet:

-   **Veil** encountered two tinkers, Cog and Kale, who expressed strong interest in returning to the Sparkworks now that it was stable. She invited them to survey the district and discuss opportunities.
-   **Arden** told Captain Vex that yes, the Bastions were going legit. She agreed to put in a good word with the shipwright and vessel guilds.
-   **Thorn** met a Firbolg woman named Boreal, wearing the same pin as the stolen crystal. She gently probed Thorn’s interest in the Earthen Loam. Thorn was noncommittal, but Boreal left the door open—and warned him to destroy the scrying crystal if he valued his privacy.
-   **Quin**, attempting to probe academic interest in memory magic, was promptly ignored—until someone recognized him not as Quin Crux but as Memo, the great merchant. A dramatic woman in a ludicrous hat clung to his arm and shared a tip: if he wanted to pursue the roots of memory work, he should seek out the “other expert” from before Dr. Solvane’s rise to power. A rural witch-doctor living in the upper districts, known only as **Nanny Pu'Pu**.

We leave our heroes basking in victory—wealthier, wearier, and waiting to hear what comes next from Dante.
