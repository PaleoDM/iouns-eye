We joined our heroes taking their last long rest before the final cultist show down. And found Ty experiencing something...altogether different.

> Ty. You retreat into your meditations. And experience something altogether quite new. 
>
> You're standing on the rocky beaches of Lake Umbrathir. But it's not quite right. There's no lights, no people. No docks. In fact, there's no Tyr'Zareth at all.
>
> This is not your body. But it is? But it's not. At least, not the one you currently wear. 
>
> The face perhaps a touch softer. More feminine? But it suits you no less. It feels right even. Like an old cloak you once loved but became to threadbare to repair.
>
> You touch your reflection in the water. It ripples...and shimmers. Water no more.
>
> It is memory. Folded across time. Moonlight scattered across the surface.
>
> And then you stand at the bow of a beautiful silver vessel. Carved from coral and mother of pearl. Above you, the sails whisper the sweet promises of an aspiring lover to the wind, which dances ever elusive, between the masts.
>
> Behind you, the caverns are chocked with the thick smoke of burning web. But ahead lies only the open waters of Lake Umbrathir. A blank canvas of endless possibility. And above, the low thrum of the fourth moon kisses your skin for the first time. Its gentle light reflecting on the lake—a mirror too vast for even the gods to crack.
>
> Oh my. But I have slept so very long..."
>
> Beside you, a woman of unimaginable beauty. Her skin the color of black pearl, interspersed with iridescent purple and pink freckles. And her eyes...galaxies of infinite depth. She smiles with just a tint of melancholy as her gaze falls upon you.
>
> "Ah, yes, I see now. So have you, my sweet champion. We'll have to awaken together. And without the luxury of time, I'm afraid."

Following this strange meditation, Ty sought out the advice of Arden, inquiring whether Arden had ever felt "as if he didn't know his true self?" After some gentle ribbing and poking/prodding, Arden came to understand that no one had ever had "the talk" with Ty. No, not that one, the elven one.

Arden explained that elves, full blooded elves, typically reincarnate, living new lives from old souls, and that during their maturation they begin experiencing memories of their former lives to help them solidify who they were, and who they may be now. No one ever bothered to tell Ty because, well, Ty's father is human...

This was most certainly NOT the right time to tackle that possible revelation. So they shelved it and set about preparing for battle. Our heroes briefly exchanged words with the innkeepers from session 2—the Stoutwaters—and the young lad Pip took Thorn to task for having been "deceitful" in his advice. Thorn did his best to present agnostic advice that wouldn't set the lad down too violent a path, but emotion and anger bubbled below the surface nonetheless.

And just like that, our party was making their way across the newly grown fungal bridges. They paid the toll to Rhyme and Reason, the two fungal guards, in the form of a book—"for what better way than to cast ones consciousness so wide?"

They briefly tried to stealth, but it was short lived as they arrived at the Cultists' barrack using Dante's map. Thankfully, the distraction set by the townsfolk, combined with their highly successful combat the night before, meant fewer cultists to fight their way through.

One cultist at a time, they mowed their way through the barracks, with most of the heavy hitters having already been felled. In the end, three newest recruits surrendered, casting off their masks and binding themselves. It became clear that they were newer, and not yet so fervent in their worship.

From their, our party turned east, aiming to rescue prisoners and potential sacrifices from their holding cells. They quickly ran into opposition and, in the fray, Ty tried to charm one of them into attacking the other, only to realize that he'd missed the code word and accidentally charmed Dante. The ensuing fight was a tad messy, but they cleaned up well and soon had everything under control. They freed as many prisoners as they could, instructing them to flee back across the fungal bridges, and steeled themselves for the summoning chamber.

Inside, they found nearly a dozen cultists, each clinging to a soon-to-be sacrificial commoner, up on daises with channels meant to funnel blood into a central pool. The mages and the priests frantically called out their magic and their spells, hoping to hasten the ritual, while our heroes moved in to do their best to thwart things.

We join our heroes having just rolled initiative as we prepare to see just what they can do to stop this ritual...
