We joined our heroes having just found respite with a set of preteens, in the Shadowfell, specifically the domain of dread known as Odaire. A young lady of about 14, who seemed to be known only as "The Birthday Host", welcomed Ty and Sir Sunder and their "poppet companions" (for the rest were stll under a glamour).

After some back and forth with the kids, our heroes were able to piece together some important information:

-   These kids seem to have originated from all over, but to a one they had a troubled past and seemed to have a theme of missing parents. Most of them were orphans or urchins or similar.
-   They all awaken in Odaire in "The Orphanage" under the oppressive watch of The Warden.
-   Typically, they age out and wander around in Odaire, growing older *only as their own perception of their maturity changes*.
-   Somewhere nearly adulthood, the poppets eventually round them up for some type of grim harvest.

From this back and forth, Veil was able to put together that the children have their hearts harvested in order to power these new poppet assassins. And anyone over about 16 seems to be a prime candidate. While our heroes talked, a pronouncement from the Playhouse came from Maligno himself, the terrible boy poppet who rules this land. Word of our heroes had reached him, and Maligno was willing to offer 5 more years of youth to anyone who would turn them in. The Birthday Host insisted none of them would snitch.

Realizing they needed out of here quickly, our heroes made a beeline for the gardens, where rumor has it they could engage with the Faerie Godmother. A crucial Pass Without a Trace and some insanely high stealth rolls got them across town safely, and along the way they saw massive and grim poppets, powered by the hearts of multiple youths. Thankfully, they went unseen.

In the garden, Veil and Ty left and offering and drew forth the Faerie Godmother—Lulu's Auntie. From her, our heroes learned some important information:

-   Travel onward, towards the Pallbearer's Domain, would be easy enough. Maligno would have some item that would be attuned to that plane, and with it Sir Sunder would be able to traverse the mists in that direction.
-   Travel back to Shadow Rifthaven would be a bit tougher—the only item that could do it would be a compass, kept by Guissepe the Toymaker.
-   Getting to Guiseppe is hard enough, but to be seen as an ally by him, they would need to carry a trinket from his past—a wind up music box, kept by the Warden at the orphanage. Our heroes didn't need telling twice.

After parting ways with the Faerie, our heroes went straight fro the orphanage to beat up the warden. And boy howdy, did they. The Warden nearly dealt Ty a mighty blow, but he made the save and it was all downhill from there. Thorn locked down three enormous threats all by himself, while Ty, Veil, and Leon took out the major bosses. Freddie provided critical support via Bless and a Turn Undead, while Sir Sunder supported our heroes at the periphery.

We join our heroes having just acquired the music box, but hearing another proclamation from Maligno. In an effort to draw out our heroes, they would be rounding up a few young ones and forcing them into a game of "cops and robbers"—a grim game where two teams are given crossbows and forced to "play" until one side has one. Our heroes need to move quickly to engage Guissepe, acquire the compass, and decide if and how they're going to engage Maligno.
