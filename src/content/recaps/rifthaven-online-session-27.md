We joined our heroes as they departed Nanny Pupu's strange hut. Once outside, old Nanny allowed Arden to take the bag off his head, quipping that she was due at least one small souvenir to remember him by. Then, she thanked Ty for the way the lad had appreciated her skeletal art—few really understood her gift, she said.

As our heroes made their way down to the main entrance to these caverns, Ty and Lulu discussed the old witch. Lulu was concerned. She seemed a scary old witch, and after all, she'd said that she *ate* those Silverweave legionaires! Ty quipped back that times were hard, and food scarce in what remained of Boughshadow. He wouldn't judge. Lulu reflected on her privilege as someone who literally cannot face hunger...

As they reached the entrance to the cavern, our heroes hungered for more information as to what they might find inside. Quin set down to divine the mathematics of the space, and learned quite a bit: within the caverns they would find several scores of legionaires, and numerous kobold and kodold adjacent creatures. But the true horror was in the never ending sight of the eyes. Eyes everywhere, and all about them every monster tainted and stained by the eyes. Veil's suspicions only hardened—she expects a beholder within.

As our heroes finished their divination, the guardian of the caverns came forth to challenge them. A gigantic, ape-like creature with the disposition and countenance of a hyena bellowed down to them. Frighteningly, it spoke common, and cast spells. Soon, it was joined by several small ones, and two tentacles eye creatures. Suddenly, our heroes fought for their lives.

And it was a terrible battle indeed. Veil and Thorn split off immediately, each killing a minion clean before they even took a turn. But the massive creature leapt down from the cavern cliffs and tore Quin asunder. Before Quin could even react, he was already down. And it was then that our heroes learned that their foe had a terrible poison that prevented healing. Arden strode forward and used his Lay on Hands to cleanse the poison so that Ty could heal Quin. Meanwhile, Veil and Thorn went to work.

The resulting battle was hard fought. The two eyeballed creatures kept slowing, stunning, and otherwise debilitating our heroes. One of them nearly brought down Ty with a wicked bite and a series of tentacles. Veil succeeded in stunning one of the beholder-kin, while Ty landed a much needed psychic lance on the Worg-lord to stunned him for a turn.

Finally able to catch their breath, Thorn and Veil formed up a proper front line once more and went to work. Things were still looking dire until Quin landed a well timed Polymorph—turning the Dire Worg into a seal pup and buying them a round in which to finish off the beholder-kin. Eventually Ty, brimming with confidence, surged forth and plunged the killing rapier deep into the creature's chest.

With all still, our heroes settled down to rest before venturing into the perilous dungeon before them. They found their dreams and meditations assaulted by a horrible, malignant entity of eyes and nightmares. Ty found himself living once more through a former life—a sea elf clinging to a raft while Lulu the Pelican fought vainly to keep them on coarse before being eaten by a massive, many-eyed shark. And we saw Veil witnessing the departure of a young boy from her family home, and saw as she struggled with in the darkness with an entity that sought to consume her. Just in time, a stuffed animal Lulu surged back into frame to defend the young Veil.

Our heroes awoke shaken, but technically rested. They didn't miss the message, however. Rests would be at a premium inside this dungeon. Steeling themselves, our party entered into the caverns, and witnessed the slaughtering ground of a handful of creatures—monster and kobold alike. As they sheltered beneath the shed carapace of a massive insectoid creature, Veil found a slate tablet with a bit of important lore:

> I was nervous at my first meeting of the Council, but Elder Thandrel gave me a nod of welcome, and that steadied me. I was determined not to let my youth dishonor their faith in elevating me.

As they were reading it, however, a war bugle sounded, and our heroes found themselves between two angry parties. On their left, a party of angry kobolds descending on them with spears. On their right, chitinous monsters gnashing their beaks and talons and chanting in Deep Speech. Each side clearly believing our heroes to be reinforcements for the other. What ever will they do?
