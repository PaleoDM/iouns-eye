We opened with our heroes heading to High Haven Arcane Academy. The plan was simple: Ty would pose as a prospective student, and the rest of the party would tag along as his entourage. It worked. They got the full campus tour.

The school of necromancy, it turns out, is doing well for itself. Banners and pamphlets lined the halls making the civic case for the dark arts. Healing should be considered necromancy. The dead can serve the living. That sort of thing. It was polished, professional, and just a little unsettling. Our heroes nodded along and kept their eyes open.

They broke away from the tour and found Professor Serrano, an old gnome and emeritus professor with a reputation. Serrano had pioneered something called "ghoul extraction," a procedure that sounded exactly as uncomfortable as the name suggests. He was friendly enough, and he took them to the person they'd actually come to see: Gideon.

The reunion between Veil and her brother was not the tearful embrace you'd expect. Gideon is young. Promoted well past his readiness, clearly a talented academic who'd been handed a department chair and was still figuring out where the armrests were. He didn't know Veil was alive. He didn't know their parents were alive. The information hit him in waves, and he processed it awkwardly, with too many questions and not enough of the right ones. Veil was left wondering if he might even have had his memory modified.

Our heroes gave them what space they could, then got to work. They reintroduced Gideon to Quin. Slowly, carefully, the shape of the Aletheia procedure started to come apart in front of him. Gideon listened. He asked questions. He got suspicious. And when the suspicion settled, he didn't immediately agree to help. Instead, he suggested Quin and Bilver Shadowgift hash it out directly. Scientist to scientist. Let the evidence speak.

The problem, of course, was that Bilver was dead. But Gideon explained that Bilver was "still pupating." His word. The man's soul was partially reconstituted in a chrysalis downstairs. Not alive, not dead. Somewhere in between.

They went down. The chrysalis was a tank, something between a laboratory fixture and a coffin, with what remained of Bilver Shadowgift suspended inside. The two former colleagues argued. Not about the ethics of soul harvesting. Not about the lives ruined or the system corrupted. About grant funding. About who was the better scientist. Academics to the end.

Then Bilver snapped. The creature broke free of the tank and let loose a howl that hit several of our heroes hard. But Veil was already moving. She locked the thing in a grapple before it could do real damage. Freddie stepped in with the antimagic field from the spell scroll they'd found last session and shut down the worst of what Bilver could do.

From there it was clean work. Veil and Thorn kept Bilver pinned and finished him. Ty dropped a massive Chromatic Orb into the surrounding mooks and Leon helped mop up the rest. It was over quickly.

In the aftermath, Veil gathered everything she could find. The plans laid out in Bilver's notes painted a picture that was worse than the soul trade alone. A full agricultural revolution built on undead labor. "Reverse mortgages for your bodies," where citizens lease their corpses after death in exchange for small sums now to clear their debts. The living, selling their future dead selves to pay for the privilege of staying alive a little longer. Sinister doesn't begin to cover it.

On the way out, Veil clocked Serrano watching them. The friendly old gnome who'd walked them in had been keeping tabs the whole time. They put the fear of Thorn in him and learned he'd been spying for the Cassini Brothers. Thorn, never one for subtlety, used his axe to dispel the abjuration ward on the doorway out, and our heroes were gone. Gideon came with them.

Gideon invoked the vote of no confidence in the Aletheia procedure. One institution down. One to go. We leave our heroes heading for the Order of the Ju Shi, where Sir Sunder will need to get past Divine Judge Vine Ashen Bloom to finish what they started.
