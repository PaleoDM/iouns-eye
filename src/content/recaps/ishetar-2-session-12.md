We rejoined our heroes in the cellar of the Temple of the Restful Lily, the old shrine to the Moonweaver and the Dawnfather. Having just defeated and restored two cursed figures—a medusa and a lamia—they discovered these were the original founders of the temple, the elven sisters **Sylvari** and **Solara**. Exhausted and shaken, the two thanked the party for freeing them after Lyra and Thump destroyed the enchanted painting that had bound them.

The sisters told their story: once they had built this place as a sanctuary for weary travelers. But in time, three other sisters arrived, slowly corrupting the temple’s purpose. Twisted by vanity and self-doubt, Sylvari and Solara had been trapped within, with consecrations laid around the grounds to ensure they could not leave.

Our heroes compared this account with what they had already learned from the awakened tree **Ambrosia** and the water nymph **Cyrena**, and concluded that the true villains were the three “sisters” they had met earlier: **Greensong**, **Azirsa**, and **Morgana**. They had already crossed paths with Greensong and her dog Morty, and with Azirsa in the gardens, but not yet with Morgana.

A plan was devised. Thump and Lyra remained in the shrine to watch over Sombra, who had begun showing troubling signs of vanity after sitting for a portrait with Morgana. Wracked with insecurity and jealousy toward Lyra, Sombra was put to work gathering the shattered remains of **Th0l’s Peregrine Society companion**, once petrified and broken here. Meanwhile, Azrael investigated further, discovering that Th0l himself had been an agent of the Peregrine Society, sent to confront threats such as this one before falling victim to the house’s magic.

Elsewhere, Vicas distracted Azirssa by feigning interest in her philosophies and even hinting at sitting for a portrait, while Rooker engaged Morgana directly. On a long walk, he carefully prodded her loyalty to her sisters. Through persuasion and manipulation, he convinced Morgana to agree that, should a battle break out, she would hold back and perhaps betray her sisters if she stood to gain from it.

When the moment came, however, subterfuge gave way to open conflict. Just as Vicas pretended to sit for his portrait, he broke the ruse and combat erupted. Morty revealed his true nature as a hellhound, a gargoyle was unleashed, and the sisters struck with their magic. The fight was brutal. Lyra fell unconscious at least once, Vicas was nearly slain, and several others were driven to single digits. Rooker’s **Hunger of Hadar** locked down much of the enemy’s spellcasting, while Thump’s **Moonbeam** and a critical **Lightning Bolt** dealt heavy damage from cover. Azrael pressed the attack, while Vicas grabbed an enemy and flew them into the air with his dragon wings before plummeting them down to the ground. Finally, Lyra rallied to deliver the final blows—incinerating the last gargoyle and hag with **Burning Hands**.

Yet the danger is not over. Morgana remains on the field, invisible but wounded, her true intentions uncertain. The hellhound still fights, and many of the enchanted portraits continue inflicting psychic torment on the temple’s guests. Sylvari, Solara, Ambrosia, and Sirena await resolution—but for now, our heroes stand bloodied and wary, uncertain if they have gained the upper hand or merely survived the first round.
