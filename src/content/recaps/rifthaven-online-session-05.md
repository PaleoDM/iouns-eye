We joined our heroes in the middle of their dungeon crawl, having uncovered the primary egress for their smuggled goods, but hoping still to find a conveninet way IN that didn't involve going through the temple to the AllHammer. As we joined our heroes, they found themselves in a large cross-roads of some kind, and were staring down at the corpses of half a dozen, very dead, Gibbering Mouthers.

They set about investigating the area, ensuring that they were safe, and hoping they could discern if there was something **even more** dangerous down here. They eventually concluded that whatever had killed these creatures was no predator—the bodies all born acidic burn marks from some corrosive chemical, but no bites or other signs of predation.

Eventually, our heroes decided to explore the northern passage, and quickly saw signs that it led to the noble's district, and likely to Lord Harwin's cellar, where they new there was an entrance. However, they quickly found this route blocked by a cave in of sorts. Ty investigated and found residue from black powder, leading them all to conclude that it had been collapsed on purpose. Ty speculated that it was to keep monsters out, but others wondered if it was to keep something IN. At the foot of the rubble, Ty discovered a set of small, cylindrical, porous white stones, which Thorn identified as the bones of a human hand. Our heroes were suitably curious, but decided they didn't need to bust the barricade down until they'd investigated the other directions and ensured that nothing would sneak up on them,

When they returned to the crossroads, they found a most unsettling sight waiting for them. A scarecrow, or some similarly aberrant poppet. It wore the tabbard and mask of a Hushed Choir cultist, but hung unnaturally from its shoulders, with the limbs dangling about at unnatural angles. There was no lower body. And the voice...was the stuff of nightmares. It spoke of a "great poppa" and of having been awoken recently and of enjoying "playtime with the cultists". Our heroes didn't know what to make of it, but they ll agreed on the course of action—burn it with fire.

The poppet led them on a short chase down the corridor, and soon a fight broke out with other abberant creatures—two nothics with a giant hideous eye and a crab monster called a chuul. The fight went better than several of the previous ones, thankfully, though it was not without some tension. Arden did his best to provide support while Thorn held the front line, but Veil took a few heavy attacks and Ty was ultimately downed as well. Even Quin found himself on his last legs, but they were able to bring the creatures down and get Arden to heal everyone.

As our heroes caught their breath and pressed on, they saw the horrible poppet once more. Ty rushed forward and hit it with Faerie Fire, revealing that it was merely a construct, being literally poppeted by a horrible puppeteer controlling it with a thin, nearly invisible, silvery thread. Upon being revealed, the creature chided them all, telling it would need to recover but would eventually come back for revenge. Their boon, for evicting this foul entity, would be that it would cease its own harassment of the cultists. A sacrifice our heroes were willing to make in exchange for not having to worry about this thing for now.

With the poppet master banished, our heroes began to explore. They came to understand that there was a large, underground chasm that bifurcated the district's under ground, and that for better or worse, they were on opposite sides from the cultist. They actually over heard two cultists discussing that they had made it in through the tenements and had tunnel access, but that they could not breach the tunnels which our heroes controlled without finding a second entrance, or else finding a way across the chasm.

As our party continued exploring, they located the true smuggling entrance—a long tunnel that would lead to the Great Rift. And with this, they began to piece things together. The tunnels' original purpose was likely an evacuation route, an egress for nobles to flee in times of violence. Unfortunately, the Rift access to the tunnels was also blocked by the chasm. Thorn investigated what remained of the bridges and found them reminiscent of gardening trellises, and realized that the bridges weren't something built of stone or wood, but were grown of plant life. The bridges were likely part of the fugal security system down here.

Convenient, because as they turned to keep investigating, our heroes found themselves staring at 2 fungal creatures and their sproutling. The one introduced itself as Spear, and the other as Acid. Their capacity to communicate with our heroes was limited but they ushered our party to the one they call Speak—a larger headed mushroom person with a greater gift for speech.

From the myconids, our heroes learned that they had been cut off from their Great Self—the primary core of the fungal colony that gives each member its place in the larger whole. Ty communed with the little creatures, and saw it ALL. He saw the profound smallness of being part of something larger than the individual, but also the horrible loneliness that goes with it. He saw the great aberrations come to the tunnel and wept as the Great Self made the decision to cut off parts of themselves into isolation, to salvage what they could of the colony.

And from it all, our heroes learned that the colony, when it was whole and inter-connected, had a deal with the folks of the district. In exchange for protection, it would run the lights and the alarms and even the bridges. Resolving to make things right, our heroes decided to see if they could save the Great Self and possible restore the old ways. But first, they'll need to find a way across the chasm,

We join our heroes taking a well deserved long rest, on a field of mossy beds in the mushroom forest.
