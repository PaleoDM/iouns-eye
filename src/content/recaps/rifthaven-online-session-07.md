We picked right up in the middle of the action, with our heroes fighting valiantly to rescue The Great Self from its blighted alter ego. At the top of the round, a few twig blights jointed the fray, turning the action economy suddenly against our party. Thankfully, Veil has a knack for killing many, weaker enemies, and she began to clean up. Things became momentarily dicey when a few of our heroes tried to use fire damage, but true emergency was averted.

Once the enemy had been defeated, our party was able to commune with the Great Self properly. They soon realized that their efforts had born fruit, as the individual members of the colony began to regain some semblance of individuality once they were properly connected to their hive mind. Through some careful negotiating, our party was able to re-establish the old truce—the colony would maintain the lights, alarms, and the bridges, in exchange for protection from underdark threats.

They called on one of the party to step forward as ambassador, and the party elected Thorn. He was gifted a Cap of the Mycelium, which gives him a new proficiency and allows him to speak with Eukaryotes.

The Great Self also bestowed upon Thorn a vision, delivered to them via the vast network of roots that connect all. Thorn saw a sharp dressed man in a crisp military uniform with a familiar insignia: that of the Silverweave Ventures. The man was deep below Rifthaven, delivering what looked like a cutting or a sappling from an old and great tree, to some wizened, cowl-faced cultist in an ancient temple. Though the Great Self did not know the importance or the context of the vision, they know Thorn would like to know. They also overheard a single command—to find more of these saplings to help power a profane ritual of some sort.

Our heroes also learned that the Great Self, like nearly all rooted life in this district, would be fundamentally limited by the lack of nutrients in the soil. They explained that in the old days, they existed in a symbiosis with the Sparkplugs, who produce a honey-like nectar or jam that would infuse the soil with nutrients. Apparently, in the old days, they were so common that the soil always ran rich, but since their departure, things have been difficult to grow.

This prompted Thorn to want to speak with one, and boy was it a trip. Using his new Gift of Gab, Thorn learned that the Sparkplug, named Willie (because all names must end in -illie), loves to manipulate moving parts. In fact, they all do. They love machinery most of all—levers or wheels or cogs or planes or ball bearings or really anything they can manipulate. It seems that the conversion of potential to kinetic energy helps them produce their jam, and they LOVE it.

Armed with this information, our party returned to the abandoned and broken cistern over the well. Arden, realizing that he had tinker tools, set about repairing it, and worked wonders! In short order he had repaired the mechanisms, and suddenly Sparkplugs were all over the machinery! Our heroes don't know how much it will help or how localized the effect may be, but they felt good about restoring some of the old order.

From there, our players fell back to the mushroom caverns and took another long rest. They awakened to a new day-night cycle controlled by the Great Self here in the caverns, and set about to clear the final leg of their tunnel—the northern passage blocked by a cave in.

Once Thorn had cleared it, they immediately were welcomed by the strength of rot and decay, mixed with black powder. Our heroes were proceeding with caution when they were assaulted by a skeletal Minotaur. Further investigation revealed strange effigies (perhaps once people?) in rich, elaborate robes with fancy jewelry. Their skeletal jaws hung agape and dark energy poured out into the caverns, empowering the numerous skeletons now harassing our heroes.

In the ensuing battle, Ty was brought perilously close to death by a Will-o'-Wisp, as Thorn did his best to keep hacking away at skeletons and destroying effigies. He quickly dispatched the skeleton and barreled forward, finding a Flaming Skeleton with a flaming maul, and made quick work of that as well. Veil and Quin hung back to save Ty, Quin finishing off the Will-o'-Wisp with a well placed magic missile. Then Veil used her crazy movement speed to fly forward and support Thorn and Arden.

We join our heroes on the 18th of Raei, staring down an invisible Will-o'-Wisp and another flaming skeleton. All around them, strange loot to explore and ahead of them, the cellars of Lord Harwin's manor? If they only survive that long!
