We rejoined our heroes at the Goldwarren encampment under Foreman **Uzoma**, finishing their conversation with **Vero**, the anxious accountant whose stolen ledger had revealed weeks of smuggled mercury and a growing conspiracy within Ember’s Post. Though the party now had leads stretching toward the palace, the temples, and the Majilis, they resolved to prioritize the Duchess’s charge first: clear the mine and rescue the trapped workers before further collapses or saboteurs could claim more lives.

Despite the strain of a long day, the group coordinated with Uzoma and descended into the debris-clogged tunnels. Thump proposed accelerating the excavation rather than waiting overnight. With the Foreman’s blessing, he unleashed a devastating Fireball to blast apart the unstable stone. Thanks to Uzoma’s preparations, the structure held, and soon a narrow passage opened—just wide enough for most of the party to squeeze through. A bit of creative maneuvering even allowed Lyra’s plated armor through. Moments before entering, Lyra spotted a golden-scaled, many-legged creature darting deeper into the dark. With miners’ lives at stake, the party pressed on.

Inside, they discovered a crossroads—one path blocked by a secondary collapse, two others leading north and south. From the southern passage came a faint dragging or thumping, and they were preparing to investigate when they were ambushed by the golden creatures Lyra had glimpsed. These ferret-like beasts moved with impossible speed. When Azrael felled the first, it burst into raw magic, leaving no body behind. The sight raised uncomfortable questions about what exactly they were facing.

As the battle unfolded, Lyra’s blast of light flushed out a second threat: an **earth elemental knight** preparing to strike from ambush. With that, the fight was fully engaged. The spinning design of the crossroads added a layer of chaos—periodically rotating the entire chamber and pitching combatants to the floor. Lyra seemed to fall prone every cycle, but always stood again in time for her next turn. The battle was more comedic than threatening, and before long the party stood victorious with minimal losses.

A moment of reflection brought an important realization. These creatures were **Auromvaxes**, a species believed extinct for generations. Once common around mining settlements, they devoured precious and heavy metals—gold for the golden strain, silver for the silver, and so on. Their insatiable diets had led to extermination centuries earlier. Why they were here, beneath Ember’s Post, was a mystery the group could not yet answer.

They moved on. Ahead, Vicas took point to scout the remaining passage, but a poor perception check and an unfortunate Constitution save caused him to walk directly into a pocket of toxic gas. He collapsed instantly. Fortunately, Azrael and Lyra heard his body hit stone and rushed to help.

A lone canary in a cage—set as warning—confirmed the danger. The party then spent several minutes debating how to retrieve Vicas without exposing themselves. In the end, they devised a clever plan: tie a rope into a loop, use Mage Hand to slip it around Vicas’s ankle, and drag him to safety from a distance. The knot held. Thump applied *Lesser Restoration*, and Vicas soon regained consciousness.

With the immediate threat neutralized, Lyra began ritually casting *Detect Poison*, while Rooker prepared *Detect Magic*. For the first time, the group witnessed their combined rituals under the new circle-casting rules. Lyra’s radiant sun-magic and Rooker’s profane, maggot-tainted arcana repelled one another like oil and water. For a moment they seemed ready to unravel—but the combined will of the casters forced them together. The result was potent.

Lyra illuminated every pocket of toxic spores ahead, guiding the party safely through the treacherous tunnels. Rooker, meanwhile, perceived magical afterimages—echoes of a **half-orc woman** moving through the same passage not long before, planting the mushroom clusters that released the debilitating fumes. The description matched only one person they knew: **Vicar Xona**, the healer tending to Duchess Lyla.

At last they reached the final cavern. Echoing from within came an unfamiliar voice—calm, irritated, and speaking of prisoners. “I only need one to lure them down here,” it said. Whoever it was, they had expected the heroes’ arrival. Low on resources but armed with new suspicions, our heroes readied themselves to charge the chamber and confront whatever waited within. That is where we ended.
