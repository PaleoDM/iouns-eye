We joined our heroes the day after their major battle, having narrowly survived, and still reeling from the effects of bluerot and several near death experiences. We opened with a serious yet uplifting scene of our heroes having dragged the crystalline statue of Janore up to the highest hill atop Firewatch Island. Lyra left a Continual Flame spell perpetually cast within his lantern, and Janore now serves as the island's lighthouse.

Then, we were treated to a rotating whirlwind of player scenes while our heroes did our best to lightly rest without sitting too idle in the abandoned fortress. Rowena explained to our heroes how this latest turn of events put them in a strange position. FIrewatch island is of critical military importance: it is well positioned to serve as the early warning sign for navel vessels moving east towards Ekkorai. And, with it already having a well built, defensible fortress, it's the first line of defense. The political elite in Emberspost had been hesitant to fully side with Ishetar rather than any one Prince back in Aroiinae. Rowena had secured their alliance by promising joint cooperation in maintaining and operating the Forst on Firewatch Island.

And so, they had come to an agreement. The folks of Emberspost, who cannot grow crops in a desert, would found a series of fishing villages in the Seraph's Pass. And the fortress itself would be deeded to Ishetar, to be maintained by the Prior and the Monks, but funded and supported by the people of Ishetar. That was, until the Prior was killed and the monks all but exterminated. Lady Rowena proposed the most logical solution: "I'd make you all Barons, and deed you the fortress, it's rights, and responsibilities."

Our heroes were...not immediately amenable. And for good reason. Lyra, introspective and insightful as always, intuited many of the political challenges this would bring. Vicas was unwilling to commit to long term change, citing his own advanced age. Azrael queried "do we have to live here?" and voiced concerns about how far South it was. But slowly, the reality of the situation began to take place. Should our heroes decline, they would be placing the fortress in the hands of Emberspost nobles, and weaking the claim of a united Ekkorai community. Nothing was overtly decided, and no documents were signed yet, but Lyra, at least, felt she owed Janore something for his sacrifice.

During this conversation, Archais, the resurrected Elven mage, came and spoke to Vicas. Archais explained that, in the week ebfore the attack, Firewatch Island had hosted a small fishing vessel making a speed run up the Ekkorai coastline, flying Aroiin colors, despite there being supposedly no more ships coming east. Archais had drunk with the first mate deep into the night, and pieced together that these were merchants delivering orders from the Guildsmen of the capital, Aroinnae, to the Timbermaster at the Turquoise Timber Trust. The message wasn't quite so overt, but Archais was good at reading between the lines—use their money and equipment to squeeze Ishetar and force Rowena to give herself up as hostage. After all, she'd command a pretty copper for ransom.

Vicas brought this news to the group and, after much discussion, they agreed that this was a loose thread that would need dealt with, and soon. They all felt that the presence of a well funded capital trust loyal to the Empire within what they now considered to be sovereign territory undermined their claims of unification, and would hinder free trade between Emberspost and Ishetar. They added it to the list.

As our heroes were spinning out on possible outcomes, they were interrupted by a beautiful tragically spectral woman floating down into their midst through the celing: the entire rom dropping 15-20 degrees in temperature. The apparition, turned and addressed Lyra pointedly, claiming that she brought a message from the fisherman Lyra had interrogated the night before, whose soul she now escorted through the Gate. The fey/undead creature, seemingly a banshee, brought the message freely, and told Lyra that the fisherman had learned a key detail he had forgotten to mention. "The pirate admire Syrgaul has struck a deal with High Prince Brof of Prusca—a right to rule across all of Seraph's pass in exchange for an undead army to march onto land against his brothers.

Our heroes sat with the grim news: no actionable items therein but no less troubling. Lyra, noticing that the specter lingered, offered the young woman a cup of tea, and a couple minutes of pleasant company. Unaccustomed to such manners, the banshee was touched at a gift so freely given. Amidst the conversation, Vicas asked if all souls are escorted such, and the banshee explained that though she does not see them all beyond personally, all are escorted by either her or her siblings. "Do you know a woman named Shallah?" whispered Vicas. "No," answered the banshee. "For a single journey is hardly sufficient time to truly no any of the mortals I escort. But I did escort her."

And with that, she banshee kissed Vicas on the forehead and he was treated to a vision for him and him alone: a great hall of food and drink a plenty with the fiercest and bravest warriors in all the land, clad in irons and furs aplenty. At the head, a great man of all beard, or all storm cloud? Or all beard... Someone loudly called if any would speak for the new warrior, and a bard sang out how the woman had died bravely in battle with the most fearsome of warriors—her own body. The soldiers all toasted her glorious and honorable death, and Shallah took her seat at the table. When it was over, Vicas asked for a drink, and promised to share it all with everyone soon.

But before he could, they needed to deal with Rooekr, who was getting worse by the minute. Lyra and Thump prepared a combined ritual—Lyra using Gentle Repose to stave of "decay" while Thump super charged Lesser Restoration. There was a violent battle happening within Rooker, and the eldritch being that clung to his soul chided the man for "the flesh being too weak to serve". As Thump powered the ritual, he came to understand the nature of this eldritch being: an entity that would move from world to world, consuming magic and divinity in its wake, unable to enter the world proper and thus cultivating hosts to birth him into it naturally. Rooker was the host, harboring the parasitic maggots within until they managed to eat their way out.

As Thump and Lyra, with Vicas's help via control water, extracted the disease, the maggots spilled out from Rooker and aimed to consume him. Combat broke out, and but for a few rolls could have gone either way. But Rooker landed some powerful crits and, powered by Buccephalus the undead spirit, managed to hold his own in combat while Lyra locked everything down with Spirit Guardians. Vicas and Azrael succesfully mopped everything up and Thump used a clever grasping vine to pull and grapple enemies into the Spirit Guardians.

When it was all done, Rooker found himself empty of the eldritch influence, and found something else altogether welling up within him. A font of arcane magic he dind't know he'd had, yet now felt had been there all along. For his trouble, Rooker lost the arm, but Vero had pre-planned for the occasion. They had tinkered up most of a magical prosthetic, and had written to "The Kip Vogels Foundation at the Kip Vogels Port for the Peculiar". Apparently, some wealthy benefactor had once paid to ensure the technology would be available across Astoria. Vero completed the ritual, which included everyone saying one nice thing about Rooker, and a swift flying Bluejay delivered a baby mimic to power the prosthesis. One final piece of artifice combined with natural magic, and the arm was operational.

We join our heroes having fast traveled back to Ishetar to circumvent the Turqoise Timbe Trust. There, they met a strange fish-like creature and his pet sea lion, who "is a diploment, arrived several weeks early for the conclave, to be hosted by the Halflings of Ahanabai. But before they unpack all of that, our heroes intend to deal with this logging company once and for all.
