We joined our heroes as they took a small rest to get to know the new party members, Freddie and Leon. Amidst one of these quieter moments, Ty confided to Veil that he was still taking the loss of Arden and Quin quite hard. Veil responded by gifting Ty the firearm they had pilfered from the old bandit captain, and challenging him to learn to wield it. Ty was...enthusiastic. After fending off some strange frog demons, the kobolds slowly led them deeper into their dens and brought them to meet with their oldest council members: a trio of kobolds that had all lived to the ripe old age of six!

The council shared a beverage of the silvery crystal liquid that our party had seen elsewhere in the dungeon, and gained the benefits fo a Telepathic Bond spell. As they talked, our heroes learned that the dragon, or something like it, does indeed appear to be real. The kobolds claimed to occasionally speak to her, though they have never been so bold as to gaze upon her.

Realizing they needed answers, our heroes dared to approach the meeting place. Along the way, they passed several mysterious pools of silvery liquid. Inside of each, they found the remains of a shattered eggshell. Thorn intuited that they had not been broken, but rather had hatched. Then, at the far end of the chamber, the dragon appeared.

A large, snarling queen of a green dragon rose up from the pool, with beads of liquid dripping from her sabred fangs. It was suitably terrifying, except that Freddie had sent ahead and Arcane Eye and was already beginning to tell that something was amiss. As our heroes conversed with this scaled queen, Veil rolled an insight check and began to discern multiple forms: not always green scales but at times skeletal and at others made of shadow. And they began also to identify multiple voices from the creature, as if it quarreled within itself.

As they pressed the creature, calling it out directly for its illusion and deception, it unraveled to reveal 4 distinct, very young dragons: one living green dragon and 3 that seemed undead in nature (skeletal, shadow, and spectral). The green one was fond of our heroes, and sought to ally with them, but the undead ones aimed to subjugate our party and fold them into their cult. Battle broke out.

In truth, the three dragons were no match for our party, even before the green dragon came to their aid. Leon continued his good showing, proving that his blade is righteous in its judgement against undead, even if his own crisis of confidence still roils within. Thorn and Veil locked down first one, and then a second dragon all on their own, while Freddie assisted Ty via a guiding bolt, and Ty revealed to everyone a new found ability to channel Chromatic Orb and Eldritch Blast through the firearm. All in all, it was a strong showing from our heroes.

When the battle was done and the blood settled, they were able to converse with the green dragon: a young baby by the name of **Scourge, Plague of the Willows**. Why Willows? Ambitions must begin modest, after all, lest one bites off more than they can chew. Most especially in a dungeon as dangerous as this.

Scourge proved to be...a problem. His hatching from the egg, and subsequent trickery of his siblings into eating one another while saving him, the runt, for last, allowed him to outlive them. Except, this means that our heroes have no dragon egg to deliver Nanny PuPu. And they're not exactly sure how she'd feel about a whole ass dragon...especially one that has grown accustomed to manipulating a cult by impersonating their own mother.

Problems for down the road, they decided. For in the immediate future, they decided that they must turn their attention on stopping the Silverweave Ventures from breaching Nanny PuPu's wards and entering the garden of the Tree of Dreams. The dragon gifted each hero a charm of Dragon Breath and, after a very tense moment in which Ty nearly pledged his eternal service to him, our heroes managed to strike a more pragmatic deal.

Scourge, now that Ozythar is dead, lacks a purpose or an ambition. In exchange for assisting in the defeat of the Silverweave Ventures, our party would help Scourge find himself a new quest of some kind. Perhaps that involves teaming up with the old witch, or perhaps that involves going off on his own, though Scourge feels personally responsible for his cult.

We join our heroes as they leave Scourge's domain and plan their assault on the Silverweave Camp. Within, they have allies in the form of a badly wounded Arden and Jerusha, as well as a potent bio weapon in the form of the Whisper-Wings, who will be eager to feed upon Quin's ashes. All that remains is to figure out how our heroes might leverage these advantages into the most favorable situation possible.
