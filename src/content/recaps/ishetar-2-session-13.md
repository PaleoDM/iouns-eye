We opened with our heroes still technically in combat. Rooker was under attack from a hellhound, while Morgana remained invisible, insisting she would not fight so long as the party did not strike her. A tense standoff followed. Vicas moved to aid Rooker, quickly dispatching the hellhound, while Rooker attempted to parley with Morgana.

The exchange revealed little hope for compromise. Morgana tried to use her invisibility to steal ritual parchments from her sisters’ corpses. She managed to recover one, but when Lyra intercepted the other, she yelped at the feel of human skin, giving away the theft. As the stalemate dragged on, Thump feigned an errant **Moonbeam** that caught Morgana, and she believed the ruse. But when it became clear she would never reveal herself, Thump cast **Faerie Fire** to expose her, and combat resumed.

Though depleted, Morgana fought alongside the grotesque hyena-like forms of her fallen sisters. Azrael held them back while Lyra and Thump retreated into the temple to guard the last profane recipe on consecrated ground. The battle turned when Vicas grappled Morgana, spread his wings, and carried her aloft. As he rose sixty feet into the air, she cried out, “No! Don’t do it! I have diplomatic immunity! You’ll regret it!” But Vicas did not relent. He released her, and she fell to her death, her body breaking on the stone below.

In the aftermath, the heroes spent several days restoring the temple. They gathered the cursed portraits, and Thump and Lyra devised a ritual to destroy them. By distilling Lyra’s **Spirit Guardians** into celestial essence and combining them with Thump’s primal power, they conjured holy fire that immolated the paintings. As they burned away, the works reformed into a radiant mosaic of the sun and moon entwined, reconsecrating the temple as a place where the Dawnfather and Moonweaver danced together. Sylvari and Solara were overcome, pledging to restore the Restful Lily to its original vision as a sanctuary.

In quieter moments, the party shared scenes with their allies. **Cyrena** gifted Azrael an ancient suit of runed armor, the **Angler’s Armor**, that allowed its wearer to move like water. **Ambrosia** gave Thump a great boomerang that always returns, even when imbued with **Shillelagh**. Lyra received a strange new companion—**Chai**, a teaweird elemental born of **Cyrena** and **Ambrosia’s** mingled magic. Ambrosia also entrusted Thump with a living cutting: a sapient sapling who one day uprooted itself and declared its single goal—to surf the waves.

Finally, **Ambrosia** and **Cyrena** bestowed greater boons. Sirena’s springs offered the power of **Greater Restoration**, while Ambrosia’s roots created a **fast travel link** between his grove and Thump’s maple in Ishetar. Meanwhile, **Lyra** devoted herself to restoring the shattered statue of **Th0l’s** Peregrine Society comrade, preparing for a reunion yet to come.

We closed with our heroes preparing for these flashbacks, taking their first true breath of rest since entering the temple, and readying themselves for the winter to come in Ishetar.
