Eventually, our heroes did hear from Dante (via Inferno of course). Dante praised them once more and re-iterated a disappointment that things got so violent so fast. He impressed upon them that he hoped they were developing some sense of ownership over their situation, and was pleased to hear they'd be taking Harwin's offer to continue to help.

Dante explained their situation plainly. The cultists had 40-50 members prepared to do violence. Dante had carefully led them breadcrumbs to a clandestine meeting set to happen the next evening between important revolutionary leaders, and had pressed them hard to commit heavy resources in that direction as an opportunity to cut the revolution off at its head. Unbeknownst to the cultists, Dante was laying a trap—the townfolk would be descending into the square in high numbers.

This left our party with two equally promising, but opposing, golden opportunities. So Dante gave them a choice:

1.  Join the battle at the town square, in the monument known as the Graves of the Revolution. With the backing of our heroes, the large contingent of cultists wouldn't be able to put up a real resistance, and it would deal a mighty and definitive blow to the Hushed Choir's offensive capabilities.

2.  Take advantage of the cultist base in the Hearthfire Commons to be emptied of half or more cultists, and try to strike hard and fast to deal the cultists a killing blow with few defenses.

The first option had little risk, but it did mean that the remaining cultists would likely dig themselves in and need to be weeded out slowly and methodically. The second option offered great opportunity, but it left our heroes exposed—any retreating cultists from the attack in the square would be sandwiching our party with no backup. Moreover, it left the townsfolk to do the work by themselves, and would almost certainly incur more civilian casualties.

In the end, our heroes elected the former, banking on their ability to bridge the tunnels to help them cross the chasm and attack the cultists with surprise anyway. The final preparations were made and the plan was set in motion. When our heroes arrived on the scene, they discovered that the Cultists had prepared a reverse ambush—a second wave of cultists designed to come in and demolish resistance of weed out escapees. This was a perfect opportunity for the expertise of our heroes, and so combat broke out.

In the ensuing melee, Ty found himself held by a Mage, and Arden leapt into draw fire from him. Thorn successfully harassed and drew attention from half a dozen cultists, while Veil bounced between the two halves doing her best to front line on two fronts. As the battle wore on, our heroes were frequently victimized by one cultist managing to hold a Bless spell on their allies, which proved to turn the tide time and time again. Eventually, Arden was brought down, and despite two healing potions, kept being knocked down once more, until he finally rolled a Natural 20 and managed to extricate himself from danger with Veil's help. Thorn and Veil managed to clean up the other front while Quin and Ty finally brought the last cultist to heel—quin incinerating the last one with a max damage firebolt.

Meanwhile, we were rolling to see how the civilians did with their ambush. Initially, it went off perfectly according to plan. They caught the cultists on the backfoot and cut their numbers extensively. Then the cultists rallied and, being more accustomed to organized battle, broke the ranks of our initial first wave of attack. Reinrocements came in the form of locals however, who sprung from sewer grates and dumped chamber pots from the windows.

The two sides went back and forth while our heroes did battle, but eventually the cultists broke and fled, and the townsfolk turned towards trying to box them in and pick off stragglers. Our heroes joined in this initiative, though Arden held back and provided moral support instead as he was nearly dead. Our party managed to rout a number of stragglers, though in the process Thorn was knocked unconscious. Thankfully, our heroes were able to find and stabilize him quickly before any harm was done.

All told, our heroes defeated 12 cultists, and the citizens an additional 24, meaning that the cults numbers were dwindled well below half! We join our heroes having retreated for a long rest and preparing to plan their assault on the Hearthfire Commons now that the fireworks have begun!
