We opened in the mists, our heroes having taken their first proper rest since arriving in the Shadowfell. Level 11 abilities in hand, they prepared to push forward.

Freddie used the downtime to cast Identify on the lance they'd brought from Odaire. It was the Curtain's Call, a weapon that allows its wielder to swap bodies with a target. It was also deeply cursed. A Legend Lore later in the journey would fill in the details: each time the body swap is used, there's a small chance the switch becomes permanent.

With the rest behind them, our heroes used the calling card and pressed through the mists. They emerged into a sad, dockside town populated by ghosts and the restless dead. Not hostile, just hollow.

They found a tavern called the Sunken Coin. The bartender poured a shot for the table with a personal touch, squeezing his own cerebrospinal ectoplasm into the glass. Ty and Leon both made the save and were treated to a flood of the man's memories: a sad life, a sadder death, and no resolution for either. From the locals, our heroes learned the lay of the land. A ferry operates out of this port, carrying souls from Rifthaven across to the Gatekeeper's final gate. The same ferryman could take them to whichever domain they needed. They just had to burn the calling card at the docks.

Before they did, Sir Sunder gathered the group and laid the mission out plainly. His investigation into Quin's death had led him to believe the Aletheia procedure has been operating as a front for the Pallbearer for some time. The connected cases all share two things: the sentenced person was widely believed innocent before the Aletheia ruled against them, and the sentenced person is never resurrected. The mission was straightforward, if not simple. Get into the Pallbearer's domain, find a smoking gun that implicates the Aletheia High Justicars, and get out with proof.

Our heroes burned the calling card at the docks. The Dancing Dirge arrived, and at its helm stood Captain Alistair, a spirited and excitable ghost of hellfire with his head detached and lopped at his belt by the ponytail. He was delighted to have passengers.

They came aboard and requested passage to the Potter's Field, the Pallbearer's domain of dread. Alistair laid out the problem: the Pallbearer instantly recognizes the presence of a living soul in his domain. Two options. Enter as undead, or cover themselves in leviathan blood to mask their living aura. Our heroes chose the blood, which meant they needed to hunt a leviathan first.

Along the way, Veil had an epiphany. She recognized the name Captain Alistair from the notes on Ty's family she'd found in Sunder's study. Some quick cross-referencing confirmed her suspicions. Captain Alistair had operated an illicit liberation network, smuggling runaway slaves from drow communities in the Underdark, ferrying them across to Tyr'Zareth and into Rifthaven as free citizens. Ty's mother Roe had operated the apothecary that kept Alistair legally employed and sailing. His father Cliff had been the master accountant who ensured the books passed inspection. Ty's parents were freedom fighters.

Ty ran to Freddie with the information, and between the two of them they sussed out the rest: Captain Alistair had been married to Cliff and Roe, making him almost certainly Ty's biological father. Freddie asked some pointed questions, but neither pirate nor son quite put it together. Still, it laid the groundwork for what will surely be some tough conversations.

Then the hunt found them. A massive leviathan of shadow and roiling darkness breached the surface and slammed into the Dancing Dirge. Freddie threw up a critical Bless just in time for Thorn to take a devastating blow that flung him off the ship into the ocean of shadow. Ty caught the worst of it next, taking two terrible hits that nearly leveled him before a flying demon swooped in and finished the job.

Veil's new ability to fly proved its worth immediately. She dealt solid damage while hauling Thorn out of the shadow sea before things got worse. Leon drove powerful strikes into the leviathan. Ty got himself back up with Lulu's help and finished things off with a volley of Eldritch Blasts. Freddie debuted Contagion, which brought one of the flying demons low.

The creatures hit hard, but our heroes hit harder. We join them having just arrived at the docks outside a white marble city in the Potter's Field, taking a short rest and applying the leviathan's blood. Captain Alistair has warned them that the domain's customs house will require them to declare their magic items and offer collateral: cherished memories, gold, a bit of their luck, or similar personal currency.

The Pallbearer doesn't know they're coming. That advantage won't last.
