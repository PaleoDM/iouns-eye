We opened with our heroes departing Ishetar and arriving at the Restful Lily, where they caught their breath and prepared for the march south. There was some discussion about whether Rooker should split off or stay behind, but they quickly decided to go as a team.

The Myreshade is a dangerous forest, but for a party of our heroes' caliber, it's the kind of dangerous that stays at arm's length. The road south was overgrown but passable, and for the most part they moved through without incident. At least, until Oakum, Thump's tressym familiar, alerted the party to something in the brush. The little cat had found and pinned a large gopher-like creature, holding it face-down against the hillside because it too was someone's familiar. In this way, whoever was looking through its eyes couldn't see a thing. Thump walked over and unceremoniously whacked it, winking the familiar out of existence. A quick investigation of the area confirmed what they'd suspected: someone had been camping here, using this spot as a lookout. A spy post.

Azrael spotted something else: fresh blood, unusual and deliberate, trailing off into the underbrush. He followed the tracks and found a dwarf ranger tangled in the brambles of a tree spirit, badly wounded and possibly beyond saving. The spirit itself wasn't hostile toward Azrael, exactly, but it made clear he wasn't welcome to approach. When pressed, it expressed something like retribution: a debt being repaid for the mistreatment it had suffered. Our heroes took the implication immediately: the forest remembers what the TTT druids have been doing to it, and it's begun to push back. Azrael, having failed to get close enough to assess the dwarf's condition and feeling the spirit's patience wearing thin, made the hard call to back off and leave the thread unpursued.

Further south, the road split: east and uphill toward the main logging site, or south along the coastline into the TTT compound itself. Our heroes spent more time than they'd probably like to admit figuring out who was going where and how.

By Lyra always had a plan. She'd walk right into camp the way she always does: as the traveling tea minister, tending to workers and offering warmth. It wasn't a cover; it's what she does. She took Azrael with her as her apprentice. At the gate, they met some friction. One guard didn't want to let them in, but another recognized Lyra and vouched for her. After some tension, she and Azrael were escorted inside and set up near the old well where Lyra typically does her work.

The others had no such luck. Vicas, Thump, and Rooker tried to talk their way in and hit a wall, figuratively and literally. The rolls were bad, but more than that, the guards simply weren't having it. The camp has changed. The garrison has locked things down and strangers aren't welcome. Our heroes were starting to understand just how tightly the TTT leadership has squeezed this place.

Inside, Lyra and Azrael got a different picture, and a more complicated one. The tension in camp doesn't run cleanly between soldiers and civilians. The real dividing line is tenure. People who've been here a long time, soldier and commoner alike, have roots in Ekkorai. They feel tied to Ishetar, to the broader community along this coast. The newer arrivals carry their loyalties back across the water to the Aroiin Empire. And someone is exploiting that friction hard.

An old midwife, sensing an opportunity, bid Azrael to go fetch water so she could speak to Lyra in confidence. She laid it out plainly: the camp is splitting at the seams, and getting worse. On his way back with the water, Azrael spotted a beautiful elven noblewoman emerging from a guarded cellar, one of the compound's secure storage areas. Unfortunately for Azrael, he rolled terribly, and the woman spotted him immediately. Worse: she *recognized* him. Not from Ishetar. Not from the road. From before.

"So good that you're finally here," she said. "You can come home. This changes everything."

Azrael didn't recognize her face. But he knew instantly, with the certainty of someone who has spent his entire life running from one thing, exactly where that recognition came from. The Order of the Celestial Seed had found him.

She didn't attack. She turned and walked toward the foreman's office with purpose. Azrael followed, stealthily, weighing his options. We thought for all the world Azrael would move to assassinate her, but chose to flee instead. Thanks to an active detect thoughts spell, she cast *Hold Person*. Azrael made the save and burst into the courtyard.

From there, everything happened at once. Azrael fled through the camp, frantically calling out through the party's telepathic link that things had gone sideways. Lyra dropped what she was doing and moved to intercept. Outside the walls, Thump shifted into a flying wild shape and took to the air while Vicas unfurled his dragonborn wings and rose alongside him, both sailing up and over the compound walls. Rooker, still stuck at the gate with the guards who'd already turned him away, was a turn or two behind. But a man with teleportation magic isn't a man who stays locked out for long.

We leave our heroes mid-crisis, scattered across the TTT compound with two inside, two coming over the walls, and one about to make an entrance of his own. The camp is watching. The guards are confused. The woman from the cellar has pursued Azrael across the alleyways. And the question hanging over everything isn't whether things turn violent. It's whether our heroes can keep them from turning violent in front of two hundred people who aren't sure yet which side to trust.
