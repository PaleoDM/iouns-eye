We opened with a prologue:

An angry and tense crowd in a North Side, out in the open air. The erratic, anxious, and heightened crowd rabbles in frustration, as the camera hones in. On a set of gallows, and 8 nooses swinging in free suspension over The Rift. We hear the thud of 8 sets of boots as the cries calls out—for crimes of inciting violence, fomenting anger, and driving unrest. Then, the creak of wood as the switch is pulled. The crowd gasps and we linger on 7 sets of boots hanging over the Rift…and one snapped Rope.

We join Tyrell, waking in a seedy dockside inn at the lakeside village in the Underdark. An old sailor comes in and chastises him for being late. The two argue, during which we learn that Ty is struggling w/ the "rules" of piracy. As the old sailor exists, Mini Ty makes his appearance—a hideous creature emanating from the shell of a chambered nautilus, with tentacles twirled into a mustache. The two banter for a moment, during which is becomes apparent that Mini Ty has no understanding of how to properly motivate regular Ty. Eventually, they settle on a strange envelope—old and faded, with crimson writing.

From there, we faded to a very different ship altogether, out in the open air. We saw a crew, working hard to prepare for what seemed to be some kind of aerial stunt. Their captain getting hair and make up done in front of the Rifthaven equivalent of ring lights. Only they were usurped by a larger, faster ship that beat them to the stunt—the AeroNaughtie Bois. Dejected, the crew of our ship got back to work, and we saw one crew member in shiny armor boasting a charismatic smile: Arden. Approached by a surly sailor named “Old Blorb”, the two struck up a conversation over the disappointment, an quickly realized that they’d never “make it big” or “gain followers” unless they found a new way to steak a name for themselves. That’s when Old Blorb handed Arden a mysterious envelope…

We cut again to a nondescript garden, where an Oak of a man knelt, tenderly caring for an overgrown bush. This man, gentle despite his size, carefully cut away at a scarred piece of root. And suddenly, Thorn was connected, to the superhighway of roots and mycelium that connect all things. When his vision returned and his disorientation passed, he found the same envelope resting beside him.

And Quin, alone in the back room of a seedy gambling den, when his boss rushes in and tells him to get rid of the floozy aristocrat that’s winning too much. The woman, on the older side but well dressed and enjoying herself, made a big show of winning over and over until she’d garnered Quin’s attention. As the elven mage escorted her out, she firmly pressed an envelope into his breast pocket and implored him to “put those discerning eyes to better use.”

And finally Veil, returning to deliver a set of names to her mentor—Stone-Whisper-Bloom-of Sunder. The elderly man meticulously polished the enormous, dolorous bells that signal incursions. As Veil reported on the names, Stone commented on the unusual nature of this task. He didn’t trust their buyer, Stone explained, and implored Veil to replace one of the names with her own and see herself into this mission. Easy enough, for one of Veil’s talents. And so it was.

When we next saw our heroes, they were convening in the Sparkworks district, on the 14th day of the 14th month. It was just before midnight. The district was damn near abandoned, and the Old Distillery was beat to shit. It seemed impossible that anyone had ever worked here, let alone made any money. But 5 glasses and a bottle laid out on the nearest table bade them all an excuse to sit, and drink, and talk.

The discussion was short lived, however, as several of our group soon heard signs of a scuffle. Making their way to the windows, Veil and Tyrell tried to investigate, and found a man bound and tied to a chair amidst a crowd of bodies. Our player sprung into action, and soon found themselves facing down what appeared to be a set of thugs and cultists.

In the scuffle, we saw Tyrell duel a cultist with his barnacle-encrusted scimitar. We saw Veil cold-cock multiple cultists, cracking their unsettling, mouthless mask. We saw Thorn deal a debilitating blow to one of the captains, and Arden pierce another in the back as they fled. And finally, we saw Quin blast another into oblivion with magic missile.

When the dust settled, our heroes rescued and med Dane—a handsome tiefling with a pet Macaque. Dante doesn’t seem to speak (is he even capable of it?), but he can communicate by controlling the Macaque as one does a familiar. Dante apologized for the poor welcome, explaining that he’s been harassed by a group of thugs he called The Ledgermen, and now a second mysterious group of cultists.

Dante explained why he’d brought them to the Sparkworks District. Rumors of old smuggling tunnels beneath the city had reached interested parties in the underground, and controlling them would prove a lucrative way to circumvent the tariffs imposed by those that run the existing tunnels. Hell, it could potentially open up revenue if they charge some of their own. Dante made them a generous offer: discover if the tunnels are real and can be brought operational once more. In exchange, they could walk away with 25 gold apiece, or a 50% stake in the tunnels themselves.

We join our heroes having long rested at the nearest “inn”. It’s the morning of the 15th and our heroes gather around the breakfast table to discuss what they’ve gotten themselves in to and what their next steps might be.
