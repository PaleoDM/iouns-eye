We joined our heroes in the Servant’s Closet, preparing their final strike against Lord Sergente. Carmen was convinced to lure him into the safe room before teleporting to safety with her grandmother. With Tota setting off the explosives, Carmen warned the party one last time—they had a mage, and teleportation was the plan.

Thorn and Veil moved first, cutting through the guard line to reach the mage before he could act. Thorn cowed one guard into opening the door, and together they carved the mage down before he ever took a turn. Arden, Ty, and Quin followed to secure the rear and keep Thorn and Veil from being overwhelmed.

The fight was brutal. Thorn, already bloodied from previous battles, held the line like a mountain, soaking damage from all sides. Ty banished one of the heavies outright, forcing Arden and Quin to protect his concentration. Arden also unleashed a new ability, radiating charisma and casting Command on another heavy, who fled so completely he threw himself from a second-story window. With those threats removed, the tide turned. Sergente, his twin bodyguards Braelin and Brolin, and the rest of the defenders were brought down.

As the manor burned around them, the party gathered incriminating documents and loot before slipping away under Ty’s skilled guidance. They witnessed Tota and Carmen escaping with Romero de la Alma, and glimpsed Haji himself on the balcony—immune to flame, accompanied by a six-armed mechanical construct. Unable to locate the heroes, Haji departed with his ally, leaving the manor to the flames.

Among the documents recovered were Captain Reeve’s infernal contract (quickly destroyed), a chromatic gemstone claimed by Quin, and notes pointing to Haji’s influence in Brimspire. The ledgers also confirmed Haji’s efforts to eliminate Sergente’s rivals, consolidating power in Maximilian’s hands.

We saw Lord Sergente turned over to the people of Salvation, who are likely to try him and sentence him either to death or to hard labor. We saw several people of Salvation who seem predisposed to hear the whispers of the plants come to Thorn, assuring him that whenever he calls for aid they will rise to his cause. Thorn, predictably, wasn't entirely sure what to make of it. And then we saw our heroes convene with Captain Reeve, who found herself paralyzed: a victim of some profane magic on Haji's part. Fire beetles began pouring from her mouth and forming up into a school mimicing a smiling tiger's face.

Haji spoke through the face then, complimenting our heroes for their success at thwarting him and assuring them that, as a business man, he was ready to turn from enemy to partner at the drop of a hat. The Ledgermen, he explained, had outlived their usefulness to him, and were now a common enemy—one which teetered on the edge of destruction after our heroes had dealt them such a strong blow. Could they work together to take them down? Our heroes declined, destroying Captain Reeve's contract once and for all, and decided instead of pay a visit to the old witch in the mountains—Nanny Pu'pu.
