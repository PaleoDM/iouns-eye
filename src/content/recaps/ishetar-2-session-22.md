We rejoined our heroes aboard **The Shooting Star**, regrouping with **Captain Alba Skylar**, **Lady Rowena**, **Vero**, and Captain Alba’s two triton bodyguards, **Dagas** and **Uthe**. With the situation on Firewatch Island clearly deteriorating, the discussion turned to defining a concrete goal.

From the tritons’ explanation, the party learned more about their role as overseers of **the Rift**—a dangerous underwater phenomenon monitored on behalf of **Tidemark Janus**. Crucially, detailed knowledge about the Rift was not recorded in written form by them. The tritons’ people live primarily underwater, making long-term written records impractical. Instead, this information had been entrusted to the monastery’s prior, **Prior Borisanth**, who safeguarded the written records personally. Recovering that knowledge now became the party’s primary objective.

Before returning ashore, Lyra brewed an especially potent tea for herself, Lady Rowena, and Thump, hoping to mitigate the particular danger posed by the peryton, whose preference for elven and halfling hearts was now painfully clear. With preparations complete, the party returned to the island to see what awaited them.

This time, the approach from the north beach was uneventful. No harpies descended, and the path remained clear. They reached the monastery courtyard and found the main doors not merely closed, but heavily barricaded from within. As they began searching for alternate points of entry, they were attacked.

A group of **assassin vines** surged from the courtyard alongside a noxious **corpse flower**, filling the space with snapping tendrils and poisonous spores. The fight was dangerous from the outset. Thump came close to being overwhelmed, but managed a well-timed Misty Step to escape the vines and reach the roof, gaining a momentary reprieve. Lyra followed by calling up *Spirit Guardians*, which immediately began to grind down the clustered enemies while the party’s damage dealers went to work. Azrael and Vicas landed several key blows at critical moments, cutting down threats before they could press their advantage.

As the fight wound down, Lyra noticed something strange: her spirit guardians were reacting oddly near one of the monastery’s outer walls, close to a line of narrow arrow slits. This structure, after all, had once been a pirate fortress, and its defensive features ran deep. As the radiant spirits pressed inward, something inside the wall took damage—and then burst outward.

The stone exploded as a massive **undead tyrannosaurus rex** tore free from the monastery’s interior, sending debris flying and immediately escalating the situation.

The timing could not have been worse. Thump had been standing directly above the breach when the creature erupted through the wall and roof. In moments, he was caught in its jaws, downed, and rendered unconscious. For a brief stretch, the danger was very real: a fall from that height could have spelled disaster.

Vicas acted immediately. In one of the most cinematic moments of the campaign so far, he sprinted up the creature’s back, struck it hard enough to force it to rear and roar, reached into its mouth, healed Thump with his healing hands, and physically hauled him free. The maneuver worked. Thump escaped, used *Misty Step* again to regain the roof, and the party closed ranks.

From there, momentum shifted. **Azrael** landed a devastating sneak attack, and the group pressed the advantage. The fight ended decisively when **Lyra** invoked *Turn Undead*. The remaining lesser undead were destroyed outright, and the undead T-rex fled—driven far down the beach and out of the immediate area. The party chose not to pursue. For now, removing it from the battlefield was victory enough.

Lyra’s *Spirit Guardians* remained active, however, and as the party pushed deeper into the monastery through the newly created breach, they encountered a group of undead ogres lingering on an interior balcony. What followed was brief and efficient—what the table jokingly referred to as “cantrips and chill”—as the ogres were methodically eliminated.

With the immediate threats cleared, the party began exploring the monastery in earnest. They learned that the structure had originally been built by pirates before being reclaimed and consecrated by the monks of **Ioun the Knowing Mistress** after the pirate captain’s ship was sunk. The monastery still bore signs of both histories.

In **Prior Borisanth’s** former quarters, Rooker uncovered a particularly important discovery: ship’s logs and charts recovered from the wreck of **Admiral Syrgaul’s** cursed vessel, the *Tammeraut*. These records contained not only detailed information related to **the Rift**, but also references to the location of **Syrgaul’s treasure hoard**.

Meanwhile, Azrael and Lyra investigated the grounds further, piecing together what had happened. It became clear that the undead assault had occurred the previous night. Most of the monastery’s inhabitants had not simply been killed—they had been taken. The undead appeared to be deliberately collecting bodies.

This theory was grimly confirmed when Azrael discovered a mound of corpses behind the monastery, stuffed into a massive fishing net and left bobbing in the bay like a grotesque buoy. Daylight had likely interrupted the process. The undead would almost certainly return after nightfall to retrieve the rest.

Finally, the party discovered a concealed trapdoor leading to a hidden cellar. Inside, they found survivors—several monks and attendants barricaded within a wine cellar, hiding among stacked barrels and hoping to avoid detection. They were alive, but badly shaken and in rough condition.

We leave our heroes here: a few hours before nightfall, with survivors to protect, critical information to secure, the looming threat of the undead returning from the sea, and hard decisions to make about rest, preparation, and how to face what comes next on **Firewatch Island**.
