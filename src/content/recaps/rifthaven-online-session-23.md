We picked up mid-combat at the Arcane Forge. Ty had opened the front door, triggering held actions from several scout captains. Their arrows landed squarely inThorn, who stood nearly impervious with his hundred hit points. Veil debuted a new area-of-effect ability, unleashing a thunderous blast of damage. Unbeknownst to the party, this prompted Romero De la Alma—locked within the forge—to begin rolling death saves behind the screen.

Arden played his usual supporting role, dispensing Bardic Inspiration and strikes with the Trident, while Thorn locked down as many foes as he could. Quinn found himself in a close duel with a mage, nearly overwhelmed. Ty went down twice, Quinn soon after, but healing came quickly: Veil poured a potion into Quinn, and Arden restored Ty with a heavier dose. As the battle closed, the heroes pushed into the forge, where they found Tota De la Alma calmly holstering her poisoned pulque syringes—having dispatched four enemies of her own.

Inside, Thorn and Veil used their speed to reach Romero just in time, stabilizing him before he failed his final death save. The reunion with Tota was heartfelt, and Romero was left profoundly grateful. With him safe, the party turned to investigating the forge. Ty discovered the memory crystals taken from Ernesto, stolen to reduce him to docile servitude. Quin studied the forge and realized its true, sinister purpose: not merely to split memories, but to harvest and separate souls—fuel for the Coinwrights’ posthumous labor contracts, and currency to be sold into the Nine Hells.

Veil unearthed critical blackmail in Haji’s notes. The rakshasa was seeking a loan from Mammon, the financier of the Hells and lord of the Third Layer, using the Arcane Forge as proof of concept to build many more. Destroying the forge, the heroes realized, would deal a crippling blow to Haji’s ambitions. Together, they rigged it to detonate within the hour. Tota drew them a map of the plantation manor and set about extracting Romero.

Before moving on, Quin helped Veil piece together the broader politics of the Hells. Seizing on the opportunity, Veil planted false evidence linking their interference to Fierna and Belial, rulers of the Fourth Layer—directly above Mammon. Her intent was to rattle Haji into believing the heroes were agents of far greater powers. To that end, she founded a clandestine spy ring of her own, christening it *The Veiled Eye*.

After a short rest, the party infiltrated the plantation manor, slipping in through a servants’ passage and crossing directly to Lord Sergente’s panic room. We rejoin our heroes at the door, ready to bring Carmen into their plan and make their final push against the Sergente noble himself.
