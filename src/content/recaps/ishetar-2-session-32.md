We opened in the Bank of Zoink, with the marble walls still ringing from the teleportation. Before our heroes could turn their attention to the ancient golden dragonborn and his enormous abacus, there was a conversation that couldn't wait.

Isla needed answers. She'd just watched her home burn and her husband pledge fealty to people she'd never met, and now she was standing in some underground vault with no idea what came next. Our heroes laid it out as plainly as they could: she and Drev were immigrants now. Asylum had been offered and accepted. The price was Drev's magic in the defense of Ishetar. Isla understood, but she didn't like it. She produced a beautiful necklace studded with diamonds and offered it outright if it meant keeping her husband off the battlefield. The party turned her down. They needed him. Isla accepted it the way a former soldier does: ready to do what she must.

With that settled, our heroes turned to Zoink and the bank itself. What they found was remarkable. Zoink maintains a demiplane, a kind of neutral magical marketplace where powerful factions, governments, and individuals across all of Astoria can trade goods and services without physically moving them. The turquoise timber trust had an account here, and thanks to the access cards and passcodes our heroes had acquired, they now had full access to it. Several hundred thousand gold, sitting in a vault that didn't care who spent it. The TTT had paid for alert insurance, which notifies account holders when funds are moved, but they hadn't paid for exclusivity. As far as Zoink was concerned, anyone with the card and the codes was a legitimate account holder. The gold belonged to our heroes now.

They took some spending money to prepare themselves and arranged for Zoink to teleport them back to Ishetar. Isla helped them fully grasp how this could help them. With gold that didn't need to physical transport out of Ishetar to be useful, they could entice all sorts of new trade partners beyond Aroiinae. She specifically mentioned the Leviathan Hunters in the Far North and the Pilgrims Beneath the Diadems. That's when the alert caught up with them.

A guild mage, a gladiator, and two soldiers arrived through the bank's entrance, responding to the insurance trigger. The fight started lopsided in our heroes' favor. The mooks went down fast. But then the mage got off a Cone of Cold that caught several party members in the blast and brought them dangerously low. A few lucky hits from the remaining fighters compounded the damage. Azrael critcritted twice and Vicas poured out damage in response. Lyra answered with a devastating area blast while Rooker landed a clutch crowd control spell at exactly the right moment. Thump locked down what was left with grasping vines. Our heroes won, but they felt it. They needed a rest before moving on.

Then they headed to Ishetar.They arrived to find the town already under siege. Several skirmishes were underway across the walls and gates. Hrothgar himself had just returned from leading the defense of one such engagement. Our heroes didn't waste time. They split up and moved to where they were needed most.

But first, Thump cast Locate Plants. He was searching for the corrupted trees among Sallow's forces, trying to get a read on the battlefield. What he got instead was a direct connection. Through the mycelium of the earth itself, Thump and Sallow locked minds. Sallow laid it bare. He was a nature supremacist. He would see all of this, every wall, every road, every settlement, torn down so that nature could reclaim what was taken from it. Thump didn't flinch. He lectured Sallow on balance, on the duty druids carry to preserve the relationship between civilization and the wild, not to destroy one for the other. Sallow was livid. The connection broke.

Our heroes took their positions.

Azrael and Thump held the South Gate. Azrael provided aerial bombardment, hurling his daggers from above and cutting down attackers as they approached. Thump spoke directly to the corrupted creatures among Sallow's forces, animal to animal, imploring them to turn around. This was wrong. This was unnatural. Go home. And many of them did.

Rooker and Vicas took the East Gate. Vicas used several potions that let him read the dynamics of the attacking force, identifying weak points in their morale and exploiting them ruthlessly. Rooker layered suggestive magic over the wavering troops, convincing them that this wasn't a cause worth dying for. Soldiers broke ranks. Weapons lowered.

Lyra held the North Gate alongside Vero and Sombra. She cast Calm Emotions into the heart of the assault, draining the fervor and fury from the attackers in a single spell. The charge faltered. The line held.

The Battle of the Gates was a resounding success. Our heroes reconvened on the walls just in time to see Sallow himself arrive on the battlefield. He came piloting a juggernaut. Part awakened tree, part mechanical war machine, the thing moved like a siege engine crossed with a titan. It was enormous, dangerous, and heading straight for the walls.

Hrothgar met it head on. The old dwarf stood directly in its path, daring the thing to step forward. It did. Its massive foot came down on Hrothgar and crushed him into the earth. Or so it seemed. The juggernaut's weight drove it straight into a dwarven pit trap that Hrothgar had prepared for exactly this kind of threat. The general had baited it with his own body. The juggernaut lurched, stumbled, and locked in place, rooted and unable to advance.

We leave our heroes charging across the battlefield toward the trapped juggernaut. Sallow is up there somewhere inside it. Hrothgar may or may not have survived what he just pulled. The machine can't move, but it can still fight, and it won't stay stuck forever. Our heroes have a window. A narrow one. They need to climb the thing, find Sallow, and put him down before the juggernaut tears itself free and finishes what it started.
